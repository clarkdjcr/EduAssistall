# Arts Education - Accomplished

Parent Guide for Accomplished Technical Theatre

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for technical theatre is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with technical theatre builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed technical theatre educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how music is defined in historical time periods and apply what they know from other subjects to technical theatre designs.
CN.2 Explore - Students identify what it takes to pursue a specific career in music after high school and analyze how innovations in technical theatre are impacting the future of the field.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise solutions to problems and tasks, create original designs, and design cohesive technical processes for implementation within production parameters.
CR.2 Adapt - Students research concepts and techniques of other technical theatre artists and explore how a theatre artist gets permission to use another’s work.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to use directorial feedback to improve while refining stagecraft skills, like set, lighting, and prop design.
PR.2 Develop - Enhance specific productions by effectively implementing stylistically rich technical designs, considering the theatrical space and its capabilities, while being mindful of how spaces and theatrical practices impact audience engagement.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the aesthetic impact of choices made by theatre technicians and examine how performers, audiences, and production are affected.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students consider how what they’ve seen or experienced in theatre shapes their tastes before providing constructive feedback based on objective critique.

How to Support Your Technical Theatre Student at Home
* Ask your student to share the work they are creating at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about what artistic choices were effective, impactful, or that you liked
* Encourage your student to design and create props, costumes, or sets at home and share their ideas with you
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small technical achievements
