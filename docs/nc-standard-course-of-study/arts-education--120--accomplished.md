# Arts Education - Accomplished

Parent Guide for Accomplished General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed music educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how music is defined in historical time periods, teaches skills, characteristics, and behaviors useful in all careers, and can be used to shift mood.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how the creative process is applicable beyond music class and explore the innovations that influence the field of music.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise melodies and compose complex musical ideas in through either music notation or by using a digital audio workstation (DAW).
CR.2 Adapt - Students arrange personal ideas into an existing piece of music and also explore how parody, adaptation, and artificial intelligence relate to copyright protection.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and the music they perform or produce, honing their ability to demonstrate more complex rhythms, pitches, harmony, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing in a variety of global styles.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop their own musical presentations and explore how audience and performer etiquette impact the experience of those presentations.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the use of complex rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of traditional, contemporary, and world music. They also examine historical information to interpret musical intent.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how their own preferences are impacted by commercial marketing. They also practice objectively evaluating others’ performances, compositions, and ideas.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to share the songs they are learning at school
* Listen together to music from different cultures, time periods, or genres, and talk about whether you like it or not - and why
* Share music that evokes a memory, story, challenge, or feeling
* Listen to parodies, discussing the source song and how it has been changed
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Encourage students to compose and produce music at home
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
