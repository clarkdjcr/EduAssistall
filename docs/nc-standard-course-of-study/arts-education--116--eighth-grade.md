# Arts Education - Eighth Grade

Parent Guide for Eighth Grade General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore influential North Carolinian styles and musicians, musical skills and processes that can be used to solve problems, and choose music to impact mood.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how music teaches skills, characteristics, and behaviors useful in all careers, and learn about innovations used to create music.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise extended melodies and record complex musical ideas in through either music notation or by using a digital audio workstation (DAW).
CR.2 Adapt - Students create an accompaniment for an existing melody as well as explain how to legally and ethically share music both physically and digitally.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and the music they perform or produce, honing their ability to demonstrate complex rhythms, pitches, harmony, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop their own criteria for selecting songs for presentation, and make recommendations to improve performer and audience etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the use of complex rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of traditional, contemporary, and world music. They also examine how a musician’s experiences influence their music.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how their own preferences are shaped by their environment and culture. They also objectively evaluate presentations, compositions, and musical ideas based on what is appropriate for the genre or style.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to share the songs they are learning at school
* Listen together to music from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Discuss whether you like a song or not - and why
* Point out when your family uses calm music or party music to influence the mood of the home
* Discuss how pirating music hurts every musician involved in the creation of the stolen music
* Discuss how the skills they practice in music class can be leveraged in all careers
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Encourage students to compose and produce music at home
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
