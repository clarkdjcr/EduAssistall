# Healthful Living - Physical Education Glossary

North Carolina Standard Course of Study Physical Education Glossary
Purpose
The Glossary of Terms seeks to address critical terms and definitions essential in building content knowledge and understanding but also in promoting consistency across physical education, increased student outcomes, and improved parent communication about the implementation of the 2024 NC Standard Courses of Study in Healthful Living. These standards will be implemented in all North Carolina schools beginning in the 2025-26 school year.

	Levels of Movement
	low level movements: rolling, sitting, kneeling, crawling, crouching
medium level movements: walking, stepping, balancing, running
high level movements: leaping, pushing off the ground, stretching into the air, skipping, jumping3
	Locomotor Skills
	basic motor skills involving a change of position of the feet and/or a change of direction of the body. Locomotor skills include walking, running, hopping, skipping, jumping leaping sliding, and galloping2
	 Manipulative Skills
	basic motor skills involving handling an object. Manipulative skills include throwing, catching, kicking, trapping, and striking2

	the ability of muscle group to execute repeated contractions over a period of time sufficient to cause muscle fatigue6
	Muscular Strength
	the muscle's ability to exert force at high intensities over short periods of time6
	Non-locomotor
	movement of the body performed from a relatively stable base of support. Examples include bending, stretching, twisting, turning, leaning, swaying, and swinging2
