# Arts Education - Advanced

Parent Guide for Advanced Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed theatre arts educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how theatre has expanded across the globe since 1900, as well as how its creation and performance are relevant in all career fields.
CN.2 Explore - Students identify what it takes to pursue a specific career in theatre after high school and evaluate the effectiveness of innovative technologies on theatrical works.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students use improvisation to develop short plays, create time-period specific stories, and create technical designs to support the era of their dramatic presentation.
CR.2 Adapt - Students explore how to incorporate various source materials while holding one theatrical vision, and also explore how a theatre artist gets permission to use another artist’s work.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students justify their choices in relation to specific and use staging conventions to refine theatrical productions.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop a variety of works for presentation, model audience, performer, and technician etiquette, enhance works with technical elements, and adapt staging and directions for a variety of stage configurations.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students analyze aesthetics and how they impact audiences.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students justify personal preferences and suggestions for improving theatrical works.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods (especially those 1900 AD - today), or genres
* Talk about the impact of history, culture, and professional theatre trends on the work
* Encourage your student to perform research to write historically-informed stories, designs, and create props, costumes, or sets at home, and share their ideas with you
* Point out when actors, writers, artists, and designers have borrowed from another artist, discussing how they had to ask permission
* Explore possible careers in theatre
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
