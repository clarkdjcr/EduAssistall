# Arts Education - Intermediate

Parent Guide for Intermediate Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed theatre arts educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students see how theatre connects to history, society, and culture from the 1400s through the 1700s and apply what they know from other subjects to solve theatrical problems.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore the roles of individuals involved in producing theatre as well as the traditional and innovative tools used by them.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students use improvisation to develop characters, create complex stories, and implement their technical designs in their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students adapt unique experiences into theatre works and evaluate the ethics involved in making, performing, and distributing both original works and adaptations.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use specific criteria to identify areas of improvement and use staging conventions to theatrical productions.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop scenes for presentation, enhance performances with technical elements, and explore how theatrical venues affect performances, including what audience behaviors are considered appropriate.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students explore how structure, genre, and style are reflected in artistic choices.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how personal preferences impact objectivity before creating objective critique of performances.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, pointing out the work of actors, designers, directors, musicians, stage crew, etc.
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods (especially those 1400 - 1799 AD), or genres
* Talk about the impact of structure, genre, and style
* Discuss theatre, musicals, films, or performances objectively, and then in relation to your preferences
* Encourage your student to write stories, create characters, and design technical elements at home, and share their ideas with you
* Point out when actors, writers, artists, and designers have borrowed from another artist, discussing how they had to ask permission
* Attend school or community performances when possible
