# Arts Education - Eighth Grade

Parent Guide for Eighth Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore influential North Carolinian Artists and works and examine how skills and information from visual arts and other content areas can be used to solve a variety of problems.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how the creative process prepares them for college and career challenges and how innovative tools and technologies are used to create images in the media.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students experiment with different techniques and methods to make art and create work that communicates ideas or feelings.
CR.2 Adapt - Students create new work by combining imagery from multiple sources and explain how to legally and ethically share visual art both physically and digitally.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students use specific criteria to improve their work and create a statement that explains the meaning and purpose of personal art.
PR.2 Develop - Students organize examples of exemplary artwork into a portfolio and demonstrate appropriate behaviors when engaging with art venues and productions.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students examine differences in movements, styles, and themes and how personal experience, culture, and environment influence choices made by artists.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students describe their thoughts and opinions about works of art and use criteria to analyze and evaluate art.
How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Explore local North Carolina artists online or in museums and discuss what makes their work unique.
* Discuss how the creative design process can be applied to multiple college and career situations.
* Look at how innovative tools and technologies can be used to create visual images in the media.
* Encourage your child to try new materials or experiment in art making.
* Have your child create original art that expresses one or more feelings or ideas.
* Have your child make art by combining imagery from numerous sources.
* Ask them to explain how they created a piece and what ideas or feelings it expresses.
* Discuss how to give credit to other artists and share artwork responsibly online or in print.
* Ask your child to describe the meaning, purpose, and choices in their artwork.
* Create a mini “portfolio wall” at home with their best pieces.
* Visit local galleries, theaters, or museums and model respectful engagement with art.
* Compare how the elements of art and principles of design are used in contemporary and historical artworks.
* Look at art together and have your child explain what they like or dislike about the work.
