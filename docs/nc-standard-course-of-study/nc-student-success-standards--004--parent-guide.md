NC Student Success Standards Parent Guide

Introduction: What are NC Student Success Standards?

The NC Student Success Standards are adopted from The American School Counselor Association (ASCA) Mindsets and Behaviors for Student Success, a set of research-based standards that describe the skills, attitudes, and knowledge students need to thrive in school and beyond. These standards help students succeed academically, build healthy relationships, make responsible decisions, and plan for their futures. School counselors use these standards to guide classroom lessons, group activities, and individual counseling.
There are two main types of standards:
* Mindsets: Beliefs and attitudes students have about themselves, school, and their future.
* Behaviors: Actions students take to support learning, manage themselves, and interact with others.
View the Student Success Standards document.

What Students Learn at each Grade Span

Grades K–2
* Mindsets:
   * Belief in self and learning
   * Feeling accepted and included at school
   * Developing confidence to try new things
* Behaviors:
   * Learning to stay organized and on task
   * Following directions and routines
   * Building friendships and showing kindness

Grades 3–5
* Mindsets:
   * Positive attitudes about learning and future goals
   * Respecting themselves and others
   * Understanding the importance of education
* Behaviors:
   * Using time wisely and completing tasks
   * Solving problems and staying motivated
   * Being a good team member and showing empathy

Grades 6–8
* Mindsets:
   * Seeing challenges as opportunities to grow
   * Confidence in decision-making and planning
   * Recognizing the value of effort and perseverance
* Behaviors:
   * Managing emotions and stress
   * Setting and working toward goals
   * Communicating respectfully and ethically

Grades 9–12
* Mindsets:
   * Taking ownership of learning and goals
   * Understanding long-term benefits of education
   * Believing in their ability to succeed and contribute to society
* Behaviors:
   * Balancing responsibilities at school, home, and work
   * Advocating for themselves and others
   * Preparing for college, careers, and adult life

How Parents Can Support Learning at Home
You play a key role in helping your child build these important mindsets and behaviors. Here are a few ways you can support them:
* Talk daily: Ask open-ended questions about their day and what they learned.
* Practice skills: Help them stay organized, manage time, and solve problems calmly.
* Model behaviors: Show empathy, respect, and responsibility in everyday life.
* Encourage effort: Praise persistence and highlight the value of learning from mistakes.
* Create a growth mindset: Remind them that intelligence and ability can grow with effort.

Partnering With the School Counseling Program
Parents and counselors are partners in student success. Here are ways to collaborate:
* Stay informed: Attend parent information nights or workshops hosted by the school counselor.
* Reach out: Contact the counselor with questions or concerns about your child’s academic, social, or emotional growth.  School counselors are trained to assist with the following:
   1. **Academic Support**
      * * Helping students set academic goals
      * * Monitoring academic progress
      * * Supporting students with learning challenges
      * * Coordinating interventions and tutoring resources
   2. **Social/Emotional Development**
      * * Providing short-term counseling for issues like anxiety, bullying, grief, or peer conflict
      * * Teaching coping strategies and conflict resolution
      * * Promoting healthy relationships and emotional regulation
      * * Referring to mental health professionals when needed
   3. **Career and College Readiness**
      * * Assisting students in exploring careers and post-secondary options
      * * Helping with college applications, financial aid, and scholarships
      * * Administering career interest inventories and assessments
   4. **Crisis Intervention**
      * * Responding to school crises (e.g., deaths, natural disasters)
      * * Supporting students at risk of self-harm or harm to others
      * * Collaborating with crisis response teams
   5. **Collaboration and Advocacy**
      * * Partnering with parents, teachers, administrators, and outside agencies
      * * Advocating for equitable access to resources and opportunities
      * * Promoting a safe and inclusive school environment
* Be involved: Participate in school events, advisory councils, or volunteer opportunities.
* Share insights: Communicate your child’s strengths, challenges, and goals to support planning and services.
