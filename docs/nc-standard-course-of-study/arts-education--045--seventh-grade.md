# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Theatre Arts Standards Unpacking Seventh Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Theatre Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 theatre programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

7.CN.1.1 Analyze how theatre influenced various developing cultures around the world from the 1400s to the present.
Glossary
* Analyze - Identifying and examining separate parts as they function independently and together in creative works and study of [the] arts.22
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described the connections between the emergence of theatre and the development of ancient civilizations around the world prior to 1400. Now, they are analyzing how theatre influenced various developing cultures around the world from the 1400s to the present. In the next level, they will identify the influence of theatre in North Carolina and the nation on history, culture, and community.
Enduring Understanding
A theatre artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of stories, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How theatre developed from 1400 to the present
* How theatre influences cultures
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Drawing conclusions based on the identification and examination of the influence of theatre on cultures from 1400 to the present
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Similarities and differences in cultural shape an artist's perspective.
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts.
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare examples of theatre movements from 1400 to the present (e.g., Kabuki, Commedia dell’Arte, Harlem Renaissance theatre) that reflect cultural values and historical context.
* Demonstrate how to analyze a theatrical form by identifying its cultural influence and explaining how it shaped or reflected that culture’s beliefs, traditions, or identity.
* Guide student analysis through questioning, helping them make connections between theatrical elements and cultural development.
* Provide analytical tools such as graphic organizers, exemplar responses, and vocabulary supports to help students articulate how theatre has influenced global cultures.
* Monitor student writing and discussion for depth of analysis, accuracy of historical context, and correct use of theatre vocabulary through formative and summative assessment, offering feedback as needed.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
* Renaissance Europe
* American Theatre trends
* Native American storytelling traditions
* Japanese Theater- Kabuki, Bunraku
* Beijing Opera
* African Theatre
* Latin American Theatre

Glossary

* Genres: Category of art or design identified by similarities in form, subject matter, content, or technique.17
* Styles: Specific movement characteristics, qualities, or principles that give it distinctive identity; style may also refer to the unique artistic choices of a particular [artist].17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students adapted familiar stories with unique perspectives for theatrical performance. Now, they are adapting stories using a variety of theatrical styles or genres for theatre. In the next level, they will adapt historical or current events applying background knowledge and research to develop character and situations for theatre.
Enduring Understanding
Theatre artists balance inspiration and originality with ethics by creatively transforming or adapting other artistic ideas into new works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Procedures for adapting stories
* - A variety of theatrical styles and genres
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Modifying stories by putting differing theatrical styles and genres into them
Evidence of Mastery
As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art.
Key Insights
* Theatrical style focuses on "how" a story is told, while genre focuses on "what" the story is about
* There are a wide variety of theatrical styles and genres that can be used to tell familiar stories in unique ways
* The theatrical styles and genres chosen to tell a story can give a familiar work a fresh look
* Since this objective has the language “styles or genres” students may adapt either to master this objective

* Guide students as they explore and experiment with multiple theatrical styles and genres when adapting stories for performance, encouraging creativity and risk-taking
* Provide materials, prompts, and inspiration to encourage bold, imaginative adaptations that stretch students’ artistic choices
* Monitor individual students’ ability to adapt stories using a variety of theatrical styles or genres through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities

Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Recognizing, listing, or naming the legal and ethical issues to be considered when using another theatre artist's ideas in the creation of new work
   * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

   * Demonstrate how personal and cultural background can influence what someone enjoys or connects with and model reflective statements like “I connect with this version because it reminds me of a story in my family”
   * Guide students in comparing their preferences to others’ and identifying how social or cultural experiences shape different reactions to the same work
   * Provide response tools that encourage comparison and reflection and foster discussions that affirm diverse perspectives
   * Monitor individual students' ability to compare personal preferences in theatrical works as it relates to social or cultural background through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Definitions of social and cultural background
   * Definitions of subjective vs objective
   * What is art / drama? What is the function of art /drama in society? How does art / drama reflect social or cultural background?

7.RE.2.2 Evaluate choices made in devised or scripted performance, based on objective criteria.
Glossary
   * Criteria - The rules or guidelines used for categorizing or judging; in arts assessment, the rules or guidelines used to judge the quality of a student’s performance.28
   * Devised Theatre - Creation of an original performance piece by an ensemble.17
   * Evaluate - A judgment about the worth or quality of something. In education, data from tests, tasks, or performances are used to make judgments about the success of the student or program.28
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students defined constructive criticism for theatrical works. Now, they are evaluating choices made in devised or scripted performance, based on objective criteria. In the next level, they will evaluate the theatrical work of others with a specific plan for improvement.
Enduring Understanding
Providing feedback through objective critique is imperative in improving artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * Objective criteria
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Assessing the quality of choices
   * -Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * It is important to stick to objective criteria for students to review works.
   * It is okay to agree to disagree.
   * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
   * Since this objective has the language “devised or scripted” either situation may be considered
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare objective performance criteria and discuss how they apply to different types of work
   * Demonstrate how to evaluate specific choices using language from the criteria
   * Guide students in analyzing the impact of choices and suggesting revisions
   * Provide opportunities to reflect on peer and personal work and apply feedback in revision
   * Monitor individual students' ability to evaluate choices made in devised or scripted performance based on objective criteria through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Definitions of Subjective versus Objective.
   * Definition of refinement, tightening, and bold choices
   * The difference between performer choice and director's vision.
