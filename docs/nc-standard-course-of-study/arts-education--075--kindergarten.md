# Arts Education - Kindergarten

Parent Guide for Kindergarten Dance

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for dance is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with dance builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed dance educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn that dance is common in communities around the world and that it is part of everyday life—at home, at school, and in their community. Students also learn about common physical challenges dancers experience.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn that there are different roles involved in the creation of dance and that dance can be created and presented with technology and digital tools.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students learn to move spontaneously in improvisation to words and sounds and create movements for the beginning, middle, and end of dance routines. Students also learn how to record their movement ideas on paper by drawing a picture or using a symbol.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to adapt movements by changing how large or small a movement is performed and examine the differences between original and copied dance ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn to use feedback from instructors to improve their dance skills and to demonstrate an awareness of how their body moves when performing basic motor skills. Students also use guidance to help them perform with focus when dancing with others.

PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to make comparisons of topics or themes for improvisation or an informal dance presentation and to use instructional support to assist with demonstrating safe and respectful behaviors when performing and observing informal performances. Students also explore how dancing with a simple prop, such as a scarf or ribbon, can impact performance.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students use instructional support to describe movements that demonstrate how the elements of dance (body, space, time, effort quality) are used in dance, and also describe how various dance movements make them feel.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine personal preferences in dance and use instructional support to identify positive feedback in self and peer-evaluation of work.

How to Support Your Dance Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your dance student:
* Clear a little space so your child can move freely and safely.
* Talk about how dance can be part of celebrations, traditions, or play.
* Point out dance when observed in different cultural settings and traditions.
* Talk about how different movements use different parts of the body. Explore the range of motion that different body parts allow.
* Play an improvisation game where your child comes up with movements to words, such as “wiggle”, “pop”, or sounds like “swoosh”.
* Help your child create a dance with three movements for the beginning, middle, and end.
* Encourage your child to draw a picture of their favorite move, like a jump, spin, or pose.
* Use scarves, stuffed animals, or ribbons to explore how props change movement.
* Watch dance together and describe what the dancers are doing and the feelings or emotions that are evoked.
* Practice giving compliments when watching your child dance.
