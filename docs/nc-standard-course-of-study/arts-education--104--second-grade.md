# Arts Education - Second Grade

Parent Guide for Second Grade General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore American music styles and composers, including those of various cultural groups in the US, along with cross-curricular connections and uses of music.
CN.2 Explore - Students look at who makes and uses music, along with why they use innovative technologies.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise and notate both rhythms and melodies.
CR.2 Adapt - Students make musical accompaniments to stories. They will also learn how to share both physical and digital copies of music fairly.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve their performance of a variety of rounds and ostinatos, honing their ability to demonstrate a variety of rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students examine what it takes to put on a musical performance, as well as demonstrate both appropriate audience and performer etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, an artist’s intent, and timbres of instruments in a variety of music.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine how music is valued differently by different people before practicing giving objective feedback.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to sing or tell you about the songs they are learning at school
* Ask students to identify a musical element in the music they listen to at home
* Talk to students about why we shouldn't pirate music or other works of art
* Discuss the music your family likes, and how it is ok for others to like and listen to other musical styles and genres
* Point out the various times your family uses music in your daily lives: during traditions, celebrations, entertainment, or other uses
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
* Play games that encourage melodic improvisation, like singing what they wore today
