# Healthful Living - High School

North Carolina Standard Course of Study
Physical Education K-12

MS = Motor Skills, MC = Movement Concepts, HF = Health-Related Fitness, PR = Personal/Social Responsibility

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Physical Education.

Ninth Grade
Motor Skills (MS)
Essential Standard:
9.MS.1 Demonstrate motor skills and movement patterns needed to perform a variety of physical activities.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MS.1.1 Integrate specialized skills and combination skills appropriately during live game play.
Verb Defined:
to form, coordinate, or blend into a functioning or unified whole: to incorporate into a larger unit
Student will know and be able to:
* Combine proficiency and refinement in locomotor, non-locomotor, and manipulative skills through appropriate activities (e.g., orienteering, rock climbing, cycling, disc golf, lifetime activities, fitness activities, dance and rhythmic activities, aquatics, individual performance activities, games and sports [net/wall, striking/fielding, and goal/target]).
* Combine and apply movement patterns, simple to complex, in aquatic, rhythms/dance, and individual and dual activities.
* Merge the skill-related components of balance, reaction time, agility, coordination, explosive power, and speed to enhance performance levels in aquatic, rhythms/dance, and individual and dual activities.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist for integration of both specialized and combination skills to assess the integration of these skills used in game play.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MS.1.2 Implement appropriate strategies and tactics based on varying game conditions.
Verb Defined:
To carry out : accomplish
Student will know and be able to:
* Implement appropriate offensive and defensive strategies in both modified and non-modified team sports, games, and activities.
* Apply advanced offensive, defensive, and transition strategies at appropriate times during game play.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist or rubric for identified activities to assess implementation of appropriate strategies and tactics based on the game situation encountered.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MS.1.3 Demonstrate basic and advanced skills to participate successfully in lifetime individual activities.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
* Demonstrate competency and/or refine activity-specific movement skills in two or more lifetime activities (outdoor pursuits, individual-performance activities, aquatics, net/wall games or target games).
* Demonstrate proficient movement skills in aquatic, rhythms/dance, and individual and dual activities.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist or rubric for identified skills to evaluate key components or provide verbal positive/corrective feedback through teacher or peers.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MS.1.4 Design and perform various movement combinations that demonstrate an understanding of rhythm and coordination to the beat of music.
Verb Defined:
To conceive or execute a plan : to draw, lay out, or prepare
To do in a formal manner or according to prescribed ritual : fulfill : carry out : do
Student will know and be able to:
* Demonstrate competency in dance forms used in cultural and social occasions (e.g., weddings, parties), or demonstrate competency in one form of dance.
* Demonstrate competency in a form of dance by choreographing a dance or by giving a performance.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist or rubric for each movement combination to evaluate understanding of rhythm and keeping to the beat.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MS.1.5 Apply correct technique for a variety of cardiorespiratory endurance, resistance training, and flexibility exercises that can lead into lifetime activity
Verb Defined:
To put to use especially for some practical purpose : to bring into action
Student will know and be able to:
* Apply competency in 2 or more specialized skills in health-related fitness activities.
* Apply, develop, and maintain the 5 components of physical fitness in enjoyable and challenging physical activities.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist to evaluate correct techniques of identified exercises in cardiorespiratory endurance, resistance training, and flexibility.
Movement Concepts (MC)
Essential Standard:
9.MC.2 Demonstrate understanding of concepts that apply to the learning and performance of movement.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MC.2.1 Integrate concepts and principles related to movement and performance in static or dynamic settings.
Verb Defined:
To form, coordinate, or blend into a functioning or unified whole : to incorporate into a larger unit
Student will know and be able to:
* Integrate movement skills learned in one physical activity into another physical activity.
* Merge biomechanical principles of movement related to weight transfer, balance, absorption, and application of force in static and dynamic settings.
* Integrate concepts of effort, flow, space, and time into establishment of mechanically correct form in static or dynamic settings. (moving into position, establishing a balanced base, preparatory phase, movement phase, follow through, and return to base)
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist or rubric to evaluate concepts and principles students integrate in a stationary or movement setting.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MC.2.2 Adapt strategies and tactics based on game conditions and situations.
Verb Defined:
To make fit, often by modification
Student will know and be able to:
* Change offensive, defensive, and cooperative strategies according to games, activities and sports situations.
* Modify current strategies to offset the opponent’s strategies.
Assessment Example:
Students will identify and evaluate game situations and adapt tactics and strategies to be successful in game situations.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.MC.2.3 Construct a plan to improve overall performance based on mechanics of movement principles.
Verb Defined:
To make or form by combining or arranging parts or elements
Student will know and be able to:

* Construct an improvement plan using movement concepts and principles (e.g., force, motion, rotation) to analyze and improve performance of self and/or others in a selected skill.
* Formulate a plan using the principles of biomechanics (leverage, force, inertia, rotary motion, opposition, and buoyancy) to achieve advanced performance in aquatic, rhythms/dance, and individual and dual activities.
Assessment Example:
A plan is constructed that identifies and evaluates key components that improves overall performance based on mechanics of movement principles.

Health-Related Fitness (HF)
Essential Standard:
9.HF.3 Demonstrate the knowledge and skills needed to achieve a health-enhancing level of physical activity and fitness.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.HF.3.1 Apply FITT and training principles to improve a self-selected component of health-related fitness.
Verb Defined:
To put to use especially for some practical purpose : to bring into action
Student will know and be able to:
* Apply the FITT (frequency, intensity, time, type of exercise) principle and other principles of training, such as overload, specificity, and progression, in accordance with personal goals, to a personal fitness plan that will result in improving health-related fitness.
* Improve and maintain physical fitness by adjusting physical activity levels according to the training principles of exercise.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist to evaluate the application of FITT and other training principles to improve a self-selected component of health-related fitness.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.HF.3.2 Analyze factors that affect personal health and physical activity behaviors to determine appropriate goals and strategies to improve health-related outcomes.
Verb Defined:
To study or determine the nature and relationship of the parts of something
Student will know and be able to:
* Analyze available fitness resources in the community.
* Determine physical activities that can be performed if one’s physical fitness program is disrupted by inclement weather, travel from home or school, or a minor injury.
* Analyze personal behaviors and health issues that may positively or negatively affect health related outcomes.
Assessment Example:
Analyze factors that affect personal health and physical activity behaviors determining appropriate goals and strategies that improve health-related outcomes through bell ringers, exit tickets, Q&A, quiz, or open discussion.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.HF.3.3 Design and implement a personal physical activity program, based on current physical activity and fitness status, that applies SMART goal setting, FITT, and training principles.
Verb Defined:
To conceive or execute a plan : to draw, lay out, or prepare
To carry out : accomplish
Student will know and be able to:
* Demonstrate program-planning skills by assessing and analyzing personal fitness levels, setting goals, devising strategies, making timelines for a personal physical fitness plan, and evaluating the components and progress of the personal fitness plan.
* Develop a two-week personal physical fitness plan specifying the proper warm-up and cool-down activities and the principles of exercise for each of the five components of health related physical fitness.
Assessment Example:
Design and implement a physical activity plan that incorporates current fitness status, SMART goal setting, and FITT principles.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.HF.3.4 Participate in targeted activities based on interests, accessibility, and personal goals to improve health-related fitness.
Verb Defined:
To take part : to have a part or share in something
Student will know and be able to:
* Participate in moderate to vigorous exercise at least four days a week in activities chosen based on personal preference.
* Participate in moderate to vigorous exercise at least four days a week in activities chosen to improve health related fitness. preference.
Assessment Example:
Record fitness/activity log indicating interests, accessibility to favored activities and the accomplishment of personal health related fitness goals.

Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
Essential Standard:
9.PR.4 Demonstrate responsible personal and social behavior that respects self and others.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.PR.4.1 Implement leadership skills to promote responsibility in self and others.
Verb Defined:
To carry out : accomplish
Student will know and be able to:
* Assume a leadership role (e.g., task or group leader, referee, coach) in a physical activity setting.
Assessment Example:
Students implement leadership skills during group physical activity or team play. An observational checklist can be created to indicate skills taught are in evidence during group activities.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.PR.4.2 Develop and execute group norms to establish and achieve group goals in competitive as well as cooperative physical activity settings.
Verb Defined:
To create or produce especially by deliberate effort over time to : expand by a process of growth
To carry (something) out fully : to put (something) completely into effect
Student will know and be able to:
* Develop group norms that guide student interaction in both cooperative and competitive physical activity settings.
* Enact group norms that promote cooperation in both cooperative and competitive physical activity settings.
Assessment Example:
Group norms are developed and shared by students. Group goals are established. Goals are then assessed as to whether or not they have been met through bell ringer, exit ticket, or open discussion.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.PR.4.3 Apply relationship skills and strategies that promote team/group dynamics and inclusion.
Verb Defined:
To put to use especially for some practical purpose : to bring into action
Student will know and be able to:
* Apply communication skills and strategies, problem-solving, and critical-thinking skills in physical activity settings that promote positive team/group dynamics.
* Apply citizenship by demonstrating respecting diversity and including others in physical activity settings.
Assessment Example:
Students apply relationship skills while engaged in group situations. An observational checklist can be created to indicate skills taught are in evidence during group situations.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.9.PR.4.4 Model leadership skills, effective communication, problem solving, and conflict resolution to promote positive team/group dynamics.
Verb Defined:
An example for imitation or emulation
Student will know and be able to:
* Exhibit proper etiquette, respect for others and teamwork while engaging in physical activity and/or social dance.
* Exhibit appropriate leadership skills while engaging in physical activity and/or social dance.
* Model conflict-resolution skills in physical activity settings.
Assessment Example:
Students model skills while engaged in group situations. An observational checklist can be created to indicate skills taught are in evidence during group situations.
