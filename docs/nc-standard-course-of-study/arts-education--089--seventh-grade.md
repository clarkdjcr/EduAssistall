# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

Parent Guide for Seventh Grade Dance
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for dance is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with dance builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed dance educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students examine the influence dance has had in various cultures and how skills and processes learned in dance can be used in academic study and daily life. Students also examine how different strategies help to address physical alignment and mental stressors common to dance.
CN.2 Explore - Students examine how dance skills, characteristics, and behaviors prepare them for success in postsecondary education and careers. They also describe how digital media has been used in a variety of dance performances.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students explore spontaneous movement invention in response to prompts from a partner or group and create dances using abstract movement phrases and simple structures. Students also use pictures, writing, symbols, or media technology to document a dance sequence.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to adapt dance phrases by changing the movement dynamics, and also explore both legal and ethical issues around creating work inspired by other artists.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use feedback from instructors, peers, and self-evaluation to improve performance and demonstrate movement skills with strength, anatomical alignment, flexibility, stamina, and coordination. Students also demonstrate movement qualities and execute each step in a dance clearly and completely.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop a theme or topic, including music choices for a group performance, and identify safe and respectful behavioral expectations for dance in a variety of cultural settings. Students also compare how a variety of production elements could strengthen the meaning of a dance.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students explain how the elements of dance (body, space, time, effort quality) are used in different dance techniques, and also examine how personal experiences and perspectives shape interpretations of dance.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students consider how personal preferences for dance might change when looking at dance through multiple perspectives and use student-created criteria to provide feedback in evaluation of dance performance and choreography.

How to Support Your Dance Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your dance student:
* Clear a little space so your child can move freely and safely.
* Talk about any dance influencers on social media your child may know and discuss their impact.
* Encourage your child to connect dance skills, such as focus, memory, and teamwork, to schoolwork or household tasks.
* Support healthy habits by encouraging your child to stretch, rest, and use calming techniques like deep breathing to reduce stress.
* Talk about skills and behaviors dancers need, such as discipline, creativity, and persistence, and how these prepare them for future college and career opportunities.
* Encourage your child to create a short dance phrase using everyday actions in creative ways, then have them draw symbols, write notes, or record a video of their dance.
* Remind your child of posture, strength, and safe movement when practicing at home.
* Compare the use of dance elements in different dance styles, such as in hip-hop versus ballet.
* Have your child identify areas of strength and need for improvement in video recordings of their dancing.
