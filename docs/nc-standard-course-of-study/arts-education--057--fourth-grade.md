# Arts Education - Fourth Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Fourth Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

4.CN.1.1 Describe how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina.
Glossary
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Customs - A usage or practice common to many or to a particular place or class or habitual with an individual; the whole body of usages, practices, or conventions that regulate social life.14
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Indigenous - Of or relating to the earliest known inhabitants of a place and especially of a place that was colonized by a now-dominant group.14
* Traditions - Pattern of practices and beliefs within a societal group.17
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students compared the purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies. At the current level, students describe how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina and broaden the scope of influence to the United States in the next level.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina
* Visual Artists who have influenced the customs, traditions or history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina
* Visual artists whose work was influenced by Indigenous and cultural groups in North Carolina
* Examples of visual artwork that reflects customs, traditions or history of indigenous and other cultural groups from North Carolina
* How the customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina may have influenced or been influenced by the field of visual arts
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* North Carolina visual artists are influenced by customs, traditions, and their cultures
* Note the conjunction “and” used three times in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how the visual arts have influenced, AND been influenced by the customs, traditions, AND history of indigenous AND other cultural groups in North Carolina. Given the scope, this objective will likely be broken apart into multiple learning activities over the course of study but ultimately the student must master the entirety of the content included in the objective
* This objective can be paired with CR.1.1 as students create personal artwork and RE objectives
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give examples of how the customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina have influenced, or been influenced by visual arts
* Expose students to the art found in, and influenced by, diverse cultures in North Carolina
* Facilitate student discussions describing artwork found in, and influenced by various cultures in North Carolina
* Provide opportunity for students to describe how the arts have influenced and been influenced by Indigenous and other cultures in North Carolina
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Comparison chart of how the arts have impacted and been impacted by customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and different cultural groups
* Collages
* Beading, wood, pottery, weaving
* Virtual museum tour

4.CR.2.2 Identify how to responsibly use resources for online access of visual artworks.
Glossary
* Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
* Use - To put into action or service.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students identified visual art works that can be used by everyone fairly and now identify how to responsibly use resources for online access of visual artworks. In the next level, students will learn to cite artists correctly when utilizing or adapting their original artwork.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Appropriate procedures for accessing visual artworks online
* How to use resources responsibly
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming the process for responsibly accessing resources for visual artworks online
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “How do I access art online?” to show mastery of this objective
* “Resources” include tools, websites, databases, apps, and digital platforms such as search engines, video-sharing sites, and social media. These are the spaces where students gain access to art, whether it’s in the public domain, hosted by a museum, or part of a subscription-based service
* Students may have false assumptions about how to responsibly access resources for visual arts
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students combine ideas from multiple sources to create artwork. Students would first learn how to responsibly access resources and then use their findings to create work in mastery of CR.2.1
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of responsible use of resources for online access of visual arts
* Guide student-driven investigation of online resource issues when creating works inspired by other artists or sharing another artist’s work
* Provide learning opportunities for the student to identify how to use resources for online access of visual artworks responsibly
* Create formative and summative assessments of the student’s ability to identify how to responsibly use resources for online access of visual artworks
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* A pre- and -post check for understanding might help students to see what they originally knew of responsible use of resources
* Have students identify if, or when, it would be appropriate to use other source material
* Teacher could project a scenario or a resource on the board and have students decide if it is appropriate or unethical (students can raise thumbs up/down or raise their hands, etc)
