# Arts Education - Intermediate

Parent Guide for Intermediate Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed visual arts educator.

Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how historical, societal, and cultural perspectives have impacted visual arts. They also solve artistic problems using skills and knowledge from other disciplines or experiences.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how the creative process is used in college and career challenges and examine how art is created, edited, presented, and evaluated using technology platforms and digital and social media.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students use creativity to make art based on everyday, practical items and personal expression, as well as create works on personal experiences and observations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to respectfully borrow ideas from other artists or cultures when creating personal work and evaluate the ethics involved in making, producing, and distributing both original works and adaptations.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students use specific criteria when making improvements to their work while also considering their unique personal style. Students also create a statement that communicates the materials, techniques, and art-making process used to create a work.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to select the best format for a portfolio presentation and examine how different venues shape interpretations of an exhibition or collection.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn how to identify concepts, themes, and viewpoints that are both visible and suggested in works of art and take multiple perspectives into account when interpreting the purpose or intent of a work of art.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explain what they like about works of art and create criteria to analyze and evaluate personal artwork or the work of others.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Look at art in books, museums, or online and discuss how history or culture influenced it.
* Encourage your child to use skills and knowledge from other subjects or personal experiences in their art making.
* Examine how different creative design processes are used in college and careers.
* Look at how technology, including digital and social media, is used to create and present art.
* Encourage art making that expresses personal experiences and observations.
* Talk about borrowing ideas from artists and other cultures respectfully.
* Ask your child to explain the process and choices they made in their artwork.
* Help them gather their artwork into a simple digital or binder portfolio.
* Look at a piece of art and discuss the skills and techniques the artist used and the story or meaning of the work.
* Encourage your child to share why they like certain artworks.
