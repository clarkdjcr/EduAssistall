# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

Parent Guide for Seventh Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students examine the influence visual artists have had in various cultures and how skills and information learned in other content areas can be used in solving artistic problems.
CN.2 Explore - Students examine how art skills, characteristics, and behaviors prepare them for success in postsecondary education and careers. They also explore the various tools and innovations used to make and produce art for the media or everyday surroundings.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students experiment with materials, techniques, and processes in traditional and creative ways and create original works that express ideas, feelings, or personal experiences.
CR.2 Adapt - Students create new work by changing the materials or tools used in the images in another artist’s work. They also explore both legal and ethical issues around creating work inspired by other artists.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students learn to apply feedback from instructors or peers to improve artwork and create a statement that describes multiple aspects of their work.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn to organize their work that shows a variety of art-making skills and make comparisons how art can be experienced differently depending on the display method.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students examine how contemporary art is different from other art movements or periods and how personal experiences and art skills shape interpretations of art.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students compare what they like about several works of art and use criteria to analyze and evaluate artwork.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Discuss how skills from other subjects show up in art.
* Examine how skills and behaviors learned in visual arts can prepare for success in opportunities beyond high school.
* Discuss the different tools artists use to create art for digital spaces or places in the local community.
* Encourage trying new materials such as paint, clay, digital apps, or recycled items when making art.
* Encourage your child to create original artwork that expresses their ideas, feelings, or a personal experience.
* Look at an artwork together and ask your child how they might create their own version using different tools.
* Discuss why it’s important to give credit to original artists when using their ideas.
* Ask your child to explain what they changed in their artwork and why.
* Create a mini gallery at home and discuss how different ways of displaying art might change how it feels.
* Help them write short artist statements describing their materials, techniques, and choices.
* Look at contemporary and historical artworks together and discuss differences.
* Look at artwork and have your child describe what they see and interpret.
* Expose your child to multiple works of art and have them compare what they like about the different works.
