# Arts Education - Second Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Dance Standards Unpacking Second Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Dance is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Dance builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Dance standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Dance Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Dance programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

2.CN.1.1 Describe how American dance reflects the heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States, including various indigenous and cultural groups.
Glossary
* Cultural - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Customs - A usage or practice common to many or to a particular place or class or habitual with an individual; the whole body of usages, practices, or conventions that regulate social life.14
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Heritage - Something transmitted by or acquired from a predecessor; Tradition.14
* Indigenous - Of or relating to the earliest known inhabitants of a place and especially of a place that was colonized by a now-dominant group.14
* Traditions - Pattern of practices and beliefs within a societal group.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students explained how dance reflects cultures, values, and ideas. At the current level, students describe how American dance can reflect the heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States, including various indigenous and cultural groups. In the next level, students will describe dance forms that exist in their local community or region.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a dance artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shapes the creation of movements, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* What is meant by American dance
* What is meant by heritage, customs, and traditions
* Examples of the heritage, customs, and traditions of diverse cultures that exist in America
* How dance can reflect cultural heritage, customs, and traditions
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of how dance reflects cultural celebrations, traditions, and artistic practices in the United States
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how American dance reflects the diverse heritage, customs AND traditions of people in the United States including various indigenous AND cultural groups. Depending on the learning activity, the heritage, customs, and traditions of people, being sure to have students include of various indigenous and cultural groups in the United States can be assessed simultaneously or broken apart to be assessed through multiple projects over the course of study but ultimately students must master the entirety of the objective
* The instruction could include physical dance instruction as well as historical information that is delivered to students so that the standard is met by relating the dance style with the history of the group from which it comes
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of how American dance reflects diverse customs, traditions, and heritages of various people and cultures in the United States
* Model describing how American dance reflects the heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States including various indigenous and cultural groups
* Guide student exploration in discovering how American dance reflects cultural heritage, customs, and traditions of diverse populations in the United States
* Design learning activities for students to summarize the characteristics of the ways through which American dance reflects the heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States including various indigenous and cultural groups
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how American dance reflects the heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States including various indigenous and cultural groups

* This objective builds on knowledge of anatomical challenges learned in Kindergarten. A review of anatomical challenges will be necessary. See the Kindergarten unpacking document for reference of teacher actions and activities
* Because of the physical demands of dance, bodies may encounter challenges related to overuse, joint and muscle injuries, fatigue, etc.

* Design learning activities for students to identify strategies to address anatomical challenges encountered by dancers
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to identify strategies to address anatomical challenges encountered by dancers
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Stretching routines for increased flexibility and range of mobility
* How to build healthy muscles through nutrition and exercise
* Applying instruction and feedback on how to correctly execute movement anatomically
* How to use modifications of movements or scaffolding of instruction to meet ability such as executing a pirouette at various levels: just the relevé for beginners, a quarter or half turn, a full turn, or more than a full turn for advanced movers
* Opportunities that allow dancers to explore their own flexibility and mobility, monitoring their personal limitations throughout the year, including foot and toe function, joint mobility, respiratory endurance, and muscle strength

2.CR.2.1 Adapt a dance sequence by changing the levels of the movements.
Glossary
* Adapt - To change, or to change something, to suit different conditions or uses.4
* Dance Sequence - A brief sequence of movements that are not necessarily ordered into a formal choreographic structure that communicates artistic intent but rather demonstrates a solution to a movement problem. A movement sequence could contain as few as two or movements.29
* Spatial Levels - The altitude of a movement or shape (high, middle, low) in relationship to its distance from the floor.29
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students adapted a dance sequence by changing the body shapes and now adapt a dance sequence by changing the spatial levels. In the next level, students will adapt a dance sequence by changing the directions or pathways of the movements.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that dancers and choreographers balance inspiration and originality with ethics by creatively transforming or adapting other artistic ideas into new works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* An original dance sequence for manipulation
* How to alter the spatial levels of movement
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Modifying spatial levels used in original dance sequences to create new movement sequences
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students may need assistance understanding how to adapt movements such as roll and spin, where roll can be done on a low level and spin is a similar movement that can be done on a high level, or making a bouncing movement on a low level and a jumping movement on a high level
* Changing the level of movements is one of the choreographic tools used to manipulate motifs to create new movement. Students can be challenged to change the level of any movement
* Students may find that as they change the levels of movement the transition used in and out of movements will also change. This may impact the timing of the movement so the tempo may also need to change so that they can appropriately perform their movements
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives as students create, perform, and respond to dance
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Create classroom activities that support student creation and manipulation of choreography
* Demonstrate how to adapt the spatial levels used in movement motifs
* Ask for volunteer ideas for how a variety of motifs might be performed on different spatial levels
* Provide practice for all students to contribute ideas for how to adapt the spatial levels of movement motifs used in choreography
* Create original choreography for students to adapt or guide students in generating original material to be used
* Design a learning activity in which students can adapt the spatial levels of movement motifs in a pre-existing dance sequence
* Guide and support the creative process
* Create formative and summative assessment on the student’s ability to adapt a dance sequence by changing the levels of the movement
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Teach students a dance sequence using middle level movements and task them to perform all the motifs on a high level and then again, using the low level, or create a sequence using only high or low movements and have students perform them using the opposite level
* Teach students a sequence with simple movements that can be adapted. Ask students to recall the sequence and change the movement levels throughout, giving them the choice in which motifs to perform on which levels

2.CR.2.2 Explain how to share dance fairly.
Glossary
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Fairness - Complying with appropriate, ethical, and equitable rules and guidelines.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students explained how and when to credit others when using their ideas in dance and now explain how to share dance fairly. In the next level, students will identify dances that can be used by everyone fairly.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How dance choreography is shared
* What “fairly” looks like in practice (getting permission or limiting use)
* How to credit artists for their work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the process for sharing dance fairly
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “How do I ethically share someone else’s work?” to show mastery of this objective
* Students should be able to explain that artists choose how and when they want their works to be shared. Just as students want others to respect when and how others share their artwork, they need to respect when and how other artists want their work to be shared
* An age-appropriate explanation of fair-use could include respecting ownership, giving credit, using publicly available resources, and making decisions about sharing their own original work
* Sharing dance fairly means giving credit to the person who made it and not taking, exhibiting, posting online, showing, or copying it without asking
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Model and narrate these positive and ethical behaviors during instruction
* Prepare student-friendly definitions for understanding of “share” and “fairly” keeping in mind ethical and legal issues
* Provide examples of dance that can be used fairly
* Guide classroom discussion about how to share dance choreography fairly
* Support student learning in how to explain how dance can shared fairly
* Provide learning activities in which students can explain how to share dance choreography fairly
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain how to share dance fairly
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Students can be paired to explain to their partners how to share dance fairly
* Students can be assisted in creating an audio/visual recording in which they explain how to share dance fairly
* Students can write a short paragraph in which they explain how to share dance fairly

* Students can observe a dance performed by other students or artists. The teacher can direct their attention to a specific sequence and identify multiple dance elements for students to look for. The students then describe how that sequence exemplified multiple elements of dance

It is important to understand personal bias to help develop how to appreciate dance objectively, regardless of personal preference
* This objective can be paired with PR.1.2 if the teacher creates classroom routines inclusive of various styles, forms, and genres for students to learn and then have students perform to demonstrate motor skills with body awareness
* Note the conjunction “or” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to explain preferences in performing dances from various styles, forms, OR genres. It is up to teacher discretion as to which students are learning and performing
* Note that this objective is plural - The student should be performing dance of multiple styles, forms, or genres over the course of study
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Lead classroom discussion on how preferences in dance can vary based on the individual
* Demonstrate explaining preferences in performing dances from a variety of styles, forms, or genres (be mindful not to impart bias but allow students to have differing perspectives)
* Provide examples of dance in a wide array of styles, forms, or genres for physical embodiment
* Guide student discovery of preferences when performing dance from different styles, forms, or genres
* Provide sentence stems to help students form descriptive sentences about their preferences  when performing various dance styles, forms, or genres
* Design activities for students to explain personal preferences in performing dances from various styles, forms, or genres
* Create formative and summative assessment on the student’s ability to explain preferences in performing dances from various styles, forms, or genres
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Design activities that ask students to explain what they like and dislike about performing a style, form, or genre
* Students can pair-share or contribute to a larger group discussion
* Four corners activity can have students grouping by level of preferences and then they can discuss in smaller groups
* Students can write a short reflection explaining their preferences as an assignment or exit-ticket activity
2.RE.2.2 Identify positive feedback and areas for improvement for self and others with guidance.
Glossary
* Feedback - A reaction or response to a particular design problem or design solution.29
* Guidance - Assistance provided temporarily to enable a student to perform a task that would be difficult to perform unaided, best implemented in a manner that helps develop that student’s capacity to eventually perform the task independently.17
* Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
* Improve - To enhance in value or quality : make better.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students provided positive feedback for self and others and now use instructional support to identify positive feedback and areas for improvement for self and others. In the next level, students master this same objective independently.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that providing feedback through objective critique is imperative in improving artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to identify the positive aspects of personal work with instructional support
* How to identify areas for improvement in personal work with instructional support
* How to identify the positive aspects of another's work with instructional support
* How to identify areas for improvement in another's work with instructional support
* How to linguistically discuss movement performance and choreography
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming the strengths and areas for improvement in personal and peer work with instructional support
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students understand that they can use feedback to improve and also give feedback to help someone else improve. It is important to identify strengths when evaluating work so students build efficacy and can continue to build on their success. Identifying areas for growth is an important aspect of constructive criticism. Students may need support in understanding the positive intention of constructive criticism
* Note the conjunction “and” used twice in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to identify positive feedback AND areas for improvement for self AND others with guidance. Both feedback that is positive and identifying areas of growth needs to be given for both self and others
* This objective can be paired with PR.1.1 in which students improve dance skills by applying feedback from instructors, peers, and self
* This objective can also be paired with other CR and PR objectives
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Lead classroom discussion on how dancers grow from constructive feedback
* Guide students on how to use a simple rubric to identify qualities of work
* Provide opportunities for students to compliment and identify areas in need of improvement in their work and the work of their peers. This can be through demonstration of skills learned or in performance of student-generated work
* Provide examples of how to identify positive feedback and areas for improvement to self or others
* Provide sentence stems to help students form positive feedback and suggestions of areas for improvement on aspects of dance creation or performance
* Design activities for students to identify positive feedback and areas for improvement for self and others with guidance
* Create formative and summative assessment on the student’s ability to identify positive feedback and areas for improvement for self and others with guidance
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Use positive behavior support to provide expectations on how to give feedback on strengths and areas of growth when watching performances of self and others
* Have students perform something they have learned or choreographed. Film the students performing and then have students give feedback on strengths and areas for improvement about their own performance and to each other by using a feedback form or sharing feedback verbally
* Provide sentence stems or wordbanks for support
* Direct line of questioning can also be used to offer support to students in prompting their feedback responses
