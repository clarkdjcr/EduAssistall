# Arts Education - Arts Ed Administrator Guides

Administrator Guide - ARTS EDUCATION
A K-12 TEACHER EVALUATION SUPPORT TOOL FOR NCEES STANDARDS 1-5
Vision for Arts Education: In today's globally competitive world, innovative thinking and creativity are essential for all school children. High quality, standards-based instruction in the arts develops these skills and effectively engages, retains, and prepares future-ready students for graduation and success in an entrepreneurial economy. Dance, Music, Theatre Arts, and Visual Arts, taught by licensed arts educators and integrated throughout the curriculum, are critical to North Carolina's 21st Century Education. (North Carolina Senate Bill 66: Comprehensive Arts Education Task Force, 2010)
ARTS EDUCATION STANDARDS
The North Carolina Arts Education Standards provide a comprehensive framework for each of the arts disciplines (Dance, Music, Theatre Arts, and Visual Arts) in the public schools. The 2024 Arts Education Standards communicate what students should know and be able to do as a result of sequential instruction at each grade level (K-8) or proficiency level (9-12): beginning, intermediate, accomplished, and advanced. Note: Vocal and Instrumental Music has a slightly different structure described below.
The intent of the Arts Education Standards is that a comprehensive understanding of one or more of the arts be accomplished by each student throughout the K-12 program. In addition to satisfying the arts education graduation requirement, students at the high school level can study an individual arts discipline as an area of interest or to prepare them for further education and/or a career in the arts.
Each Arts Discipline Has Standards That Include:
* CN.1: Relate art forms and artists to past and present events
* CN.2: Explore the future of the art form and cross-disciplinary skills
* CR.1: Create original works
* CR.2: Adapt works ethically and legally
* PR.1: Present works with technical accuracy and expression
* PR.2: Develop as performers, presenters, audiences, and production crew
* RE.1: Analyze works and artistic techniques
* RE.2: Evaluate works objectively and without bias
PREPARING STUDENTS FOR SUCCESS
A student’s learning experiences must include the acquisition of rigorous academic content and the durable skills and mindsets students need to thrive in the post-secondary plan of their choice.
Arts Education courses support the development of the durable skills specified in the North Carolina Portrait of a Graduate: Adaptability, Collaboration, Communication, Critical Thinking, Empathy, Learner’s Mindset, and Personal Responsibility. These, and other college, career, and post-secondary skills, allows for deeper student learning and skill attainment and bridges the gap between K-12 education and workforce readiness. These durable skills are expressly supported in the Arts Education Standards in the CN.2.1 objective at every grade level and are also present throughout a variety of objectives developing a student’s ability to engage deeply with both content and others in their classrooms.
COMPREHENSIVE ARTS EDUCATION (CAE)
 CAE circle, broken into equal thirds: Arts Education, Arts Integration, Arts Exposure

CAE in North Carolina is a three-pronged approach helping students develop these skills as well as effectively engage, retain, and prepare them for graduation and success in tomorrow’s global economy. These three components of CAE are interdependent and are all necessary.
Arts Education: Standards-based instruction in Dance, Music, Theatre Arts, and Visual Arts taught by licensed arts educators in fully resourced classrooms.
Arts Integration: Connecting arts with other content areas so students learn both simultaneously, deepening mastery and developing critical and creative skills.
Arts Exposure: Experiences inside and outside school—residencies, performances, gallery visits—show how the arts function beyond the classroom and rely on collaboration among schools, communities, and arts partners.

THE ARTS EDUCATION CLASSROOM…
Students should consistently engage in all four artistic processes: creating, presenting, responding, and connecting.
* Creating = generating or adapting artistic ideas under teacher guidance.
* Presenting = rehearsing, performing, or sharing completed work.
* Responding = analyzing and interpreting meaning in artistic works.
* Connecting = linking artistic ideas to personal experience, history, culture, current events, and future implications.
Suggested Course Progression
The North Carolina Arts Education Standards communicate what students should know and be able to do as a result of instruction at each high school proficiency level: beginning; intermediate; accomplished (shown below as proficient, updated in 2024); and advanced. Note: Vocal and Instrumental Music have a different, five-proficiency pathway.
In Dance, General Music, Theatre Arts, Technical Theatre, and Visual Arts:
* Beginning: Standards are for students with no or limited K-8 progression in the arts education discipline (dance, music, theatre arts, or visual arts).
* Note: Vocal & Instrumental Music uses two proficiency levels in place of the one Beginning level. The base level, novice, can be started in middle school or at any point in a student’s musical career.
   * Novice: Standards are for students with no or limited K-8 progression in vocal or instrumental music (band, choir, orchestra, percussion, modern band, guitar, etc).
   * Developing: Standards are for students who have achieved novice level standards in vocal or instrumental music (band, choir, orchestra, percussion, modern band, guitar, etc).
ALL ARTS AREAS:
↓
Intermediate
Standards are for students who have had a complete K-8 progression or who have achieved beginning level standards in the arts education discipline (dance, music, theatre arts, or visual arts).
↓
Accomplished
Standards are for students who have achieved intermediate level standards in the arts education discipline (dance, music, theatre arts, or visual arts).
↓
Advanced
Standards are for students who have achieved accomplished level standards in the arts education discipline (dance, music, theatre arts, or visual arts).
APPROPRIATE EXPECTATIONS
Elementary arts classes typically meet weekly with K–5 standards written for at least 50 minutes of instruction per week; by Grade 5, students should understand the art form’s history, create original work, analyze quality, explain preferences, and connect the art form to cultures and events. Middle school classes usually meet daily; Grades 6–8 standards assume 90 minutes per week and require teachers to scaffold based on prior experience while ensuring all students meet Grade 8 expectations across all processes. High school courses span multiple proficiency levels; students should have access to 4–8 blocks of arts study and advance through levels while teachers differentiate for mixed-level classes (“stacking”).
ARTS EDUCATION IN NC POLICY
Chapter 115C requires instruction in arts as part of a sound basic education. ESSA recognizes arts as part of a Well-Rounded Education and allows Title IV, Part A funds to support arts access. Session Law SL2020-78 requires one standards-aligned arts credit for graduation. The Arts Proficiency Endorsement (Grad-007) requires four arts courses, a minimum unweighted GPA of 3.0 in each arts course, and 40 hours of arts-related extracurricular activities.
