# Arts Education - Sixth Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Sixth Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The 6-8 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

6.CN.1.1 Analyze the effect of geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts.
Glossary
* Analyze - Identifying and examining separate parts as they function independently and together in creative works and study of [the] arts.22
* Develop(ment) - To (cause something to) grow or change into a more advanced, larger, or stronger form; to invent something or bring something into existence.4
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States. At the current level, students analyze the effect of geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts. In the next level, students will analyze how individuals and groups of artists have influenced various cultures.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How the geographic location and physical environment shapes perspectives in a given time, place, or period
* How the geographic location and physical environment serve as sources of inspiration for artistic expression
* The impact of the geographic location and physical environment on the medium used in artistic expression
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Identifying and examining how the geographic location and physical environment impacts the development of visual arts
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Understanding how art materials are directly tied to geographical features (i.e. clays from different parts of the world, wooden masks, woodblock prints)
* Artists create with local materials accessible to them
* Geographic locations includes the regional culture as well as environmental factors
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to analyze the effect of both the geographic location AND the physical environment on the development of visual arts
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give examples of how the geographical location and physical environment has impact on visual art
* Guide student investigation in how various geographical locations and physical environments impact the tools, materials, subjects, and themes in visual art
* Provide learning activities in which students can analyze the effect of the geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to analyze the effect of geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Africa
* Asia
* Europe
* The Americas
* Australia
* Urban art in urban areas

Knowledge

* The artistic technical skills, characteristics, and behaviors necessary for success in art careers
Skills

* Recognizing, listing, or naming the skills, characteristics and behaviors that contribute to success in arts careers
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* This objective teaches students of the value and importance of the transferable skills they learn in the arts
* Arts education and experiences teach individuals more than just artistic technique. Behaviors, attitudes, character traits, cognitive processes, and creative design and presentation skills are equally developed
* Skills are inclusive of the psychomotor, affective, and cognitive skills developed in the making, presenting, and responding to art
* Use Portrait of a Graduate Durable Skills and other durable skills such as creativity, patience, determination, self-regulation, etc.
* Make sure students understand art careers extend beyond the roles of fine artists
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective. Students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to identify the art skills, characteristics, AND behaviors needed for success in art careers. 6.CR.1.1 Use materials in both traditional and non-traditional ways to create art.
Glossary

* The creative design process
* The elements of Art
* The principles of design

Evidence of Mastery

Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights

* Understand and use a variety of traditional and non traditional methods in creating art to express artistic ideas. Allow students to follow along in demonstrations when using a new technique or approach and support experimentation
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective- students should be able to show mastery of their ability to use materials in both traditional AND non-traditional ways to create art. 6.CR.2.2 Describe how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affect the ability of artists to create original or derivative art.
Glossary
* Artists - A person who creates art (such as painting, sculpture, music, or writing) using conscious skill and creative imagination; a person skilled in any of the arts.14
* Copyright - Form of protection grounded in the U.S. Constitution and granted by law for original works of authorship fixed in a tangible medium of expression, covering both published and unpublished works.17
* Creative Commons - Copyright license templates that provide a simple, standardized way to give the public permission to share and use creative work on conditions of the maker’s choice.6
* Derivative - Having parts that originate from another source.14
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Fair Use - Limitation in copyright law which sets out factors to be considered in determining whether or not a particular use of one’s work is “fair,” such as the purpose and character of the use, the amount of the work used, and whether the use will affect the market for the work.17
* Open Source - Computer software for which the copyright holder freely provides the right to use, study, change, and distribute the software to anyone for any purpose.26
* Original Art - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14
* Public Domain - A work of authorship is in the “public domain” if it is no longer under copyright protection or if it failed to meet the requirements for copyright protection. Works in the public domain may be used freely without the permission of the former copyright owner.32
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students cited artists correctly when utilizing or adapting their original artwork and now describe how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affect the ability of artists to create original or derivative art. In the next level, students will identify the legal and ethical issues for creating derivative art.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Definitions for fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and Creative Commons
* The limits of using material covered by these terms in the making of original or derivative art
* The protections these terms provide artists in the ownership of their artistic work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the effects fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and Creative Commons have on Visual Artists in the creation of original or derivative art
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “What laws protect and guide my ability to make art?” to show mastery of this objective
* Knowing the nuanced differences in these terms develops greater understanding of the responsible, legal, and ethical issues when creating art
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students combine ideas from multiple artists to create a new work of art. Before creating the artistic work, students would first need to understand how the principles of fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons support or restrict access to using other’s artistic ideas
* Note the conjunctions “and” “or” used in this objective. The student should be able to show mastery of their ability to describe how each: fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, AND creative commons affect the ability of artists. However, whether the student is describing how each affects the creation of original OR derivative art is dependent on the learning activity and can be decided by the teacher. Autonomy can also given to the student to decide how they want to demonstrate their mastery of the objective
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Create student friendly definitions of the terms
* Provide examples that demonstrate how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affects creation of original or derivative works
* Guide classroom discussion on how copyright, public domain, creative commons, fair use, and open source impacts the work of artists
* Guide student learning in how to describe how the creation of original or derivative works can be affected by fair use, creative commons, open source, copyright, and public domain
* Provide learning activities in which the student can describe how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affect an artist’s ability to create original or derivative works.
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affect the ability of artists to create original or derivative art
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Scenarios
* Creating a “Use guide”
* Social Contract
* Stealing or sharing? Game
* Current Events
   * Jeff Koons v. Rogers

6.PR.1.1 Improve the use of elements of art and principles of design using feedback based on given criteria.
Glossary
* Criteria - The rules or guidelines used for categorizing or judging; in arts assessment, the rules or guidelines used to judge the quality of a student’s performance.28
* Elements of Art - The “visual tools” artists use to create works of art. These include form, shape, line, texture, color, space, and value.2
* Feedback - A reaction or response to a particular design problem or design solution.29
* Given - Qualities or traits for assessing achievement level that are provided to students.17
* Improve - to enhance in value or quality : make better.14
* Principles of Design - The way the elements of art such as balance, movement, pattern/repetition, unity/variety and emphasis are used to create a composition.22
* Use - To put into action or service.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students applied changes to personal art based on self and peer-evaluation. At the current level, students improve the use of elements of art and principles of design using feedback based on given criteria. In the next level, students will revise artwork by applying instructor or peer feedback.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that visual artists improve by incorporating feedback into their artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The elements of art
* The principles of design
* How to use feedback from given criteria on the elements of art and principles of design to improve personal art
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Enhancing elements of art and principles of design in a work of art based on evaluatory feedback from given criteria
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* This objective is about APPLYING feedback and should be paired with the RE.2.2 objective throughline about GIVING feedback.
* This objective can be paired with CR & RE objectives as students create original artwork, present work created to peers or teachers, learn how to self-evaluate their work and give and receive constructive feedback, and then make revision of the work
* This objective does not clearly specify WHO gave the feedback (teacher, peer, self) so the given criteria can be used by either teacher, student, or peer to evaluate the use of the elements and principles in student work. The RE.2.2 objective has the student DEVELOPING relevant criteria so it could be suggested that the student is self-evaluating their work using their rubric of relevant criteria to evaluate the use of elements and principles and then demonstrating how they are using that feedback to make revision to their work
* Students may be able to identify areas for improvement in self-evaluation but might not always know how to make improvements
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to use feedback based on given criteria to improve the use of elements of art AND principles of design in a work
* Creating art is an iterative process in which feedback from others and self is essential for growth and improvement
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide learning activities to support feedback from evaluation of student artwork (RE objective)
* Guide students through the creative design process and their improvement of work based on feedback
* Provide an opportunity for students to show how feedback from given criteria was used to improve the use of elements and principles of design in their work
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to improve the use of elements of art and principles of design using feedback based on given criteria
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Use RE OBJECTIVE TO PROVIDE FEEDBACK FIRST using visual feedback charts and visual rubrics. After evaluation and feedback, students are given time to make improvements on their work
* Take photos of before and after improvement student work
* Students can identify improvements they made in their use of the elements of art and principles of design based on the feedback from given criteria they received

6.PR.1.2 Create an artist statement describing elements of art and principles of design used in personal art.
Glossary
* Artist Statement - Information about context, explanations of process, descriptions of learning, related stories, reflections, or other details in a written or spoken format shared by the artist to extend and deepen understanding of his or her artwork; an artist statement can be didactic, descriptive, or reflective in nature.17
* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Elements of Art - The “visual tools” artists use to create works of art. These include form, shape, line, texture, color, space, and value.2
* Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences.17
* Principles of Design - The way the elements of art such as balance, movement, pattern/repetition, unity/variety and emphasis are used to create a composition.22
* Use - To put into action or service.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students created an artist statement using given criteria and now specifically describe elements of art and principles of design used in personal art in an artist statement. In the next level, students will create an artist statement considering multiple perspectives.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that visual artists communicate their artistic knowledge through artist statements.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to discuss the creative design process in organized form
* Vocabulary for tools, media, and methods used
* The format or structure for an artist statement
* How to articulate the ways the elements of art and principles of design were used in the creation of a work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Conceiving a description of the use of the elements of art and principals of design in the making of and meaning of personal art
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to linguistically describe their artwork and the creative design process used
* This objective is written to be paired with CR objectives as it is asking students to discuss aspects about their creative work. It can also be partnered with RE objectives in analyzing and evaluating their work
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to create an artist statement describing elements of art AND principles of design used in personal art.. These two things can be assessed simultaneously in the description of the creation of one artistic work or the student can show mastery of each one separately in the description of making of different works over the course of study
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give students the opportunity to create personal art using the elements and principles (CR objective)
* Provide examples of artist statements that discuss the elements and principles and the works being described
* Ask students to identify how the artist described the elements and principles used in the exemplar
* Work with students to design an outline for their artist statements
* Generate sentence stems if helpful or necessary
* Provide guidance of their writing
* Provide a rubric for them to use as a guide in developing their work
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to create an artist statement describing elements of art and principles of design used in personal art
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Scaffold the activity by using the criteria to write a collaborative artist statement with a project all students commonly created then have the students select one piece of art they have created to use for their personal artist statement
* Provide an outline, sentence stems, and word bank if appropriate and necessary
* The students can submit a written artist statement or record themselves using an audio/video blog

6.PR.2.1 Identify personal artwork that meets multiple criteria.
Glossary
* Criteria - The rules or guidelines used for categorizing or judging; in arts assessment, the rules or guidelines used to judge the quality of a student’s performance.28
* Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
* Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students selected personal artwork for a portfolio that meets given criteria and now identify personal artwork that meets multiple criteria. In the next level, students will organize personal artwork that illustrates a variety of skills.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that visual artists intentionally curate and prepare visual artworks for audiences.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to identify personal artwork that satisfies multiple criteria
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming personal artwork that satisfies multiple criteria
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* This objective is written to be paired with CR objectives as it asks students to identify personal artwork. It can also be partnered with RE objectives in analyzing and evaluating their work

* The objective does not mention who determines the criteria therefore, it can be chosen by the teacher or developed in classroom collaboration with the students, by peers, or designed by the student

* Guide students in the identification of personal work that meets multiple criteria
* Create formative and summative assessments of the student’s ability to identify personal artwork that meets multiple criteria
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Using the elements of art and principles of design in a rubric for identifying personal art that meets multiple criteria
* Criteria could also include identifying work that demonstrates specific techniques or use of media
* Criteria could also include identifying personal artwork that the student is most proud of, that best represents them, or that they worked the hardest to develop, etc.
