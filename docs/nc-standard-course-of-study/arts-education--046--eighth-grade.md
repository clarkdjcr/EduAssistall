# Arts Education - Eighth Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Theatre Arts Standards Unpacking Eighth Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Theatre Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 theatre programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

8.CN.1.1 Identify the influence of theatre in North Carolina and the nation on history, culture, and community.
Glossary
* Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Community - A unified body of individuals: such as a group of people with a common characteristic or interest living together within a larger society; a body of persons of common and especially professional interests scattered through a larger society.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students analyzed how theatre influenced various developing cultures around the world from the 1400s to the present and now they are identifying the influence of theatre in North Carolina and the nation on history, culture, and community. In the next level they will analyze the development of theatre from the 1400s through the 1700s, identifying connections to history, society, and culture.
Enduring Understanding
A theatre artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of stories, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Examples of NC theatre that has influenced national history
* Examples of NC theatre that has influenced national culture
* Examples of NC theatre that has influenced national community
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming the influence of theatre on history, culture, and community
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
* Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Growth and influences of theatre in NC and the United States.
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts.
* Since this objective has the language “NC and the nation” both must be considered in the contexts of “history, culture, and community” where all three must be considered.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare examples of influential theatre in North Carolina and the broader U.S., such as The Lost Colony, Unto These Hills, and Broadway works that shaped public discourse.
* Demonstrate how to identify and describe the impact of a specific theatrical work on a community’s historical events, cultural identity, or civic engagement.
* Guide students in categorizing theatre examples by their influence on history, culture, and community in both NC and national contexts.
* Provide tools like anchor charts, example responses, and checklists to support students in naming and describing theatre’s role in shaping society.
* Monitor students’ ability to accurately identify and describe relevant examples using appropriate academic and theatre-specific vocabulary through formative and summative assessment, offering feedback as needed.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
* Community Theatre
* Children’s Theatre
* NC Outdoor Drama  EM
* Broadway
* Off Broadway, etc.
* Regional Theaters
* Pop culture and media
* Commercialization

8.CR.1.1 Improvise conflict and resolution scenarios for original scripts or devised theatre.
Glossary
* Devised theatre - Creation of an original performance piece by an ensemble.17
* Improvise - To make, invent, or arrange offhand; to make or fabricate out of what is conveniently on hand.14
* Original - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students improvised characters and situations for developing original scripts or devised theatre. Now they are improvising conflict and resolution scenarios for original scripts or devised theatre. In the next level, they will develop a variety of unique characters within given situations through improvisation.
Enduring Understanding
Exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of theatre artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Components of conflict and resolution scenarios
* Devised theatre is group creation of original performance pieces
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Spontaneously inventing conflict and resolution scenarios
Evidence of Mastery
* Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Character motivations may collide to create conflict.
* Characters use various tactics that lead to resolving conflict.
* Conflict involves a variety of internal and external forces.
* There are differences between scripting and devising theatre.
* There are guidelines for the art of improvisation
* Since this objective has the language “conflict and resolution scenarios” both must be considered to master this objective.
* Since this objective has the language “original scripts or devised theatre” either one may be completed. Students are not required to do both to master this objective.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

* Conflict in motivations and alternative resolutions.

8.CR.1.3 Create simple lighting and sound designs to support and enhance dramatic presentations.
Glossary
   * Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
   * Dramatic Presentations - Referring to the production of a play or dramatic work, or the specific acting/technical job of a production member in a play.29
   * Lighting - The illumination of the performance dictated by the given circumstances of the play, including the source of light, the time of day, the mood of the play, as well as the specific requirements of the scenic design (for example, the areas to be lit).29
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students created a variety of simple props and set pieces to support and enhance dramatic presentations. Now they are creating simple lighting and sound designs to support and enhance dramatic presentations. In the next level, they will implement original technical designs into theatrical works using available facilities, technology, and tools.
Enduring Understanding
Unique ideas are actualized by theatre designers and craftspersons intentionally to fulfill dramatic purposes.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * Components of simple lighting designs
   * Components of simple sound designs
   * Considerations for supporting and enhancing dramatic presentations
   * Lighting instantly transforms backgrounds, mood changes, actions, and locations.
   * Sound affects mood, action, and location in theatrical work.
   * Principles of lighting and sound affect actors, audiences, costumes, and makeup
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Creating new lighting and sound designs
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Lighting and sound effects are used not only as simple  illumination and volume enhancement onstage, but also to help create an environment and mood for the audience to suspend belief and invest in the story being performed.
   * When creating lighting and sound effects, it is important to stay true to the director's concept.
   * Students do not need to execute their designs to master this objective, but only to create designs for lighting and/or sound
   * This objective will be most meaningful when supporting improvisations (CR.1.1), original creation (CR.1.2), or as part of presenting works (PR.1.2). This objective has students creating the technical elements used in PR.2.3
   * Since this objective has the language “lighting and sound” both design types must be created to master this objective
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare materials for simple lighting and sound effects (e.g., flashlights, colored gels, lamps, portable speakers, sound clips, recording apps). Share pictures or videos of simple lighting and sound effects used in theater or familiar stories and discuss how these elements support storytelling
   * Demonstrate how to design simple lighting and sound effects to create environment and mood, highlighting creative freedom and imaginative choices. Simple lighting and sound effects may be created as separate projects depending on student focus
   * Guide students in designing and building their own simple lighting and sound effects
   * Provide key aspects of the play that inform design choices such as setting, time period, mood, theme, character traits, and plot details
   * Monitor individual students’ ability to design simple lighting and sound effects through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Historical points of view on the use of lighting and sound in props and sets.  (How would outdoor lighting, candelabras or gas light give a different mood to the production?)
   * Tools and safety
   * Applying science of color mixing and sound waves to the creation of lighting and sound production
   * The psychology of color
   * Exploration of creating sound live or pre-recorded
   * Adding background music or ambient sound to create atmosphere
   * Lighting and sound for CR.1.1, CR.1.2, or CR.2.1
   * Lighting and sound for PR.1.2, PR.2.1, or PR.2.3

Theatre artists balance inspiration and originality with ethics by creatively transforming or adapting other artistic ideas into new works.
Knowledge

   * Historical and/or current events
   * Procedures for using research to develop characters and situations

Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

   * Factual accuracy can strengthen the believability of fictional stories
   * Since this objective has the language “historical or current” students may adapt either kind of event to master this objective
   * Since this objective has the language “background knowledge and research” students must engage both to master this objective
   * Since this objective has the language “character and situations” students must engage both to master this objective
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

   * Provide resources, graphic organizers, and research tools to help students gather and organize information that will inform their adaptations
   * Monitor individual students’ ability to adapt historical or current events applying background knowledge and research to develop character and situations through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities

      * Environment - Physical surroundings that establish place, time, and atmosphere/mood. 21
      * Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences. 17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students compared personal preferences in theatrical works as it relates to social or cultural background. Now they are explaining the impact of culture and environment on personal perspective. In the next level, they will explain how personal preferences impact the ability to view theatrical works objectively.
Enduring Understanding
An observer's personal experiences shape the way they perceive and engage with theatre works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
      * Vocabulary to critique theatrical works.
      * Difference between cultural and environmental influences
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
      * Describing the reasoning individuals, communities, and/or cultures have when they value some theatrical works over others
      * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
      * Art is subjective. Personal background and experience can influence the interpretation of a story and performance, but it does not have to color our overall experience
      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
      * Since this objective has the language “cultural and environmental” both must be considered
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
      * Prepare theatrical works that reflect different cultures and environments and offer examples of how audiences may respond differently depending on context
      * Demonstrate how to explain personal perspective using cultural reference points, such as “I grew up hearing stories like this, so I understood it differently” or “This setting reminds me of where I’m from”
      * Guide students in reflecting on how their environment, traditions, or background influence their preferences and understanding of performances
      * Provide graphic organizers, sentence stems, and open discussion protocols to help students articulate how personal context shapes interpretation
      * Guide students to develop skills and vocabulary to critique theatrical works.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
      * Definitions of cultural vs environment
      * Definitions of subjective vs objective
      * What is art/drama?  What is the function of art/drama in society?  How does art/drama reflect culture and environment?

8.RE.2.2 Evaluate theatrical work of others with a specific plan for improvement.
Glossary
      * Evaluate - A judgment about the worth or quality of something. 28
      * Works - Something produced by the exercise of creative talent or expenditure of creative effort: artistic production.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students evaluated choices made in devised or scripted performance, based on objective criteria. Now they are evaluating the theatrical work of others with a specific plan for improvement. In the next level, they will generate objective critique of theatrical works using given criteria.
Enduring Understanding
Providing feedback through objective critique is imperative in improving artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
      * Criteria for evaluation
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
      * Assessing the quality of others' theatrical work
      * Creating a specific plan for improving others' theatrical work
      * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
      * Artists can be proud of their work and still want to improve it
      * "The only wrong choice is no choice" encourages action decision-making and risk taking
      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
      * Prepare frameworks that help students move from observation to constructive suggestion
      * Demonstrate using observation notes to craft a thoughtful improvement plan
      * Guide students in using critique to support revision of peer work in small groups or stations
      * Provide models and checklists to support actionable and respectful feedback delivery
      * Monitor individual students' ability to evaluate theatrical work of others with a specific plan for improvement through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
      * Definitions of Subjective versus Objective
      * Definition of refinement, tightening, and bold choices
      * The difference between the performer's choice and the director's vision
      * Constructive criticism highlighting successes and areas needing improvement can lead to growth in performance
