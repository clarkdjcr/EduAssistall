# Arts Education - First Grade

Parent Guide for First Grade General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore musical connections to culture, values, and ideas, especially traditions, celebrations, and entertainment, along with cross-curricular connections of music.
CN.2 Explore - Students name the roles of people who listen to and distribute music. They also examine how innovations have impacted music.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise both rhythms and 3-pitch melodies, and learn to notate their rhythmic ideas.
CR.2 Adapt - Students create musical accompaniments to existing stories and examine how to give credit to other artists when borrowing their existing ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn to use feedback to improve their performance of a variety of unison songs, honing their ability to demonstrate a variety of rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing, interpreting Italian terminology.
PR.2 Develop - Students examine what tools are needed to put on a musical performance, as well as comparing audience and performer etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify contrasting forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, emotions communicated, and timbres of voices in a variety of music using Italian terminology.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine what they prefer in music before giving constructive feedback to others.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to sing or tell you about the songs they are learning at school
* Ask students to identify a musical element in the music they listen to at home
* Talk to students about why we should give credit to the ideas of others
* Discuss the music your family likes
* Point out the various times your family uses music in your daily lives: during traditions, celebrations, entertainment, or other uses
