# Arts Education - First Grade

Parent Guide for First Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students compare theatre types and identify how they use theatre skills in other arts subjects.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn the roles and tools of actors, directors, and designers.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise during dramatic play, and create original sequences of events, costumes, and props.
CR.2 Adapt - Students sequence events on familiar stories and examine how to give credit to other artists when borrowing their existing ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use feedback from their director and learn to pantomime stories.
PR.2 Develop - Students explore characters and settings, practice appropriate audience behavior, adapt available resources for costumes and props, and examine how a performance space affects movement and vocal choices.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify character relationships and how artistic choices communicate meaning.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine what they prefer in theatre before giving positive feedback to themselves and others about their artistic choices.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing what they liked
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the role of the director, actor, and designers (costume, set, lighting, sound)
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.), especially in relation to other audience members
* Encourage your student to tell stories and create costumes and props for their stories at home out of craft supplies or blankets.
* Talk to students about why we should give credit to the ideas of others
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
