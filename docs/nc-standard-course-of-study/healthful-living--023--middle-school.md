# Healthful Living - Middle School

North Carolina Standard Course of Study
Physical Education K-12

MS = Motor Skills, MC = Movement Concepts, HF = Health-Related Fitness, PR = Personal/Social Responsibility

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Physical Education.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.MC.2.1 Apply concepts and principles related to movement and performance in static or dynamic settings.
Verb Defined:
To put to use especially for some practical purpose : to bring into action
Student will know and be able to:
* Static Stretches
   * Wall sits
   * Planks
   * Lunge
   * Yoga Poses
   * Tricep Stretch
   * Bicep Stretch
   * Quadricep Stretch
   * Hamstring Stretch
   * Hurdle Stretch
Grade 6:
* Move without equipment
* Move into open space
* Move in varying pathways
* Move at various speeds
* Move away from the object
* Create space using pivots and fakes or give and go
* Throw to a stationary target

Grade 7:
* Move changing pace and directions
* Reduce open space with locomotor movements
* Receive from a moving passer
* Throw to a moving target

Grade 8:
* Reduce the space in open pathways
* Reduce spacing by not allowing a catch
* Throw to a moving target with defenders
* Apply pass patterns with and without defenders

Skill checklist for identified concept or movement principles to evaluate increased performance in static or dynamic settings.

PE.6-8.MC.2.2 Understand and apply game strategies and tactics at appropriate times and in appropriate ways.

to grasp the meaning of

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.MC.2.3 Demonstrate understanding of the proper form and the mechanics of various skills or sequences of movement to improve performance.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Select the correct mechanics needed to perform the skill in practice or gameplay situations.

* Forearm pass vs. overhead pass or drive in volleyball
* Fingertips up vs. fingertips down when fielding softball/baseball, catching football
* Appropriate use of backhand, overhand, underhand, hammer throw in Frisbee games
* Appropriate use of bounce pass, chest pass, and overhead pass in basketball
* Appropriate use of forearm, backhand, drive, drop in badminton, pickleball, and tennis
* Appropriate use of close dribble loose dribble acceleration, deceleration in soccer
* Appropriate use of inside of foot, outside of foot and instep to control and pass the ball in soccer
Grade 6:
Students will create open space to perform correct mechanics for specified skill.

Grade 7:
Students will use body control and spacing to use correct mechanics for specified skill (knowing location of opponent).

Grade 8:
Students will perform specified skill with accuracy during practice and game play while maintaining control, spacing, correct mechanics, and knowing the location of the opponent.
Assessment Example:
Skill checklist or rubric to evaluate key components or skills identified by the teacher.

6 – 8 Grade Span Health-Related Fitness (HF)
Essential Standard:
By the end of grade 8
6-8.HF.3 Demonstrate knowledge and skills needed to achieve a health-enhancing level of physical activity and fitness.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.HF.3.1 Describe the FITT principle and training principles (frequency, intensity, time, type, overload, progression, specificity), and how they affect physical fitness for each of the five health-related fitness components.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:

Grade 6:
Identify the components of the FITT formula for different types of physical activity.

Grade 7:
Describe the components of the FITT formula for different types of physical activity.

Grade 8:
Describe how the FITT principle affects each of the five health-related fitness components.
Assessment Example:
Students will describe both the elements of the FITT formula and training principles with an emphasis on how they affect fitness in each of the five health related components during open discussion, bell ringer, exit ticket, Q&A, or quiz.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.HF.3.2 Summarize the potential short-and long-term physical, social, and emotional benefits of physical activity.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Grade 6:
* Summarize the components of physical activity that provide opportunities for reducing stress.
* Summarize how being physically active leads to a healthy body.

Grade 7:
* Summarize different types of physical activities describing how each exerts a positive effect on health.
* Summarize the positive mental and emotional aspects of participating in physical activity.

Grade 8:
* Summarize the social benefits of physical activity.
* Summarize the connection between fitness and overall physical and mental health.
Assessment Example:
Students summarize the potential short-and long-term physical, social, and emotional benefits of physical activity
during open discussion, bell ringer, exit ticket, Q&A, or quiz.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.HF.3.3 Evaluate progress towards achieving physical activity and fitness guidelines.
Verb Defined:
To determine the significance, worth, or condition of usually by careful appraisal and study

Student will know and be able to:
Grade 6:
* Evaluate self-selected physical activity goals for aerobic activity and muscle/bone strengthening.
* Evaluate opportunities in the school and community for regular participation in physical activity.
* Evaluate a program designed and implemented by the student for remediation of an area of weakness.

Grade 7:
* Evaluate barriers for maintaining a physically active lifestyle and seek solutions to eliminate them.
* Evaluate physical activity adjustments based on the quantity needed for a minimum health standard.
* Evaluate a program designed and implemented by the student for remediation of two areas of weakness.

Grade 8:
* Evaluate available technology needed to monitor the quantity of exercise needed for a minimum health standard.
* Evaluate a program designed and implemented by the student to improve levels of health related fitness and nutrition.
* Evaluate a program designed and implemented by the student for remediation of three areas of weakness.
Assessment Example:
Students maintain a physical activity log for minimum of 2 weeks to reflect and evaluate progress made towards achieving physical activity and fitness guidelines.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.HF.3.4 Participate in a variety of moderate to vigorous activities with the goal of accumulating at least 60 minutes of physical activity per day, either during or outside of school, at least 5 days per week.
Verb Defined:
To take part : to have a part or share in something
Student will know and be able to:
Grade 6:
* Participates in moderate to vigorous physical activity 3 times a week outside of school.
* Participates in a variety of aerobic fitness activities in a physical activity setting at school.

Grade 7:
* Participates in moderate to vigorous physical activity 4 times a week outside of school.
* Participates in a variety of strength and endurance activities, such as weight and resistance training in a physical activity setting at school.

Grade 8:
* Participates in moderate to vigorous physical activity 5 times a week outside of school.
* Participates in a variety of self-selected aerobic activities, such as walking, jogging, biking, swimming, or skating in a physical activity setting at school.
Assessment Example:
Students record a fitness/activity log to determine the amount of activity they engage in inside/outside of school.

6 – 8 Grade Span Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
Essential Standard:
By the end of grade 8
6-8.PR.4 Demonstrate responsible personal and social behavior that respects self and others.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.PR.4.1 Demonstrate self-management, responsible decision making, and problem-solving skills in a physical activity setting.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
  Student will know and be able to:
Demonstrate appropriate behavior while maintaining self-management, using appropriate decision making, and problem-solving skills within a physical activity setting.

* Self-Management
   * adhere to deadlines
   * understand and get to know others in the physical activity setting
   * analyze independent behaviors from the class
   * checklist of behaviors assessment
   * students’ response to teacher and peer officials with positive feedback and good sportsmanship
   * positive attitude
   * sportsmanship
* Decision Making
   * participate in establishing rules, procedures, and etiquette that are safe and effective depending on the activity
   * analyze appropriate techniques and choices made
   * analyze various situations to determine appropriate response to best demonstrate good sportsmanship
   * etiquette
   * fair play
   * teammate and opponent support whether win or lose during group play
* Problem-Solving
   * officiating techniques
   * students officiate games and activities
   * students make appropriate calls during game play
   * conflict resolution skills
Grade 6:
* Demonstrate effective communication and creative thinking skills to solve problems, make decisions and resolve conflict with others, and promote safe participation in physical activities.
* Exhibits personal responsibility by using appropriate etiquette, demonstrating respect for facilities and exhibiting safe behaviors.
Grade 7:
* Exhibits knowledge of procedures, rules, and appropriate etiquette in physical activity settings by self-officiating modified physical activities/games.
* Demonstrates self-officiating on the honor system, rather than by an outside observer such as a referee or teacher. (Consult with the teacher as needed for additional guidance.)

Grade 8:
* Demonstrate methods of fairplay, officiating, and problem-solving for more productive self-management and decision making skills.
* Demonstrates knowledge of proper etiquette, respect for others, integrity, effective communication, problem-solving skills, conflict-resolution skills, self-management and teamwork skills while engaging in cooperative and dynamic physical activity
Assessment Example:
An observational checklist to evaluate key components of self-management, responsible decision making, and problem-solving skills to provide positive/corrective feedback through teacher or peers.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.PR.4.2 Demonstrate skills to work cooperatively within a group to establish and achieve group goals in physical activity settings.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Display the roles and responsibilities necessary for group success by comparing their behavior with those roles and responsibilities to ensure that they are productive members of the group.
* Group Cooperation
   * achieve group goals in competitive settings (small-sided/modified games)
   * achieve group goals in cooperative settings
   * demonstrate positive behaviors that contribute to group success
   * work to modify rules/strategies/tactics of activities to include every participant
      * student groups to develop a video or role play to…
         * show a rule
         * official’s signal for rule violation
         * result of a violation
   * analyze potential solutions
   * accept decisions made by officials (students/teachers)
   * positive peer interaction and encouragement
   * each member of a group has an important role to play. If a member neglects his or her responsibility, then the group’s performance will suffer.
Grade 6:
Teacher selected groups. Have teams develop ownership like in Sport Ed. Model.
* Demonstrate agreement on a common goal when participating in a cooperative physical activity.
* Demonstrate integrity and apply rules/etiquette for a team-building activity.
* Demonstrate cooperation with a small group of classmates during adventure activities, game play or team-building activities.
* Demonstrate collaboration on common goals and agree on possible solutions to a variety of physical challenges.

Grade 7:
Teacher selected or group division. Teams need buy-in. Ex. Sport Ed. Model.
* Display problem-solving with a small group of classmates during adventure activities, small-group initiatives or game play.
* Exhibit responsible social behaviors by cooperating with classmates.
* Demonstrate cooperation skills by establishing rules and guidelines for resolving conflicts.

Grade 8:
Teacher selected or group division. Teams need buy-in, understanding, and a why. Ex. Sport Ed. Model.
* Demonstrate the role and responsibilities of a leader.
* Demonstrate appropriate encouragement and feedback to peers without prompting from the teacher.
* Demonstrate social and safety skills to achieve group goals in physical activity settings.
Assessment Example:
An observational checklist created to evaluate key components of the skills to provide positive/corrective feedback through teacher or peers.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.6-8.PR.4.3 Apply relationship skills and strategies that promote team/group dynamics and inclusion.
Verb Defined:
To put to use especially for some practical purpose : to bring into action

Model support toward individuals of all ability levels and encourage others to be supportive and inclusive of all individuals.

   * group challenges

   * competitive games/activities
   * cooperative games/activities

   * ropes course

   * assigns roles and responsibilities

Incorporate student accountability and cooperation.

* Demonstrate integrity in physical activity settings to allow all students to feel safe, included, and treated fairly.

Grade 7:
Increase accountability of cooperation amongst students.
* Implement opportunities that allow everyone to participate and succeed.
* Engage in supportive behaviors that may include listening, helping, encouraging, ensuring everyone is included, taking turns, following rules, and modifying rules as needed.

Grade 8:
Increase accountability and level of challenges amongst students.
* Utilize appropriate encouragement and feedback such as positive specific comments about what a peer is doing well, specific comments that may help a peer improve skill/play; and effective verbal and nonverbal communication skills.

Students create an environment that supports all participants.

Grade 7:

* Use effective communication skills to solve problems and resolve conflicts that arise.

Grade 8:

* Implement appropriate encouragement and feedback to peers without prompting from the teacher.
Assessment Example:
Students demonstrate appropriate resolution skills when conflict arises.
