# Healthful Living - 4th Grade

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)

Essential Standard:
4.PCH.1 Apply personal behaviors and practices that promote health and prevent disease.
Clarifying Objective:
4.PCH.1.1 Compare methods that prevent the spread of germs.
Verb Defined:
To examine the character or qualities of especially in order to discover resemblances or differences
Student will know and be able to:
Understanding the science behind germs and their spread can help people take ownership for personal hygiene and help reduce the spread of germs. Germs are microorganisms like bacteria and viruses that can make people sick. They are spread through coughing, sneezing, touching contaminated surfaces, and contaminated food.

Common methods to prevent or reduce the spread of germs include:
* Handwashing vigorously with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds (be sure to get the fingernails)
* Using a hand sanitizer when soap and water are not available
* Covering coughs and sneezes with the elbow and or a tissue
* Disinfecting surfaces like doorknobs and desks
* Avoiding close contact with someone who is sick
* Staying at home when sick
* Proper food storage

Clarifying Objective:
4.PCH.1.2 Describe factors which lead to sufficient sleep and regular physical activity.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Sufficient, high-quality sleep and regular physical activity contribute to overall well-being. Understanding these factors and ultimately developing a personal plan to incorporate these into daily life are important healthy habits that lead to student health. Fourth graders (ages 6 to 12) should get 9 to 12 hours of sleep each night.
* Factors that lead to good sleep:
   * Consistent sleep schedule
   * Avoiding screens at least an hour before bedtime
   * Creating a cool, calm, and dark sleeping environment
   * Regular bedtime routine (brushing teeth, reading a book, etc.)
   * Avoid heavy meals before bed
   * Finish physical activity at least 2 hours before bedtime
* Factors that lead to regular physical activity:
   * Finding physical activities that are enjoyable
   * Understanding that there are multiple types of physical activity:
      * everyday movement
      * play
      * aerobic and anaerobic
      * strength
      * balance
   * Recommendation of at least 60 minutes of physical activity over the course of the day
   * Engaging in physical activity with friends to provide motivation

Verb Defined:
To produce through imaginative skill : to make or bring into existence something new
Student will know and be able to:
Preventing oral health issues like cavities and gum disease is beneficial for overall well-being. Creating a personal dental health plan can help maintain healthy teeth and gums as part of good oral health.

* Good dental health practices include:
   * Brushing teeth twice (2x) a day for 2 minutes using a fluoride toothpaste.
   * Flossing once (1x) a day to clean between teeth.
   * Visiting the dentist twice (2x) a year for a checkup and cleaning.
   * Eating foods that are good for the teeth, like fruits, vegetables, and calcium-rich foods (dairy), while limiting sugary drinks and snacks.

* Poor dental health can lead to oral health issues like cavities, gum disease, bad breath, and overall poor health.

Clarifying Objective:
4.PCH.2.2 Illustrate skills for providing first aid for choking victims.
Verb Defined:
To make clear by giving or by serving as an example or instance
Student will know and be able to:
Choking happens when something blocks the airway, making breathing difficult or impossible. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), choking accounts for approximately 3,000 deaths annually in the United States. Children under 5 and elderly adults are at the highest risk with food consumption the leading cause.

   Signs of choking
* Inability to Speak or Cough
* Clutching the Throat
* Gasping for Air or Wheezing
* Panic or Agitation
* Blue or Pale Skin (Cyanosis)
* Loss of Consciousness
* Inability to Breathe or Speak Clearly

   First aid for choking
1. Assess the Situation
   * Ask: "Are you choking?" If the person can cough, speak, or breathe, encourage them to keep coughing to try and expel the object.
   * If the person cannot cough, speak, or breathe, they need immediate help.
   * Encourage Coughing (if the person can cough)
   * If the person is still able to cough, encourage them to continue doing so. It may help dislodge the object.
2. Perform Back Blows
   * Position:
      * Stand behind the person. For adults and children over one-year-old, place your arms around their waist.
   * Technique:
      * Lean the person forward at the waist (this helps gravity dislodge the object). Deliver 5 sharp back blows between the shoulder blades using the heel of your hand.
      * Check if the object has been cleared after each blow.
3. Perform Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)
   * If back blows don’t work, or if the person is unconscious, perform abdominal thrusts:
   * For an Adult or Child Over One-Year-Old:
      * Stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist.
      * Place a fist just above their navel, with the thumb side against their abdomen.
      * Grasp the fist with your other hand and perform quick, inward and upward thrusts.
      * Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.
4. If the Person Becomes Unconscious
   * Call Emergency Services: Dial emergency services (911 or local number).
   * Perform CPR: If the person becomes unconscious and you cannot clear the airway, start CPR:
      * For adults:
         1. Perform chest compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
   * Check the Mouth:
      * After each set of compressions, check the mouth for the object. If you can see it, remove it, but do not blindly sweep the mouth, as you might push it deeper.
5. Recovery Position
   * Once the object is expelled and the person is breathing, keep them in the recovery position (on their side) and monitor their breathing until help arrives.

Important Notes:
* Never slap or hit an infant on the back—use back blows.
* If you're unsure, it's always better to act quickly and call for emergency help.

4.PCH.3 Understand body systems and organs, their functions, and their care.
Clarifying Objective:
4.PCH.3.1 Identify the basic components and functions of the respiratory system.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
The respiratory system is responsible for the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide. Oxygen is essential for life.

* Nose and Nasal Cavity
   * Function:
      * The primary entryway for air that filters, humidifies, and warms or cools the air as it enters.
* Pharynx (Throat)
   * Function:
      * A passageway for air, food, and liquids. It directs air into the larynx, while food and liquids are directed to the esophagus.
* Larynx (Voice Box)
   * Function:
      * It serves as a passageway for air and contains the vocal cords, which protect the trachea by preventing food from entering the airway.
* Trachea (Windpipe)
   * Function:
      * The tube that conducts air from the larynx to the bronchi.
* Bronchi and Bronchioles
   * Function:
      * The trachea divides into two bronchi, each leading to a lung. The bronchi branch into smaller bronchioles, which direct air deeper into the lungs.
* Alveoli
   * Function:
      * Tiny, sac-like structures where gas exchange occurs. Oxygen from the inhaled air diffuses into the blood, and carbon dioxide from the blood diffuses into the alveoli to be exhaled.
* Lungs
   * Function:
      * The lungs house the bronchi, bronchioles, and alveoli. They are responsible for the exchange of gases (oxygen and carbon dioxide) with the blood.
* Diaphragm and Intercostal Muscles
   * Function:
      * The diaphragm is the primary muscle involved in breathing. When it contracts, the chest cavity enlarges, drawing air into the lungs (inhalation). The intercostal muscles between the ribs assist with expanding and contracting the chest.

Examples:

* Tapping fingers interpreted as impatient or nervous
* Putting your heads in your hands interpreted as boredom or weariness

Essential Standard:
4.ICHR.2 Understand the changes that occur during puberty and adolescence.
Clarifying Objective:
4.ICHR.2.1 Summarize physical and emotional changes during puberty.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Puberty is a stage of development that includes the beginning of secondary sex characteristics and reproductive capacity.

For girls, the physical changes are the growth of pubic hair, the development of breast buds and then breasts, widening of hips, hair under arms and on legs. Within a few years of the beginning of puberty, girls' ovaries will cause the ova to mature (ripen) and be released. This makes her capable of conceiving a child. Beginning in puberty and for several decades, she will experience ovulation-menstruation (when her body prepares for conception and then releases menstrual blood if fertilization does not take place).

In boys, the shoulders widen, hair grows in the pubic area, and on the face, the voice deepens (gradually), the testes and penis become larger, and the testes begin to produce sperm. Boys’ bodies begin to “practice” for reproductive function by experiencing spontaneous erections and nocturnal emissions.

In both boys and girls, the sweat glands become more active. Hormones cause the oil glands to produce more oil, which may cause pimples. Growth spurts and growing pains occur as the long bones in the arms and legs grow faster than the rest of the body.

Both boys and girls will experience emotional changes. Their confidence may not be as high as it has been previously. Some emotions are linked to confusion about the changes in their bodies; other emotions are triggered by mood changes related to hormone production. Moods can change quickly from anger and sadness to excitement and joy.
Clarifying Objective:
4.ICHR.2.2 Recognize that individuals experience puberty at different ages and for different lengths of time (early, average, late).
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge formally : to acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Young people develop at different times and rates.

Girls usually begin puberty and complete those changes one to two years before boys. Girls may begin as young as eight and finish developing while still a teenager.

Boys are more likely to start at 10 to 12 and may not finish developing until they are in their early 20s. A young person may be referred to as early, average, or late maturing.

As young people move through the transitions of puberty and adolescence, it is important to remember that even though they start at different ages, they all end up in the same place. The late maturing youth “catch up” with the early maturing youth. All need reassurance that puberty and adolescence are survivable.

Verb Defined:

The USDA classify foods in 5 different food groups: fruits, vegetables, grains, protein foods, and dairy.

Foods are divided into different food groups because they provide different nutrients. The foods in each food group have similar nutrient content. When eating meals and snacks that contain multiple food groups, bodies get a variety of nutrients. The school and breakfast menus offer a variety of foods from all 5 food groups.

It is important not to label foods as “good” and “bad” and to acknowledge different families eat different foods.

For example, meats, eggs, nuts and beans all contain protein and are in the protein group.  Apples, bananas, oranges, and kiwi contain carbohydrates, fiber, and vitamins are all in the fruit group.

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and Other Drugs (ANCOD)

Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.1.1 Summarize the short-term and long-term effects of nicotine products.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Nicotine is a poisonous chemical that is found in tobacco products. It has multiple side effects that affect the user.

Short term effects
* Increased heart rate
* Increased blood pressure
* Stimulation of the body
* Heightened alertness
* Nausea
* Dizziness

Long term effects
* Addiction
* Damage to lung tissue
* Increase risk of heart disease
* Increase risk of cancer

Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.1.2 Explain why nicotine products are addictive.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Tobacco products contain nicotine, a drug that causes the brain to release dopamine from the brain to get the “feel good” feeling. Nicotine is released quickly into the bloodstream and delivered to the brain.

Products include:
* cigarettes
* cigars
* chewing tobacco
* dip
* vapes
* ENDS (electronic nicotine delivery systems)

Essential Standard:
4.ANCOD.2 Understand why people use nicotine products.
Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.2.1 Identify possible personal and societal influences on nicotine use.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or  unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Young people may try nicotine products out of curiosity, peer pressure, adult (or older sibling) modeling, or to look older or more sophisticated. Some youth experiment with behaviors out of rebellion. Tobacco companies appear to market directly to a youthful audience.

Societal influences on nicotine use:
* Accessibility and availability of nicotine products
* Cultural norms and social environment
* Media portrayal (including television, movie, music, and sports figures)
* Peer and sibling views and use of tobacco
* School academic achievement
* Involvement/participation in sports and activities
* Parental attitudes, opinions, and feelings

Personal influences on nicotine use
* Self-esteem and self-image levels
* Perception of nicotine use on one’s behavior and appearance
* Knowledge/beliefs/attitudes
* Level of self-efficacy in ability to refuse offers of tobacco

  Influences for avoiding nicotine
* Doing well in school
* Participating in structured, extra-curricular activities
* Parents setting and consistently enforcing realistic rules
* Non-use among family and peer

Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.2.2 Analyze the use of various marketing strategies to influence people to purchase various nicotine products.
Verb Defined:
To study or determine the nature and relationship of the parts of something
Student will know and be able to:
Despite restrictions on the marketing of nicotine products, children continue to be exposed to advertising and promotional activities.
Research studies have found that kids are more sensitive to nicotine advertising than adults, are more likely to be influenced by cigarette marketing than by peer pressure, and that a large portion of underage experimentation is attributable to advertising and promotion.

Marketing Strategy examples:
* Nicotine ads use positive-smoking images rather than information to portray the attractiveness and function of smoking
* Nicotine products are often placed close to candy displays and other products popular with kids.
* Marketing capitalizes on the disparity between an ideal and actual self-image
   * affecting their perceptions of the pervasiveness, image, and function of smoking
   * create the image that nicotine use smoking is attractive

Essential Standard:
4.ANCOD.3 Apply risk reduction behaviors to protect self and others from alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and other drug use.
Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.3.1 Identify refusal skills to resist the pressure to experiment with a variety of nicotine products.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Peer pressure is one of the main temptations to experiment with nicotine products, in spite of all the known adverse effects. Refusal skills should be taught and practiced.

Refusal Skills:
* Say “No, thanks” plainly and firmly.
* Walk away and leave the situation.
* Be a broken record.
* Give an excuse why you can’t.
* Suggest an alternative activity
* Make a joke.
* Use flattery/turn the tables.
* Give a reason why it’s a bad idea.
* Ignore the suggestion.
* The power of numbers. Sometimes “we” sounds stronger than “I”.

Clarifying Objective:
4.ANCOD.3.2 Select strategies to use in avoiding situations in which smoked and electronic nicotine products are being used to minimize exposure to secondhand smoke and aerosol.
Verb Defined:
To choose from a number or group : pick out
Student will know and be able to:
Strategy examples:
* When visiting another dwelling
   * go into another part of the house
   * go home
   * go outside

* While a passenger in a car
   * ask permission to roll down the window
   * ask the person not to use the product

* When at public events
   * distance yourself if possible

* When outside the entrance of a public building
   * use alternate entrance
   * hold your breath until you are away from the area

* As a family, establish rules to keep smokers outside and maintain a smoke-free home and car.

* Encourage family members to quit smoking.
