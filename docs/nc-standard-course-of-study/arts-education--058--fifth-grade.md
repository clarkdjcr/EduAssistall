# Arts Education - Fifth Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Fifth Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

5.CN.1.1 Describe how the visual arts has affected, and been affected by, the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States.
Glossary
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Customs - A usage or practice common to many or to a particular place or class or habitual with an individual; the whole body of usages, practices, or conventions that regulate social life.14
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Indigenous - Of or relating to the earliest known inhabitants of a place and especially of a place that was colonized by a now-dominant group.14
* Traditions - Pattern of practices and beliefs within a societal group.17
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in North Carolina and now broaden the scope of influence to the United States. In the next level, students analyze the effect of geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States
* Visual Artists who have influenced the customs, traditions or history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States
* Visual artists whose work was influenced by Indigenous and cultural groups in the United States
* Examples of visual artwork that reflects customs, traditions or history of indigenous and other cultural groups from the United States
* How the customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States may have influenced or been influenced by the field of visual arts
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of the ways in which the visual arts has affected, and been affected by, the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Expose students to the art found in, and influenced by, diverse cultures in the United States
* Visual artists are influenced by customs, traditions, and their cultures
* Note the conjunction “and” used three times in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how the visual arts has affected, AND been affected by, the customs, traditions, AND history of indigenous AND other cultural groups in the United States. Given the scope, this objective will likely be broken apart into multiple learning activities over the course of study but ultimately the student must master the entirety of the content included in the objective
* This objective can be paired with CR.1.1 as students create personal artwork and RE objectives
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give examples of how the customs, traditions, and history of Indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States have influenced, or been influenced by visual arts
* Expose students to the art found in, and influenced by, diverse cultures in the United States
* Facilitate student discussions describing artwork found in, and influenced by various cultures in the United States
* Provide opportunity for students to describe how the arts have influenced and been influenced by Indigenous and other cultures in the United States
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups in the United States
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Harlem Renaissance Art
* Comparison of art from different Indigenous tribes in the United States
* Folk Art
* Written assignments
* Illustrations (comic strips or visual presentations)
* Use of graphic organizers

Evidence of Mastery

Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* This objective can be paired CN objectives as students learn about art in personal, societal, cultural, historical, and daily life contexts along with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation

Knowledge

Skills

* Referencing the original visual artist when using their work
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery

* Note the conjunction “or” used in this objective. Students should be able to show mastery of their ability to cite artists correctly when utilizing OR adapting their original artwork. Learning activities in support of mastery of this objective such as using another artist’s artwork in an exhibit or report, etc., or adapting someone’s work in the creation of personal artwork can be decided upon by the teacher
