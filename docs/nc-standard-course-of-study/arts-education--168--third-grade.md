# Arts Education - Third Grade

Parent Guide for Third Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn to compare the purpose of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies, and how the visual arts connect to other content areas and everyday life.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn how to categorize a variety of artists and art careers based on similarities and differences, and to compare how art has stayed the same and how it has changed over time with the use of new technology, tools, and digital media.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students use their imagination and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan, and develop personal art. They also use realistic sources of inspiration to create original art.
CR.2 Adapt - Students create their own work inspired by the style of another artist or artwork and learn which artworks can be fairly used by everyone.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students use constructive feedback from instructors, peers, and self to improve composition and art skills and explain the themes, materials, and process used in the making of personal art.

PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to use given criteria to select personal artwork for display. They also learn how art exhibitions add value to communities.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn to recognize the skills and techniques used by artists in various works of art and reference how artistic choices are used in a work of art to communicate a “story”.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine their personal preferences when viewing art and learn how to use specific criteria to give respectful feedback about art and composition.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:

* Visit a local museum, community art event, or cultural festival together and look at the different purposes of art.
* Point out art you see daily, such as in murals, logos, quilts, or digital art.
* Provide simple materials such as paper, pencils, crayons, recycled items, or playdough for open-ended creating.
* Encourage your child to draw or paint from their imagination or from something meaningful to them.
* Create a small “art gallery” space at home on the fridge, a corkboard, or a wall to display work.
* Encourage your child to talk about the materials, choices, or story behind their artwork.
* Look at a piece of art and discuss the skills and techniques the artist used and the story or meaning of the work.
* Encourage your child to explain why they like or don’t like a piece of art.
