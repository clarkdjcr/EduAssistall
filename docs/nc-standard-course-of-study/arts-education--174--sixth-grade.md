# Arts Education - Sixth Grade

Parent Guide for Sixth Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn how the geographic location and physical environment impact the subject matter and materials used in visual arts, and how visual arts skills and concepts can be useful in daily life.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn of the skills, characteristics, and behaviors necessary for success in various art careers and make comparisons in how innovative tools and ideas have been used in a variety of artworks.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students learn to use materials in traditional and creative ways and create art that uses original imagery to express ideas and feelings.
CR.2 Adapt - Students combine ideas from multiple artists to create a new work of art and learn terminology related to the copyright rules used to guide artists in creating original art or making new art inspired by existing works.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students use feedback based on specific criteria to improve their work and create a statement that describes their work and the design choices they made.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn to select personal artwork that meets multiple criteria, and how appropriate behaviors used when engaging with art might differ based on the setting in which it is displayed.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students examine artistic styles, subject matter, and art movements from various historical periods and suggest reasons for why an artist made specific choices in their work.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students give reasons for what they like about certain works of art and develop evaluation criteria relevant to the style, purpose, cultural context, or goal of a work.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Point out how local environments, landscapes, weather, or landmarks could inspire an artwork or materials used in artmaking.
* Ask your child how art skills, such as design, problem-solving, or creativity, might help in at home chores and tasks.
* Share examples of art-related careers and discuss what skills might be important for those jobs.
* Encourage your child to use art materials in new and creative ways.
* Have your child create art that expresses their unique ideas and feelings.
* Invite your child to combine two different artists’ styles into one new piece.
* Have your child explain their choices and process in making art.
* Display their work on a wall or table and talk about how showing art at home feels different from seeing art in a museum or gallery.
* Expose your child to art from various historical periods and discuss observations.
* Examine a work of art and how the artist chose to communicate meaning.
* Have your child explain what they like about different works of art.
