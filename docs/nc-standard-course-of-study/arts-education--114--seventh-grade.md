# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

Parent Guide for Seventh Grade General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore influential musicians, musical skills and processes that can be used in any situation, and explore how the media uses music to affect people.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how music teaches habits and behaviors useful in all post-secondary pursuits, and learn about tech tools used to create, edit, and present music.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise short melodies and record musical ideas in simple forms through either music notation or by using a digital audio workstation (DAW).
CR.2 Adapt - Students create a rhythmic variation of an existing musical phrase and explore both legal and ethical issues around creating arrangements, including cover songs.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and the music they perform or produce, honing their ability to demonstrate rhythms, pitches, harmony, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students compare song choices for a specific musical presentation and evaluate appropriate performer and audience etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the use of rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of traditional, contemporary, and world music. They also examine how their personal experiences influence their taste in music.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students compare what they like about multiple pieces of music and use given criteria to objectively evaluate their own presentations, compositions, and musical ideas.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to share the songs they are learning at school
* Listen together to music from different cultures, time periods, or genres, and talk about how it makes you feel
* Point out when ads and the media use music to evoke feelings in the listener – evaluate how effective it was at using music to impact the listener’s mood
* Discuss how the skills they practice in music class can be leveraged in all post-secondary pursuits
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Point out when musicians, writers, artists, and designers have borrowed from another artist, discussing how they had to ask permission
* Encourage students to compose and produce music at home
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
