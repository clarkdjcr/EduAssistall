# Healthful Living - 2nd Grade

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

Second Grade

Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)

Essential Standard:
2.MEH.1
 Demonstrate healthy expression of emotions, mental wellness, and healthy behavior.
Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.1.1 Demonstrate appropriate ways to express needs, wants and feelings.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Everyone has needs, wants, and feelings, and it is important to express them clearly. Appropriate demonstration of needs, wants, and feelings is critical for mental health, relationships, and communication. Appropriate demonstration should include;

Expressing needs:
* use I messages
   * be specific in the address
   * be polite while speaking
   * speak in a calm manner
    Expressing wants:
* use I messages
   * make the want easily understood
   * be sure the want is realistic
   * speak in a calm manner
 Expressing feelings:
* use I messages
   * be honest
   * explain the reason for the feeling
   * speak in a calm manner
Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.1.2 Identify behaviors to avoid risk and promote mental wellness.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Potentially harmful situations will occur, and there are measures that can be taken in order to avoid or minimize the risk. Understanding the possibility for danger is a skill that keeps young people safe. Practicing what to do in risky situations will help a child avoid risks for injury, illness, or hurting others in the future.

* Consider the consequences to actions that could result in emotional or physical harm.
* Avoid peers who are engaging in risky behavior.
* Surround yourself with peers who are engaging in positive, healthy decisions.
* Using refusal strategies such as saying “no.”

Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.1.3 Examine the influence of peers, media, social media, technology, and the family on feelings, emotions, and behaviors.
Verb Defined:
To inspect closely : to inquire into carefully
Student will know and be able to:
Societal influences such as peers, the media, social media, technology, and family have great potential to impact feelings and emotions. Recognizing the existence of these influences and identifying both their positive and negative aspects are crucial for developing critical thinking skills.

A person can unknowingly be persuaded by their peers in various situations, such as:
* treatment of classmates
* academic performance
* overall attitude
* expression of feelings

The influence of media on children extends to health-related issues. While the media has the power to educate on a wide variety of health-related topics, such as nutrition and exercise, it can be a negative force through images and advertisements.

The relationship between a person and their living environment is also an important factor. The people with whom life is shared, family,  shape the view of the world. Observable behaviors in the home may shape feelings and emotions, regardless of the intent. Families work together best when all members help each other feel loved and accepted.

When the impact of peers, media, and the family on feelings and emotions is acknowledged, better informed, healthy choices and decisions. are able to be made.

Essential Standard:
2.MEH.2 Explain how challenges are opportunities for growth.
Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.2.1 Model accessing a resource to get help with solving a problem.
Verb Defined:
An example for imitation or emulation
Student will know and be able to:
Challenges can be opportunities to learn and grow. Seeking help appropriately reinforces the growth mindset by showing that asking for help is a positive, proactive step. Accessing resources, whether people, tools, or strategies, builds confidence, resilience, and problem-solving skills.
* Challenges are a normal part of learning and provide opportunities to grow.
* Different problems might need different kinds of resources (e.g., a teacher, a friend, or a book).
* Identify a challenge or problem they need help with.
   * Select an appropriate resource to address the challenge
      * Parent, guardian, or trusted adult
      * Use a resource that has been approved by a parent, guardian, or trusted adult

Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.2.2 Recount at least two strategies to solve a problem.
Verb Defined:
To relate in detail : narrate
Student will know and be able to:
There are often multiple ways to solve a problem. Trying different strategies can help when the first attempt doesn’t work building persistence and flexibility that can lead to success.
Strategy examples
* Brainstorming
   * a process that allows multiple ideas to be created that may solve the problem
* Role Play
   * gives the students the opportunity to practice ideas to solve problems
* Seek Support
   * seek the advice of a parent, guardian, or trusted adult
* Problem Solving Model
   * break problem into smaller pieces
   * seek assistance/support
   * reflect on outcome
   * adjust strategy

Clarifying Objective:
2.MEH.2.3 Identify strategies that can be applied to various situations to promote resiliency.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:

Resiliency means to “bounce back” (like jumping on a trampoline) and try again when things are hard. There are resiliency strategies that can help a person adapt and recover from difficult challenges.

Strategies:
* Promote a Growth Mindset
   * Frame difficult times as opportunities to learn and not as failures. Praise the effort more than the result when necessary.
* Emotional Awareness
   * It’s okay to express feelings appropriately during difficult situations.
* Problem-Solving Skills
   * Use the situation as a chance to teach how to solve problems to build resiliency.
* Encourage Positive Self-Talk
   * Turn negative self-communication into positive. For example, “I can’t go this, why do I try,” to “I’m going to keep on trying till I get this done!”
* Provide Consistency and Predictability
   * Predictable routine and rule make people more at ease when they know what to expect
* Model Resilience
   * Learn by observing an adult “bounce back from a difficult time.
* Physical Health
   * Physical Health promotes resilience.

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)

Essential Standard:
2.PCH.1 Explain personal behaviors and practices that promote health and prevent disease.
Clarifying Objective:
2.PCH.1.1 Recall the benefits of good dental health.
Verb Defined:
To call back : to bring back to mind
Student will know and be able to:
Teeth and gums are very important. Permanent teeth need to last for an entire life span, so it is important to take care of them.
Benefits:
* Healthier Gums
* Better diet
* Speaking correctly
* Avoid cavities
* Better overall health

Clarifying Objective:
2.PCH.1.2 Execute the proper techniques for brushing teeth.
Verb Defined:
To carry (something) out fully : to put (something) completely into effect
Student will know and be able to:
The following steps are recommended by the American Dental Association:
* Place a soft brush at a 45-degree angle against the gum.
* Move the brush gently in short strokes.
* Brush the outside, inside and flat parts of teeth.
* Be sure to rinse well.
* Brush teeth twice a day

Clarifying Objective:
2.PCH.1.3 Define sleep and its contribution to overall well-being.
Verb Defined:
to determine or identify the essential qualities or meaning of
to discover and set forth the meaning of (something, such as a word)
Student will know and be able to:
Sleep is defined as the natural, easily reversible periodic state of many living things that is marked by the absence of wakefulness and by the loss of consciousness of one's surroundings. It helps children's brains develop by releasing hormones that support growth and repair, and by helping them retain information. Children who don't get enough sleep may have smaller volumes of gray matter in areas of the brain that control attention, memory, and inhibition. Sleep is a time for restoration, repair and rebuilding. It  allows time for the body to recharge and retain all the information from the day.

Clarifying Objective:
2.PCH.1.4 Demonstrate ways to prevent the spread of germs that cause common communicable diseases.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
The best way to prevent the spread of germs is by frequent washing of the hands and knowing the times when it is necessary. Other examples:
* single use of tissues
   * throw them away after use
* sneeze and cough into the elbow instead of hands
* do not share hygiene products (brushes, combs)
* clean surfaces with disinfectant
* avoid touching the face
* avoid close contact
* acquire vaccinations

Clarifying Objective:
2.PCH.1.5 Explain the dangers associated with excessive sun exposure and methods for protecting oneself from these dangers.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Excessive/prolonged sun exposure without proper protection can lead to sunburn. Using sunscreen and avoiding sun exposure between the hours of 10 am and 2 pm can decrease the likelihood of a sunburn. Also, wearing clothing that has UV protection can also help with skin exposure.

Guidance for sunscreen:
* use broad spectrum 15+
* apply liberally (cover all areas exposed to the sun, especially the face, ears, and feet
* reapply every 2 hours
* read label to determine if it is water-resistant

Interpersonal Communication and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)

Essential Standard:
2.ICHR.1 Identify healthy and effective interpersonal communications and relationships to enhance wellbeing.
Clarifying Objective:
2.ICHR.1.1 Describe characteristics of a trusted friend.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Characteristics of a good friend:

* Respect a friend’s right to be different.
   * Differences make everyone special.
* A good listener.
   * Take the time to listen to a friend’s problem or happiness.
* If a friend is in danger, encourage him or her to tell a parent or another trusted adult.
   * If necessary, tell an adult. Good friends help keep one another safe.
* Honor promises.
* Abstains from applying pressure to engage in harmful or wrong things.
* Show friends that they are:
   * cared for
   * needed
   * accepted

Clarifying Objective:
2.ICHR.1.2 Demonstrate techniques of active listening.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly

Student will know and be able to:

Active listening is an important part of communication. It involves more than hearing as it requires full attention to the speaker.

Active Listening Techniques

* Pay attention:
   * Focus all attention on the person speaking
* Show that listening is taking place:
   * Maintain eye contact, nod the head and follow any movement of the speaker by turning the head.
* Don’t Interrupt:
   * Let the other person finish.
* Provide feedback:
   * Restate what the person has said
* Respond Appropriately:
   * Give answers that make the speaker know  the message is understood

Clarifying Objective:
2.ICHR.1.3 Identify the characteristics of bullying and develop skills to respond appropriately.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or
unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Bullying is a repeated behavior that is done on purpose with the intent to control or harm another person. may appear as any of the following:
* physical violence
* looks
* gestures
* actions
* threats
* name calling
* remarks
* rumors
* social exclusion
* harassment

Responses:
* Report the behavior to a trusted adult
* Use problem-solving skills to resolve the conflict
* Express feelings using  “I” messages
* Listen to each other/talk about a solution with a teacher, counselor or principal.

Clarifying Objective:
2.ICHR.1.4 Exemplify how to communicate with others with kindness and respect.
Verb Defined:
To show or illustrate by example
Student will know and be able to:
Communication with others should be done in ways that are positive, kind, and respectful. These communication skills are foundational for building healthy relationships, resolving conflicts, and enhancing emotional well-being. By modeling and practicing kind and respectful communication, empathy, trust, and the ability to connect effectively with others is developed.
* Kind communication includes
   * using gentle words
   * showing empathy
   * being polite
   * helps others feel valued
* Respectful communication means
   * listening carefully
   * waiting their turn to speak
   * using a calm voice
   * helping others feel valued
* Kind and respectful communication builds strong friendships and helps solve disagreements.

Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)

Essential Standard:
2.NPA.1 Explain where food and water come from and their importance to the body.
Clarifying Objective:
2.NPA.1.1 Explain the importance of eating a variety of foods from different groups according to the USDA.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
The USDA divides foods into the following groups:
* fruits
* vegetables
* grains
* protein
* dairy

The food groups are roughly divided by what nutrients they contain. Eating foods from different food groups is important as it provides the body with the nutrition it needs. When eating a meal, it's important to include multiple food groups.

At this grade level, do not yet introduce the different nutrients each food group provides or comment on foods being “good” or “bad.”

Clarifying Objective:
2.NPA.1.2 Classify foods into groups according to USDA.
Verb Defined:
To consider something as belonging to a particular group
Student will know and be able to:
The USDA divides foods into the following groups:
* fruits
* vegetables
* grains
* protein
* dairy.

For combination foods, the food groups are named based on the foods that are present. For example,
Fried rice may have grains (rice), vegetables, and protein (chicken/fish/egg).

Clarifying Objective:
2.NPA.1.3 Describe the body's signals when you are hungry and thirsty.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Food and water are very important to our body. Our bodies have built-in systems to tell us when we are hungry and thirsty.  These signals can be louder or quieter for different people.
When someone is hungry, they may:
* think about food and eating more
* feel like their stomach is empty
* have increased salivation when thinking about food
* have a growling/rumbling stomach
* have low energy or feel tired
* have trouble concentrating
* feel irritable or angry

When someone is thirsty, they may:
* have dry mouth
* think about water or drinking
* have a headache
* have an urge to drink
* have an itchy throat

Clarifying Objective:
2 NPA.1.4 Compare and contrast possible benefits of eating a meal with family or friends versus eating alone.
Verb Defined:
To examine the character or qualities of especially in order to discover resemblances or differences
Student will know and be able to:
Humans are social beings and benefit from being in community with others. When looking into different cultures, all cultures include eating and sharing meals with other people. Research shows that eating with others has many benefits, outside of the nutrients the food provides, such as better mental health, improved self-esteem, improved eating habits, and many others.  In contrast, eating alone may give someone a chance to more easily listen to their body’s signals of hunger and fullness, to relax and regroup, or take a break from others if they are more introverted.

Essential Standard:
2.NPA.2 Examine the benefits of physical activity.
Clarifying Objective:
2.NPA.2.1 Explain why the body needs daily physical activity.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Physical activity is beneficial in many ways.
Physical Health:
* Helps prevent and manage many diseases
* Improves physical abilities
* Enhances bone and muscle health
  Mental/Emotional Health
* Reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety
* Develops better social and problem-solving skills
* Improves Self-image
* Improves Self-confidence
 Brain Health
* improves focus
* improves academic performance

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and Other Drugs (ANCOD)

Essential Standard:
2.ANCOD.1 Understand household safety procedures for products and medicines with adult supervision.
Clarifying Objective:
2.ANCOD.1.1 Define medication and ways that it can be helpful and harmful.
Verb Defined:
to determine or identify the essential qualities or meaning of
to discover and set forth the meaning of (something, such as a word)
Student will know and be able to:
Medication can be defined as a substance used to cure, treat, or prevent illness
Medicines can be helpful in treating maladies when used appropriately by following directions on the bottle/label..
It is not legal or safe to use medicines inappropriately. Some health risks associated with inappropriate medicine and drug use include:
* headache
* dizziness
* nausea
* vomiting
* diarrhea
* heart rate changes
* breathing problems
* body function changes
* organ damage and death.

Taking too much medicine, skipping doses, taking medicine at the wrong time, not as directed or taking the medication of someone else can also have serious health risks.

Clarifying Objective:
2.ANCOD.1.2 Identify the importance of following healthcare provider directions with medications.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Doctors base medication on symptoms, diagnosis, allergies, weight, height, and age. If a person does not follow directions, the condition could get worse, adverse reactions may occur, or the effectiveness of the medication may be impacted.
