# Arts Education - First Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking First Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

1.CN.1.1 Explain how visual arts are used in cultures around the world.
Glossary
* Culture(s) - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Use - To put into action or service.14
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how art changes over a period of time. At the current level, students explain how visual arts are used in cultures around the world. In the next level, students describe how the diverse heritage, customs, and traditions of various cultures in the United States can reflect American visual arts.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* What is meant by culture
* Examples of Global cultures
* How visual arts exists as a common artform across all cultures
* How art has purpose and meaning in global cultures
* Examples of how visual arts exists in global communities
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the reasoning for the ways in which visual arts are used in cultures around the world
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Art is global, and has meaning and purpose in its existence and representation of all cultures in global communities
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Expose students to artworks and art materials from a variety of art communities and global cultures
* Guide student investigation into the existence and purposes of art in diverse global communities
* Design learning activities for students to explain the use of visual art in global cultures
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain how visual arts are used in cultures around the world
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Sculptures from different cultures
* Street art and murals around the world
* Art in celebrations
* Patterns and textiles

1.CR.1.1 Experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events.
Glossary
* Experiment (with) - try out a new procedure, idea, or activity.14
* Ideas - An entity (such as a thought, concept, sensation, or image) actually or potentially present to consciousness.14
* Materials - Substances out of which art is made or composed, ranging from the traditional to “nonart” material and virtual, cybernetic, and simulated materials.17
* Theme - A subject or topic of discourse or of artistic representation.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students built foundational knowledge in creating art through exploration and imaginative play with materials and now experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events. In the next level, students will experiment with creative solutions to challenges to create art.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of visual artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to safely use tools and materials
* How to experiment with materials and new tools
* The elements of art
* How ideas, themes, or events can be expressed in visual art
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Trying out new materials in the creation of personal artwork that communicates combinations of ideas, themes, or events
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* Artwork can vary with materials, ideas, themes or events
* Trying new materials and new ideas is how artists grow
* Adapt materials to assure individual student needs are met
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
* Note the conjunction “or” - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, OR events. The teacher can decide if the student is to use ideas, themes, or events based on the learning activity. Autonomy can also be given to the student in selecting one of the three in their demonstration of mastery of this objective.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide engaging activities that explore a variety of art tools and materials
* Model safe art procedures
* Demonstrate new tools and supplies
* Provide clear expectations in the appropriate and safe use of tools and materials
* Design learning activities for students to experiment with materials in the creation of art
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Value scale paintings
* Building drawings
* Mono-prints with found objects

1.CR.2.2 Explain how and when to give credit to others when creating derivative work.
Glossary
* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Derivative - Having parts that originate from another source.14
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4

Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students developed foundational knowledge in learning the difference between original and copied visual arts ideas or works. At the current level, students explain how and when to credit others when creating derivative work and in the next level, will explain how to share visual artworks fairly.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* When to credit others for use of their ideas or work
* How to credit others for use of their ideas or work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the reasoning/process for crediting others when using their visual arts ideas or works
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “If an artwork isn’t mine, how and when do I credit the person who made it?” to show mastery of this objective
* Artists are often inspired by other artists but they need to give credit to the original artists if using their ideas
* To explain “how” to give credit, a student must explain that all artists must name the original artist and artwork when they create art inspired by or copied from another artist’s work
* To explain “when” to give credit, a student must share that all artists must share the information when they create art inspired by or copied from another artist’s work
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students differentiate between sharing ideas and copying
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective. Students need to show mastery of their ability to explain both how AND when to credit other artists when using their ideas
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Lead a class discussion about providing credit to the original creator
* Model how to credit artists when using their ideas
* Teach students when and how to credit other artists when using their ideas
* Provide opportunities for students to explain how and when to credit other artists when using their ideas
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain how and when to credit other artists when using their ideas
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Students can exchange their explanations in a pair-share discussion
* Teacher can provide various scenarios to the class and have students explain how and when an artist should be credited for their ideas
