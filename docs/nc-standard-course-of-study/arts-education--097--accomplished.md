# Arts Education - Accomplished

Parent Guide for Accomplished Dance

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for dance is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with dance builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed dance educator.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students investigate the historical development of dance and how dance skills can be used to strengthen work in other academic subjects or career fields. Students also develop a plan to prevent, monitor, and overcome physical and mental challenges common to dance.
CN.2 Explore - Students investigate the differing requirements needed to pursue a career in dance and examine how contemporary media has influenced innovation in dance creation and documentation.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students design and use a movement score to prompt spontaneous movement in improvisation of a solo and use choreographic principles, structures, and processes for the selection of relevant movement choices to create a solo. Students also use pictures, writing, symbols, or media technology to recreate a dance phrase.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to create an adaptation of a significant work by selecting a key element to use and another to modify and also explore how parody, adaptation, and artificial intelligence relate to copyright protection.

Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students apply feedback from a variety of sources to improve personal performance when dancing alone and refine movement skills in extended dance phrases with physical awareness. Students also execute each step in a dance clearly and completely with focus and full-body energy to express ideas.
PR.2 Develop - Students make improvements to a theme, topic, or musical selection for a live or recorded solo, duet, or group performance and determine safe and respectful behavioral expectations for formal and informal performances in a variety of cultural contexts. Students also create production element designs that would strengthen the meaning of a solo, duet, or small group dance.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students examine similarities and differences of dance elements used in different interpretations of the same choreographic work and give reasoning for their interpretations of dance based on personal experiences and the historical and cultural background of the work.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students identify moments in a dance when they can physically feel or emotionally connect with the movement and create criteria to provide detailed feedback for performance and choreography.

How to Support Your Dance Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your dance student:
* Clear a little space so your child can move freely and safely.
* Encourage your child to notice how skills like focus, discipline, and teamwork in dance also help with schoolwork and jobs.
* Develop a family plan to include stretching, hydration, balanced meals, and strategies for handling stress.
* Research the training and skills required for different dance careers.
* Have your child design a movement score to prompt an improvisation of a solo.
* Have your child choreograph a solo that expresses a relevant topic or idea and design possible production elements to enhance the meaning.
* Encourage practice of routines to improve skills.
* Discuss the interpretation of different choreographed works.
* Watch dance together and share moments where you each felt the movement in your own body while watching.
