# Arts Education - Kindergarten

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Kindergarten
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

K.CN.1.1 Describe how art changes over time.
Glossary
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline by establishing foundational knowledge that art, both personal and as it exists around the world, develops over a period of time. At the current level, students describe how art changes over time. In the next level, students explain how visual arts are used in cultures around the world.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* That personal art can change over time
* That visual art as an art form changes with innovation and other societal, cultural, and daily life influences
* How to describe the ways in which art has evolved from one stage or time period to another
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of the ways in which art can evolve
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Use art from prehistoric to contemporary
* This objective can be paired with other CN objectives if examining how visual arts exist in daily life, including home and school has evolved (CN.1.2) or how art has changed with technology and digital media tools (CN.2.2). It can also be paired with CR.1.2 as students evolve their own original art that expresses personal identity that does not rely on copying or tracing.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of a variety of artworks, artists throughout different time periods, and places around the world
* Design learning activities for students to describe how art changes over time
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how art changes over time
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Cave paintings to graffiti
* Mark making
* Variety of portraits throughout history, from various countries
* Landscapes from throughout history
* Virtual tours of museums (local and globally)
* Compare clothing styles from people in artwork
* Discover/compare buildings, technologies, landscapes
* Scavenger hunt of various materials, subjects

* Use - To put into action or service.14

Knowledge

* That art can be found throughout natural and man-made environments
* Examples of how visual arts exist in daily life environments with a specific focus on home and school contexts.
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming how visual arts exist in daily life environments including home, and school
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery

Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Art exists in multiple forms in our daily lives
* Note the conjunction “and” - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to identify how visual arts exist in daily life, including home AND school. Depending on the learning activity, the student can identify examples of art found both in home and school in one assessment, or home and school can be explored independently in different learning activities
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

* Facilitate classroom discussion about art in daily life
* Provide examples of visual artworks that exist in home and school environments
* Provide introductory art vocabulary in a variety of ways
* Design learning activities for students to identify where they experience art in their daily lives, including home and school environments.
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to identify how visual arts exist in daily life, including home and school
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Examining artworks in whole group
* Gallery walk with teacher selected examples
* Student show and tell
* Scavenger hunt

K.CR.1.1 Create original art through exploration and imaginative play with materials.
Glossary
* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Explore - Investigate multiple possibilities to learn more about an idea.17
* Imaginative Play - Experimentation by children in defining identities and points of view by developing skills in conceiving, planning, making art, and communicating.17

This objective develops the EQ-throughline by establishing foundational knowledge in using exploration and imaginative play with materials to create art. At the current level, students create art through exploration and imaginative play with materials. In the next level, students will experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of visual artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to creatively experiment with materials in the making of personal art
* How to safely use tools and materials
* The elements of art
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Using exploration and creative experimentation of materials to create an original work of art
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* It is important to note that students at this level possess a wide range of abilities. In the case of special needs students and accelerated learners, adjustments will need to be made.
* This objective can be paired with CN.2.1 in which students explain how all people are artmakers and with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation

* Design learning activities for students to use imaginative play and exploration in the creation of personal art
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to create original art through exploration and imaginative play with materials
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:

K.CR.1.2 Create original art that expresses personal identity that does not rely on copying or tracing.

* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Original Art - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14

Identity involves a sense of continuity, or the feeling that one is the same person today that one was yesterday or last year (despite physical or other changes). Such a sense is derived from one’s body sensations; one’s body image; and the feeling that one’s memories, goals, values, expectations, and beliefs belong to the self.20

Knowledge

* What is meant by personal identity and things about themselves in defining their personal identity

* How to use tools and materials safely in the creation of personal art

* The elements of art

Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* This objective encourages creative exploration and personal development of artistic skills
* This objective can be paired with CN1.2 with students identifying how visual arts exist in daily life, including home and school, CN.2.1 in which students explain how all people are artmakers, and other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

K.CR.2.2 Explain the difference between original and copied visual arts ideas or works.

Knowledge

Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

* Students should be able to answer the question, “What is copied art, and what is original art?” to show mastery of this objective.
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 as students demonstrate sharing their ideas with others. Students can be guided in understanding when they are making original choices versus copying the ideas or works of others in the creation of an array of art projects. This objective can also be paired with CR.1.2 in which students create personal art without relying on copying or tracing that expresses personal identity
* Note the conjunctions “and” “or” used in this objective. Because the student is being asked to differentiate between originality AND copying, both concepts need to be addressed; however, whether the student is explaining the difference between original and copied ideas OR original and copied works is dependent on the learning activity and up to the teacher
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Facilitate discussion about what it means to copy ideas or works versus using and creating original ideas and work
* Show examples of copied and original works
* Design learning activities for students to explain the difference between original and copied visual arts ideas or works
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain the difference between original and copied visual arts ideas or works
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Students can pair-share to explain if the work they created was their own original creation or copied from the ideas or works of another
* Students can be assisted in making a video recording in which they explain the difference between original art and art that is copied from the ideas or works of another artist
* The teacher can ask students as they circulate the room if what they are working on is an original creation or work copied from the ideas of work of another and have them briefly explain the difference
* At the end of a course, students can do a portfolio review of their work, explaining the difference between which projects were original works of art and which were derived from copying the ideas or works of others
