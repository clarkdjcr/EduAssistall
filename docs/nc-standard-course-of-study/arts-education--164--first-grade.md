# Arts Education - First Grade

Parent Guide for First Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn how visual arts exist in cultures around the world, along with cross-curricular connections of visual arts to other arts disciplines.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn of various careers in the visual arts and how technology, innovations, and digital media are used to make art.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students experiment with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events and use imaginary sources of inspiration to create original art.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn to differentiate between sharing ideas and copying, and examine how to give credit to other artists when borrowing their existing ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students learn how to use positive feedback from instructors and peers to improve personal works of art and how to identify the elements of art (Line, Shape, Form, Color, Value, Texture, Space) used in personal art.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn how personal artwork can be presented to others and the variety of ways that audiences can interact with works of art.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn to describe the media, process, and tools used to create art and how meaning is conveyed through the use of symbols, subjects, or themes.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine personal preferences for why a work of art is personally appealing and provide positive feedback for personal and peer works.
How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Explore art from different cultures.
* Share examples of careers in art.
* Explore how technology can be used to make and share art.
* Encourage your child to create original art using a variety of materials.
* Have your child use their imagination to come up with creative ideas for art projects.
* Discuss the difference between sharing an idea and copying someone’s work. Remind them to give credit when using ideas from others.
* Have your child explain the elements of art, including line, shape, color, texture, etc.,  in their own artwork.
* Let them choose a way of displaying their art at home.
* Look at artwork together and discuss how the art was made.
* Talk about how symbols or themes in art tell a story or show meaning.
* Encourage them to share what they like about their own and others’ art, and practice giving kind, specific feedback.
