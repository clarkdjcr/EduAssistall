# Healthful Living - 1st Grade

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

First Grade

Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)

Essential Standard:
1.MEH.1 Understand the connection between healthy expressions of emotions, mental wellness, and healthy behaviors.
Clarifying Objective:
1.MEH.1.1 Identify appropriate ways to express needs, wants and feelings.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Everyone has feelings and emotions, and throughout the day, feelings and emotions can change. Children can feel comfortable or uncomfortable. It is important to know how to calmly express their feelings in ways that do not hurt others.

Expressing needs:
* use I messages
* be specific
* be polite
* speak in a calm manner

   Expressing wants
* use I messages
* make the want easily understood
* be sure the want is realistic
* speak in a calm manner

   Expressing feelings
* use I messages
* be honest
* explain the reason for the feeling
* speak in a calm manner

Facial expressions and body language may also communicate needs, wants, and feelings as well.
Clarifying Objective:
1.MEH.1.2 Describe how different situations impact emotions and vary among individuals.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Children develop a deeper understanding of emotions and how situations can make them feel differently. They are also learning that people might respond to the same situation in different ways. Identifying and expressing emotions in healthy ways builds a foundation for mental wellness and positive relationships. By recognizing emotional differences, empathy and respect can be developed for others' feelings.

* Situations can make people feel different emotions (e.g., happy, sad, scared, excited).

* The same situation might make one person feel one way, and another person feel a different way.

* It's okay to feel a variety of emotions and talk about them with trusted adults or friends.

Clarifying Objective:
1.MEH.1.3 Utilize effective communication to express and cope with emotions.
Verb Defined:
To make use of : turn to practical use or account
Student will know and be able to:
Everyone has feelings and emotions which can change throughout the day. It is important to know how to calmly express their feelings in ways that do not hurt others. Feelings can be expressed by facial expressions, body language, or by using words.

* Understand the emotion being felt
* Use I messages to express the emotion
* Use active listening to understand both sides of the communication
* Express the emotion in the correct manner

Essential Standard:
1.MEH.2 Remember that effort and practice lead to improved skills.
Clarifying Objective:
1.MEH.2.1 Identify more than one strategy to solve a problem.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Challenges can be overcome with effort, persistence, and trying different strategies. Mistakes are part of learning and help foster a growth mindset. If one strategy doesn’t work, it’s important to try another. Exploring multiple approaches to solving a problem builds resilience and confidence in the ability to tackle difficult tasks.
Problem-solving strategies:
* Brainstorming
* Trial and error
* Identify possible causes
* Define the problem
* Work backward

Clarifying Objective:
1.MEH.2.2 Identify resources to get help with solving a problem.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
A person doesn't have to solve every problem on their own. Identify and use resources, like people, tools, or materials, teaches when to seek help and solve problems. Asking for help is a smart and positive step and aligns with growth mindset principles by encouraging collaboration and resourcefulness.
* Ask a parent, guardian, or trusted adult at home
* Ask a teacher or school counselor to assist with a problem at school.
* Different problems may need different resources (e.g., a teacher for learning help, a friend for sharing toys).

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)

Essential Standard:
1.PCH.1 List various personal behaviors and practices that promote health and prevent disease.
Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.1.1 Explain that germs produce illness and can be spread from one person to another.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Germs are so small they can only be seen with a microscope, but they can cause big problems like fevers, headaches, sore throats, rashes, and stomachaches. Germs are found everywhere and can be transferred through body, air, or surface contact. They enter the body through the nose, mouth, or cuts on skin. People don’t know that germs have invaded their bodies until they get sick.

Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.1.2 Demonstrate measures for preventing the spread of germs.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
The best way to prevent the spread of germs is by the frequent washing of the hands. Other methods of germ prevention:
* single use of tissues
   *  throw them away after use
* sneeze and cough into the elbow instead of hands
* do not share hygiene products (brushes, combs)
* clean surfaces
* do not share food, beverages, or hygiene products

Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.1.3 Summarize the transition between primary and permanent teeth and steps for seeking help for dental problems.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Everyone has two sets of teeth: primary and permanent. Primary teeth grow in from birth. It is normal for first graders to start losing these teeth so the permanent set of teeth can grow. For a while, a child will have a combination of primary teeth and permanent teeth. Children will lose baby teeth between ages of 6 and 13. Permanent teeth fill in from ages 6 to 22, with wisdom teeth coming in last. When problems occur with teeth, including cavities or dental injury, a dentist should be visited.

Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.1.4 Use appropriate clothing according to the weather.
Verb Defined:
To put into action or service : utilize
Student will know and be able to:
Clothing should be chosen according to the weather or season:
* Spring
   * lightweight clothing
   * thin layers that can be removed with increasing temperatures during the day
* Summer
   * lightweight, breathable fabric, shorts, t-shirts, and flowy sundresses
   * hat and sunglasses for extra protection from harmful sun.
* Fall/Winter
   * use thicker fabrics like wool, fleece, and denim to retain body heat
   * layer up with a base layer like a long-sleeved shirt or turtleneck, then add a sweater or jacket. Finish with a scarf or hat.

Essential Standard:
1.PCH.2 Understand necessary steps to prevent and respond to unintentional injury.
Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.2.1 Identify and describe situations that cause injury.
Verb Defined:
Give an account in words relevant to the situation
Student will know and be able to:
Injury can happen in various situations. Accidents are a part of everyday life. Learning to be aware of our surroundings keeps us safe.
Situations that may cause injury.
* wet floors
* loose items on the ground
* hot surfaces
* sharp items
* electrical outlets/cords
* cracked or uneven playground surfaces
* engaging in activities without proper safety equipment

Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.2.2 Identify items that can cause burns and strategies to prevent fire and burn injury.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Identify the following as potential hazards that could cause a burn injury
*  hot liquids
*  flames from fires
*  steam
*  hot cooking surfaces
*  electrical appliances
*  chemicals like acids or drain cleaners
*  sunlight (sunburn)
*  heated appliances like irons or curling irons
*  even certain types of batteries, if mishandled

To prevent fires and burn and burn injuries
* keep flammable products, such as matches, lighters, and candles out of the reach of children
* install and maintain smoke alarms
* frequently check heating equipment
   * turned off
   * unplugged when not in use.

Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.2.3 Execute the Stop, Drop, and Roll response.
Verb Defined:
To carry (something) out fully : to put (something) completely into effect
Student will know and be able to:
A fire will not burn without oxygen. If clothing catches on fire, it is best to use the Stop, Drop, and Roll response.

STOP:
* never run.
* do not wave arms.
DROP:
* drop to the ground
* and put hands over face for protection.

ROLL:
* Roll back and forth until the fire is completely out.
Clarifying Objective:
1.PCH.2.4 Execute an emergency phone call.
Verb Defined:
To carry (something) out fully : to put (something) completely into effect
Student will know and be able to:
In emergency situations, call 9-1-1.
During the call:
* State
   * name
   * address
   * what is wrong

* When calling 9-1-1
   * speak loudly
   * speak clearly
   * allow the emergency operator to hang up first.

Interpersonal Communications and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)

Essential Standard:
1.ICHR.1 Recognize healthy and effective interpersonal communications and relationships.
Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.1 Explain the importance of demonstrating respect for the personal space and boundaries of others.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
The classroom space has to be shared by everyone, but each person needs some part of that space as their own. Each person has their own personal space and boundaries. There are multiple benefits of respecting space and boundaries:
* Develops respect
* Helps prevent conflict
* Promotes trust
* Allows comfort to be developed

Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.2 Describe positive characteristics that are unique to each individual.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Each person has their own positive characteristics. These characteristics may include:
* Personality
* Curiosity
* Compassion
* Humor
* Hobbies/Interest
* Creativity
* Physical abilities
* Cognitive abilities

Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.3 Demonstrate how to tell a parent, guardian, or trusted adult when feeling threatened or unsafe.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
When feeling threatened or unsafe, communication with a parent, guardian, or trusted adult is vital to ensure safety. For the message to be heard, the student should:

* Find an appropriate space to share where there will not be any interruptions
* Speak clearly and calmly
* Explain why you feel threatened or unsafe
* Describe the situation
* Ask for help
* Listen to the answer given by the adult
* Follow up later if necessary

Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.4 Recognize that anyone who has experienced bullying or mistreatment is not at fault.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
It's vital to understand that people who have experienced bullying or mistreatment are never to blame. It is the fault of those who engage in bullying and mistreatment, not the victim. Everyone should be treated with respect, kindness, and dignity.  Those who have been the victim of this treatment should be supported, not blamed.

Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.5 Demonstrate effective refusal skills, including saying no and moving away to enforce personal boundaries for yourself and others.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Effective refusal skills are necessary to ensure comfort and safety. There are several ways to effectively communicate refusal.
* Say No
   * be firm but calm in speaking
   * be respectful in speaking
   * repeat if necessary
* Move away from the person or situation
   * Walk away if possible
* Give a reason
   * My parents don’t want me to do this
   * This is not safe

Clarifying Objective:
1.ICHR.1.6 Identify a parent, guardian, or trusted adult within your home, school and community who can be informed when feeling threatened or harmed.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
It’s important to know who to turn to when feeling threatened or being harmed

* Parent(s)
* Guardian
* Trusted adult within your home
   * Grandparent
* Teacher
* School Counselor
* Resource Officer

Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)

Essential Standard:
1.NPA.1 Describe where food and water come from and their importance to the body.
Clarifying Objective:
1.NPA.1.1 Name the reasons why we need to drink water.
Verb Defined:
To mention or identify by name
Student will know and be able to:
Water is essential to the body’s function and health, as over half of the body is made of water.  Water is essential for overall health, digestion, waste elimination, nutrient delivery, joint lubrication, saliva production, skin health, organ function, and well-being. Water can be consumed through all beverages and many foods, especially fruits and vegetables.

Educators should not categorize certain beverages as “good” or “bad” at this grade level.

Clarifying Objective:
1.NPA.1.2 Explore a variety of foods and why it’s important to eat them.
Verb Defined:
To investigate, study, or analyze : look into
Student will know and be able to:
Being exposed to a variety of foods is helpful to providing all the necessary nutrients for our body. A variety of foods including;  fruits, vegetables, grains, dairy products, and meats/beans/nuts/eggs. Different foods provide our body with different nutrients. A variety of foods can be explored from reading books about different foods, watching videos, visiting a farm or farmer's markets, and/or having no-pressure taste tests.

Clarifying Objective:
1.NPA.1.3
Categorize the sources of a variety of foods.
Verb Defined:
To put into a category : classify
Student will know and be able to:
Plants provide fruits, vegetables, grains, and some sources of protein (nuts and legumes). Animals are the source of meats, fish, and poultry and also provide milk from which cheese, yogurt, and ice cream are made. Farms, ranches, and orchards are the sources of most foods. Grocery stores sell foods from farmers and also purchase foods from factories that create and package foods. Many families shop at farmers’ markets to buy the freshest foods available.

Essential Standard:
1.NPA.2 Recognize the benefits of physical activity for the body.
Clarifying Objective:
1.NPA.2.1 List physical activities that are beneficial to your body.
Verb Defined:
Catalog : checklist : to make a list of : enumerate
Student will know and be able to:
Physical activity is any voluntary movement that uses energy. It has many health benefits at every age. During childhood, regular physical activity helps build strong bones and muscles and helps make the heart and lungs work efficiently. Physical activity also benefits sleep, self-esteem, mood, and stress levels. Physical activity, especially play, provides opportunities for children to make friends and practice teamwork, following rules, and good sportsmanship. Physical activity can include:
* walking to school or the store
* playing tag on the playground
* playing sports
* dancing to a favorite song
* walking
* running
* playing
* riding a bike.

Clarifying Objective:
1 NPA.2.2 Describe enjoyable physical activities that can be done inside and outside of school.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Physical activity is any voluntary movement that uses energy. Activities that are done at school may include:
* playing on the playground
* walking to different parts of the school
* participating in activities in gym class
* playing tag
* playing sports at school

   Activities done outside of school may include:
* playing sports
* walking to school or other locations
* dancing in a class or at home
* gymnastics
* playing outside
* riding a bike

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and Other Drugs (ANCOD)

Essential Standard:
1.ANCOD.1 Understand household safety procedures for products and medicines with adult supervision.
Clarifying Objective:
1.ANCOD.1.1 Recognize the harmful effects of medicine when used incorrectly.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Taking too much medicine or medicines intended for friends or family can have very serious side effects:
* overdose
* harmful side effects
* nausea
* allergic reactions
* changes in organ functions
* death
* taking old medicine may reduce the effectiveness of the drug

Clarifying Objective:
1.ANCOD.1.2 Recognize how to behave safely with medicines and household cleaners.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
It is dangerous for a child to take medicine by himself or from a friend. The family or a trusted adult of the child should always follow doctor's instructions or over-the-counter labels for taking medicine. Poisoning can occur by eating, drinking, touching, or smelling a household cleaner.
Safety with medicines”
* household cleaners or their containers should not be touched
* if a household cleaner is found, it should be left where it is
   * the container should not be opened or smelled
* If an unmarked container is found with liquid in it, it should never be swallowed
   * some household cleaners can be bright yellow, red, or blue
   * may look like juice or sports drinks.

Clarifying Objective:
1 ANCOD.1.3 Explain the importance of asking a parent, guardian, or trusted adult before handling household products.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Many products in the home, such as cleaners, are potentially dangerous. Common household products can be harmful or fatal if swallowed, inhaled, touched, or placed in the eye. Parents, guardians, or trusted adults should be consulted before handling.

Clarifying Objective:
1 ANCOD.1.4 Identify strategies for reporting harmful substances.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
When a harmful or unknown substance is found, a child should not come into contact with it but should consult a parent, guardian, or trusted adult. Other sources that may be contacted:
* Poison Control Center 1-800-222-1222
* The number should be stored in a central location
   * on the refrigerator
   * by a landline
   * programmed into a cell phone
* 9-1-1 if an emergency involving a household product or medicine occurs
* At school
   * tell a teacher, faculty, or staff member
