# Arts Education - Eighth Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Eighth Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The 6-8 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

8.CN.1.1 Explain why and how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina.
Glossary
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Ideas - An entity (such as a thought, concept, sensation, or image) actually or potentially present to consciousness.14
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students analyzed how individuals and groups of artists have influenced various cultures and now explain why and how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina. Students with a K-8 continuum in Visual Arts should progress to the intermediate level where they will explain the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts. Beginning level students will describe how the emergence of visual arts as an art form has influenced society.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How visual arts have influenced the ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina
* Purposes of visual arts in North Carolina
* Examples of artists or works that were influential in shaping ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the reasoning of why and how the visual arts influence the ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “and” used twice in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to explain why AND how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, AND experiences in North Carolina. Depending on the learning activity students can explain both why and how visual arts influence all three: ideas, beliefs, and experiences in NC or this objective can be broken apart and covered in multiple ways throughout the course of study. Ultimately the student needs to master the entirety of the content of the objective
* This objective can be paired with other CR objectives as students use ideas in the creation of personal and derivative art and with RE objectives in the analysis and evaluation of art
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of how ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina have been influenced by visual arts
* Lead classroom discussion on how visual arts can influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences
* Guide student exploration in the various ways ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina have been influenced by visual arts
* Provide learning activities in which students can explain why and how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain why and how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* NC artists past and present
* Art industries
* Art used for social and activist awareness
* Infographics
* Art used at celebrations and events to create ambiance
* Art in community parks and exhibits
* NC Pottery

8.CR.2.2 Explain how to share works of art legally and ethically in both physical and digital spaces.
Glossary
* Digital Media - Anything in electronic form including photos, images, video, audio files, or artwork created or presented through electronic means; a gallery of artwork viewed electronically through any device.17
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Ethical - Moral guidelines and philosophical principles for determining appropriate behavior.17
* Legal - The legislated parameters and protocols of media arts systems, including user agreements, publicity releases, copyright, etc.17
* Works - Something produced by the exercise of creative talent or expenditure of creative effort: artistic production.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students identified the legal and ethical issues for creating derivative art and now explain how to share works of art legally and ethically in both physical and digital spaces. Students with a K-8 continuum in visual arts should progress to the intermediate level where they will evaluate the ethics of making, producing, and distributing both original and derivative art. The high school beginning level establishes the foundational knowledge by having students compare artistic adaptation, appropriation, fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons in the visual arts.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Procedures for legal and ethical sharing of artwork in physical spaces
* Procedures for legal and ethical sharing of artwork in digital spaces
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the parameters for legally and ethically sharing works of art physically and digitally
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “What are the legal and ethical procedures for sharing someone else’s work online versus sharing it in person or in print?” to show mastery of this objective
* It’s important to understand that legality and ethics are separate ideas: both need to be considered in this objective. For example, a student might legally use cultural symbols or artwork from another community by obtaining permission or following copyright laws, but still act unethically by presenting those works without respect for their cultural significance or proper credit. This kind of cultural appropriation disrespects the original creators and their traditions. Recognizing this difference helps students see that following the law doesn’t always mean they’re doing what’s right
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students combine imagery from multiple sources to create personal artwork. The students would first understand how art is legally and ethically shared in digital and physical spaces before finding imagery of artists to use in the creation of their personal artwork
* Note the conjunction “and” used twice in this objective -students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to explain how to share works of art legally AND ethically in both physical AND digital spaces. Depending on the learning activity, the legal and ethical explanations of sharing art in both digital and physical environments can be assessed simultaneously or assessed separately in multiple learning activities. This decision can be made by the teacher, or autonomy can be given to the student to decide how they want to show mastery of objective
