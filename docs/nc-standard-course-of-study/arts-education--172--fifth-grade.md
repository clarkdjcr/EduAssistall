# Arts Education - Fifth Grade

Parent Guide for Fifth Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore visual arts in cultures within the United States, including indigenous and other cultural groups, as well as explore how concepts learned in visual arts can be applied to other content areas.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how art skills can be useful in various careers and learn of artists or works of art innovative for their time.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students learn to use different materials and techniques inspired by ideas and imagery from around the world and create original works of art of realistic and imaginary images, abstract designs, and artwork that does not represent objects.
CR.2 Adapt - Students create personal art inspired by ideas of other artists and learn to correctly cite artists when using or adapting their original artwork.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students learn to use feedback from self and peer-evaluation to improve their work, and how to use criteria in making a statement to describe their work.

PR.2 Develop - Students learn to use criteria when choosing personal art for a portfolio and make comparisons in the purpose and experiences various art museums, galleries, and other venues provide.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn to sort American artists by their style, the type of art they create, or the art movement they belong to. Students also compare interpretations of art with their classmates.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students give reasons for why they like certain works of art and learn how to identify evaluation criteria relevant to the style, type, and cultural or historical background of a work of art.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:

* Compare art from other cultures your child learns about.
* Have your child talk about the connections they see from what they learn in art to other subjects.
* Explain how art skills can be used in different careers.
* Expose your child to artists and works that were innovative for their time in history.
* Provide different materials to use in artmaking.
* Encourage your child to try different techniques and create both realistic and abstract art.
* When your child is inspired by another artist or online image, help them to cite their source.
* Have your child tell you about their artmaking choices and process.
* Collect your child’s artwork and have them make selections of works for display.
* Visit a local gallery or take a virtual museum tour online and discuss the experience.
* Expose your child to American Artists and have them sort them by style, media, themes, or other similarities.
* Look at art together and compare interpretations.
* Ask your child to tell what they like about different works of art.
