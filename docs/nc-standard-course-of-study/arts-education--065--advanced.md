# Arts Education - Advanced

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Advanced Honors
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a students’ choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator. Credit earned from middle and high school courses meeting these minimums satisfies the Arts Education Graduation requirement and applies toward the Arts Education Diploma Endorsement. Courses that do not meet these minimums are not designed to build proficiency and are often used to spark interest in more intensive proficiency-based study later in a student’s education.
To account for variations in skill progression, as well as to embed multiple entry points across the K-12 spectrum, the objectives in the high school NCSCOS for Visual Arts are organized by proficiency level, rather than grade level. Minimum proficiency outcomes are provided at each level to show what a student should know and be able to do by the end of a course or program when they create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts. Courses using the Advanced High School standards are inherently honors.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

AD.CN.1.1 Describe how the visual arts are impacted by history, society, culture, and personal life.
Glossary
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences.17
* Society - An enduring and cooperating social group whose members have developed organized patterns of relationships through interaction with one another; a community, nation, or broad grouping of people having common traditions, institutions, and collective activities and interests.14
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how society, culture, and politics are reflected in visual arts and now describe the impact of history, society, culture, and personal life on visual arts.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How visual arts draws inspiration, meaning, and purpose from historical, societal, cultural, and personal life
* The impact of societal, cultural, and historical events on Visual Arts
* Visual Artists whose work was impacted by societal, cultural, historical, and personal events
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of how visual arts are impacted by societal, cultural, historical, and personal life events
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Art plays an integral role in documenting history as it reflects society, culture, and personal lived experiences
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how the visual arts are impacted by history, society, culture, AND personal life. Depending on the learning activity, all four aspects: history, society, culture, and personal life can be addressed in one assessment or explored separately in multiple assessments over the course of study. Ultimately the student must master the entirety of the objective
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of how history, society, culture, and personal life has impacted visual arts
* Guide student exploration of the influences history, society, culture, and personal life has had on visual artists and on how art is created, edited, presented, and viewed
* Lead classroom discussion on the impact of history, society, culture, and personal life on visual arts
* Provide learning activities in which students can describe how the visual arts are impacted by history, society, culture, and personal life
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how the visual arts are impacted by history, society, culture, and personal life
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Artist study in which students examine the identity and influences on the artist’s work
* Pair this with CR.1.2 and have students create their own artistic work that they can describe how history, society, culture, and personal life impacted their artistic choices
* Collages
* Multi-modal timelines
* Pair with RE objectives to analyze a work of art and describe how impacts of history, society, culture or personal life were evident in the artistic choices made by the artist

AD.CR.2.1 Demonstrate how appropriation and appreciation applies to creating personal artwork with new tools and media.
Glossary

Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students demonstrated awareness of the implications of appropriation and appreciation when creating personal artwork and now demonstrate how appropriation and appreciation applies to creating personal artwork with new tools and media.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that visual artists balance inspiration and originality with ethics by creatively transforming or adapting other artistic ideas into new works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The difference between drawing inspiration from a culture or another artist's work (appreciation) and exploiting or misrepresenting it without context or permission (appropriation)
* The implications of using images or ideas of others without giving proper credit
* The importance of attributing sources and ensuring respectful representation
* How to use new tools and media safely and appropriately
* The elements of art
* The principles of design
* The creative design process
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Showing/Execute/Modeling how appropriation and appreciation applies to creating personal artwork with new tools and media
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* It is important for artists to know how to be respectful and ethical when using cultural references in personal art
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.2 in which students explain how to gain the legal right to use another artist's work. This objective can also be paired with CN objectives as students learn about art in personal, societal, cultural, historical, and daily life contexts, especially CN.2.2 in which students analyze how visual arts tools, ideas, innovations, and technologies are expanding opportunities in business, careers, and life. Lastly, this objective can also be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation of student work
* Note the conjunction “and” used twice in this objective - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to demonstrate how appropriation AND appreciation applies to creating personal artwork with new tools AND media. Student work could show the demonstration of both knowledge of appropriation and appreciation and use both new tools and media, or demonstration of appropriation and appreciation when using new tools and media can be assessed separately in the creation of multiple works over the course of study

* Note the conjunction “and” used twice in this objective - students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to use relevant traditional AND contemporary criteria, AND personal artistic vision when critiquing personal work. Both sets of criteria and how well the work satisfies artistic vision can be used simultaneously in the critique or used independently over multiple critiques of personal artwork
* Specificity is the hallmark of an effective critique
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Have students provide examples of personal artwork for critique (created in mastery of CR objectives)
* Provide or guide students in developing traditional and contemporary criteria to be used in evaluation of work
* Have students reflect on their artistic vision used in creation of the work
* Guide students in using visual literacy skills to evaluate personal art based on traditional and contemporary criteria as well as artistic vision
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to critique personal art considering relevant traditional and contemporary criteria as well as artistic vision
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Objective response to art through critiques
* The role of critiques in VA
* Have students use a subset of vocabulary that is the focus of their evaluation
* Practicing using traditional and contemporary criteria in the evaluation of art as a class, with a partner, then independently on examples of other artwork
* Students can write a critique or record a video blog of their critique of their personal work
