# Healthful Living - 5th Grade

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

The circulatory and respiratory systems work in close partnership to deliver oxygen throughout the body and remove carbon dioxide waste, essentially relying on each other to expedite the gas exchange at the cellular level; the respiratory system brings oxygen into the lungs, while the circulatory system transports that oxygen via blood to the body's tissues, and simultaneously collects carbon dioxide from those tissues to be expelled by the lungs through breathing.

Clarifying Objective:
5.NPA.1.3 Calculate nutrients based on the components of a Nutrition Facts label.
Verb Defined:
To determine by mathematical processes : to solve or probe the meaning of
Student will know and be able to:
The Nutrient Facts label is a label on packaged food that details the nutritional content of a serving size of that food.  A serving size is merely a measurement (like a gallon of paint) used when calculating nutrition information. It is not instructions on how much of the food a person should eat. For example, a serving size of bread is a slice. Typically, two slices of bread are eaten in a sandwich.

The Nutrient Facts label includes:
* Serving Size
* Calories (per serving size) - a measurement of energy
* Nutrients
* Percent Daily Value- of the nutrients per serving size based on a 2,000-calorie diet
* Footnote

People have different nutrition needs. Calories should not be labeled as something bad; Calories are good!  A calorie is a measurement of energy, and all bodies need calories to live. Food labels use a 2000-calorie diet as an average, and many children need much more than this.

Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
The physical effects of alcohol, nicotine, and cannabis depend a great deal on how much of the substance has been consumed and a person's individual tolerance.

Some short-term effects of alcohol:
* Lowered inhibitions
* Poor coordination
* Slurred speech
* Inability to think clearly
* Lack of coordination
* Nausea/sickness
* Death
Some long-term effects of alcohol:
* Alcoholism
* Liver disease
* Diabetes
* Poor Physical Health
* Poor Emotional Health

  Some short-term effects of nicotine:
* Increase heart and blood pressure
* Brain activation, feeling of pleasure
* Nausea
* Lightheadedness
* Reduced appetite
* Shortness of breath

Some long-term effects of nicotine:
* Chronic diseases (heart, stroke and respiratory)
* Cancer
* Eye disease
* Gum disease
* Diabetes

   Short-term effects of cannabis:
* Mood change
* Impaired attention and short-term memory
* Dry mouth
* Increased appetite
* Motor skill impairment

Some long-term effects of cannabis:
* Brain fog
* Lowered motivation
* Attention difficulties
* Learning difficulties

Personal and societal influences to use alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and other drugs come in many forms. Many parents and other adults use alcohol socially. TV, the internet, and movies make the use of these substances normal.

Possible personal influences:
* Attempts to be more assertive, talkative, or popular and forget their self-esteem issues
* To cope with stress
* Impulsive behavior
* Genetic predisposition toward dependency
* Low self-esteem
* Lack of assertive communication skills

  Possible societal influences:
* Ads/media that depict positive experiences
* To appear older and mimic the behavior of others, including parents and older siblings
* Ability to get or acquire the substance
* Peer pressure and negative peer groups
* Lack of school involvement
* History of conflicts and stress
* Poor/no relationships with non-parental adults

5.ANCOD.2.2 Analyze the use of various marketing strategies to influence people to use alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and other drug products.
Verb Defined:
To study or determine the nature and relationship of the parts of something
Student will know and be able to:

Each year, a person sees close to 40,000 television commercials along with other forms of advertising including magazine ads, billboards, websites, brand-related accessories, signage at sporting events, sponsorship of professional/college teams, sports TV and radio programs. Child development experts have voiced concerns about the possible links between exposure to advertising and the development of attitudes towards alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and other drug (ANCOD) products.

Marketing strategies:
* Reinforce the idea that the use of ANCOD use is an everyday activity
* Increases positive expectations about drinking
* ANCOD use is a primary ritual into adulthood in current society
* ANCOD media placement, especially during weekends and prime time during favorite shows, sports programming and films
* Routine placement of ads in magazines with large youth readerships
* Rhythmic, pop, urban, and alternative music make repeated references to ANCOD
* Cross-marketing, through clothing, brand-related products and special promotions

5.ANCOD.3.1 Use refusal skills to resist the pressure to experiment with alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and other drugs.
Verb Defined:
To put into action or service : employ : utilize
Student will know and be able to:
While it’s important to teach about the dangers of drugs, alcohol, and tobacco, it’s important to learn how to say “No.”
When refusing:
* state reasons for saying no with confidence.
* don’t get into an argument.
* remember, the goal is to refuse what is being offered.

 Ways to say  no:
* Say, "No, thanks."
* Be a broken record.
   * Keep saying no as many times as needed, either to cause the person pressuring them to stop, or to stall and think of something else to say.
* Give a reason.
   * This reason could be simply, "I'm not allowed to do that," or "That's bad for you."
* State possible consequences
   * "I don't want to do that; it will make me sick,"
* Walk away or ignore the offer.
* Change the subject or suggest doing something else
   * "Let's do ____ instead,"
* Assert yourself.

One of the best methods to avoid drugs, alcohol, and tobacco is to spend time with people who don't use these substances.

5.ANCOD.3.2 Create a plan for maintaining a drug-free lifestyle that includes challenges to the plan and ways of overcoming the challenges.
Verb Defined:
To produce through imaginative skill : to make or bring into existence something new
Student will know and be able to:
A plan for maintaining a drug-free lifestyle should include:

* Set Clear Goals
   * Establish a SMART goal for remaining drug-free
   * Challenges
      * Goals are not specific or measurable
   * Overcoming challenge
      * Check goal progress
      * Edit goals as necessary or needed

* Develop a Support System
   * Develop relationships with those who are able to provide support
   * Challenges
      * Social circles don’t provide support
   * Overcoming challenges
      * Establish a new social circle

* Develop Healthy Coping Mechanisms
   * Manage stress and other difficult situations without turning to drugs
   * Challenges
      * Using drugs to cope with difficult situations
   * Overcoming challenges
      * Develop healthy mechanisms
         * physical activity
         * painting
         * hobbies
         * breathing exercises

* Develop a healthy routine
   * Develop healthy habits to remain drug-free
   * Challenges
      * returning to old habits
   * Overcoming challenges
      * prioritize healthy habits
      * create a daily schedule to follow
