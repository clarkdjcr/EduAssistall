# Arts Education - Advanced

Parent Guide for Advanced Vocal & Instrumental Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for vocal and instrumental music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with vocal and instrumental music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed music educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how music has expanded across the globe, and how its creation and performance are relevant in all career fields. They also explore the best strategies to stay healthy as musicians.
CN.2 Explore - Students identify what it takes to pursue a specific career in music after high school and explore how musical innovations are expanding opportunities.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise accompanied melodies and notate original musical ideas with accompaniments.
CR.2 Adapt - Students arrange an existing melody for 3-4 complex parts and explore how a musician gets permission to use another musician’s work.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve their technique while reading rhythmic and pitch notation to sing or play complex repertoire with correct tempo, forms, dynamics, textures, articulations, phrasing, and ensemble awareness.
PR.2 Develop - Students produce a musical performance, choosing musical selections, designing the experience, and setting up the production equipment needed. They model active listening and appropriate etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students analyze what makes songs engaging while exploring how effectively a musician conveys their intent.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students defend their personal music preferences within historical and social contexts while being able to critique musical performances, compositions, arrangements, and improvisations objectively.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Provide time and space for your student to practice at home
* Encourage your student to perform for you, family members, or stuffed animals
* Listen to music together from any genre or culture, noticing what makes it unique
* Share your favorite songs and ask what your student enjoys about theirs
* Point out when musicians, writers, artists, and designers have borrowed from another artist, discussing how they had to ask permission
* Attend school concerts or community performances when possible
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Encourage students to improvise, write, or record their own musical ideas
