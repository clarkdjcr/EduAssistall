# Arts Education - Beginning

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Technical Theatre Standards Unpacking Beginning Proficiency Level
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Students at the high school level will have the option of studying an individual arts discipline as an area of interest, or specializing or completing a concentration in studies to prepare them for further education and/or a career in the arts. The Standards communicate what students should know and be able to do as a result of instruction at each proficiency level: beginning and accomplished (9-12). Beginning High School Technical Theatre standards are designed for students with no or limited K-8 progression in technical theatre education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed music educator. Credit earned from middle and high school courses meeting these minimums satisfies the Arts Education Graduation requirement and applies toward the Arts Education Diploma Endorsement. Courses that do not meet these minimums are not designed to build proficiency and are often used to spark interest in more intensive proficiency-based study later in a student’s education.
The North Carolina Theatre Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 theatre programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

B.CN.1.1 Describe the historical development of theatre technology.
Glossary
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Historical Period - The historical period focuses on when the dance was made. The relevant developments in that era may influence the dance.11
* Technology - The mechanical aspects and contexts of arts production, including hardware, software, networks, code, etc.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective begins the EQ-throughline at the foundational level for high school aged students and is for those who have not had previous education in technical theatre. This objective develops the EQ-throughline because students are describing the historical development of theatre technology. In the next level they will analyze how historical technical theatre practices have been used and adapted throughout various time periods.
Enduring Understanding
A theatre artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of environments, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* What theatre technology is
* How theatre technology has developed over time
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Using both theatrical and non-theatrical vocabulary in written or verbal descriptions
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Humans have always desired to create environments and relate ideas through the use of technology and design
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare curated examples of theatre technologies from major historical periods and provide accessible context for their development
* Demonstrate how theatre technologies evolved over time by comparing design innovations across eras using images, diagrams, or models
* Guide students in identifying key features and functions of theatre technologies from different time periods and how they addressed the needs of their era
* Provide opportunities for students to describe technological developments using accurate disciplinary and academic vocabulary in writing or discussion
* Monitor individual students’ ability to describe the historical development of theatre technology through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
* Greek Theatre technology (Machina, Ekkyklema, Periaktoi, Anapiesma)
* Roman Theatre technology (Scaena frons, Velarium, Aulaeum)
* Medieval Theatre technology (Pageant Wagons, Hell’s mouth effects)
* Italian Renaissance Theatre technology (Proscenium Arch, Pole and Chariot System)
* 20th Century Stagecraft (Platforms, Wagons)
* 21st Century Stage Machinery (Hydraulics, Air Casters)
* Theatre Lighting technology (Oil lamps and reflectors,  Incandescent lighting and control, LED lighting)

   * Ex:  Set/Props — (1) Actor runs into flat, flat fall mid-scene (2) Rainstorm the night before causes flood onstage (3) Telephone is placed too far from actor during scene
   * Ex: House Management – (1) Digital ticketing system stops working  (2) Audience member gets sick during performance (3) House lights not dimming as planned/scheduled.

      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
      * Since this objective has the language “aesthetic and practical” both choices must be analyzed
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
      * Prepare examples of technical theatre works highlighting both aesthetic and practical (reasonable, economical, or obtainable) choices

      * Guide students in examining and discussing how aesthetic and practical decisions shape the meaning and feasibility of theatrical designs

B.RE.2.1 Explain how personal preferences impact the ability to view theatrical works objectively.
Glossary
      * Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
      * Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences.¹⁷
      * Works - Something produced by the exercise of creative talent or expenditure of creative effort: artistic production.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because students are explaining how personal preferences impact the ability to view theatrical works objectively. In the next level, they will describe how previously experienced work can impact personal preference.
Enduring Understanding
An observer's personal experiences shape the way they perceive and engage with theatre works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
      * The effect of bias on making observations objectively as a technical theatre artist
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
      * Describing the reasons why personal preferences can impede the ability to objectively  view theatrical works
      * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
      * Theatrical works come in many forms, including size, scale, genre, style, and budget. Technical theatre artists develop their own preference for certain styles, genres, and types of productions
      * Technical theatre artists need to reflect and evaluate how and why they are drawn to specific artistic and technical elements, including trends and applications they personally observed or studied
      * Theatre artists need to reflect and evaluate how and why their personal preferences for various theatre elements and types of productions impact their ability to make observations objectively versus subjectively
      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts

B.RE.2.2 Critique technical choices objectively.
Glossary
      * Critique - Individual or collective reflective process by which artists or designers experience, analyze, and evaluate a work of art or design.¹⁷
      * Technical Elements - Include the set, lighting, sound, costumes, properties, and hair and makeup.²⁹
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because students are critiquing technical choices objectively. In the next level, they will provide constructive feedback offering justifications for suggested improvements in terms of utility and effectiveness.
Enduring Understanding
Providing feedback through objective critique is imperative in improving artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
      * Objective criteria
Skills

      * Analyzing and evaluating a work for the quality of technical choices

      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
