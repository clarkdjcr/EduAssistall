# Arts Education - Second Grade

Parent Guide for Second Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn about American theatre and identify how theatre is connected to other school subjects.
CN.2 Explore - Students compare the roles and tools of actors, directors, and designers.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise characters, create original scenes with dialogue, and make costumes, props, or masks for their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students adapt a familiar story into a theatrical presentation. They will also learn how to share both physical and digital copies of theatre fairly.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use directorial feedback to refine their movements and use nonverbal elements to communicate characters.
PR.2 Develop - Students choose a scene for presentation, examine how a performance space affects appropriate audience behavior, use costumes, props, or masks in their presentations, and adapt their theatrical choices to fit a performance space.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify character traits and explore how character traits were shown through artistic choices.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students use theatrical vocabulary to describe what they prefer in theatre before giving both positive and constructive feedback to themselves about their artistic choices.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the American theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing what they liked, and encouraging the use of theatrical vocabulary
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the role of the director, actor, and designers (costume, set, lighting, sound)
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.)
* Encourage your student to tell stories and create costumes, props, and masks for their stories at home out of craft supplies or blankets.
* Talk to students about why we shouldn't pirate theatre, film, movies, or other works of art
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
