# Arts Education - Kindergarten

Parent Guide for Kindergarten Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore theatre in local and global communities, as well as identify how they use theatre skills at both home and in other school subjects (like telling stories).
CN.2 Explore - Students learn the roles and tools of actors and directors.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise stories, use pre-writing activities to create original stories, and arrange set pieces for new environments.
CR.2 Adapt - Students reenact stories and examine the differences between original and copied theatrical ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students listen to their director and use both verbal and non-verbal communication to present stories.
PR.2 Develop - Students explore movement and vocal choices, practice appropriate audience behavior, imagine objects and locations to support their dramatic presentations, which happen in a variety of spaces.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify characters, settings, and the emotions they have when watching performances.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine what they like about theatre before giving positive feedback to themselves on their artistic choices.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the local and global theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing what they liked
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the role of the actors and director
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.)
* Encourage your student to tell stories and create environments for their stories at home out of craft supplies or blankets.
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Talk to students about why we shouldn't copy the work of others and call it our own
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
