# Arts Education - Beginning

Parent Guide for Beginning General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed music educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how music has evolved over time, is influenced by concepts from other content areas, and impacts mood in everyday life.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore the musical roles individuals have in creating, producing, designing, and marketing music, along with the innovations that influence and support the development, distribution, and consumption of music.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise melodies and compose musical ideas in through either music notation or by using a digital audio workstation (DAW).
CR.2 Adapt - Students explore changing musical elements in an existing piece of music and learn legal terminology related to copyright protection of other musicians’ work.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and the music they perform or produce, honing their ability to demonstrate complex rhythms, pitches, harmony, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students compare what production elements will make the best musical presentation, and define appropriate active listening and audience etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the use of complex rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of traditional, contemporary, and world music. They also examine how a musician’s intention is communicated.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how their own preferences impact their ability to be objective. They also practice objectively evaluating their own musical efforts.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to share the songs they are learning at school
* Listen together to music from different cultures, time periods, or genres, and talk about whether you like it or not - and why
* Share music that evokes a memory, story, challenge, or feeling
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Encourage students to compose and produce music at home
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
