# Arts Education - Fourth Grade

Parent Guide for Fourth Grade General Music

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore music styles and composers from North Carolina, including indigenous and other cultural groups,  as well as explore how non-musical concepts influence the creation of music and connect to emotions.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore music careers in NC and examine how music has changed and remained the same as technology has evolved.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise pentatonic melodies and notate 2-part musical ideas.
CR.2 Adapt - Students explore the connections between poetry and melodies, and they learn how to responsibly access music online.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and their performance of a variety of songs with occasional harmonization, honing their ability to demonstrate even more complex rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students compare how song choices for performance affect production needs and create criteria for appropriate performer and audience etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify and describe rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of global music. They also compare varied interpretations of music.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students compare their preferences for music with their peers before practicing giving feedback.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to sing or tell you about the songs they are learning at school
* Ask students to use Italian terms to identify musical elements in music on the radio
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, etc.
* Explore the music of various cultural groups in North Carolina
* Explore the Blueridge Music Trails and the African American Music Trails created by the NC Arts Council
* Discuss interpretations of music. Use music without words (Lo-Fi Beats, Instrumental House, Classical, Bluegrass, Film Scores, Jazz, etc.)
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
* Discuss how your family legally and ethically accesses music through radio, streaming, purchases, concerts, etc.
* Play games that encourage melodic improvisation, like singing what they learned in math
