# Arts Education - Fifth Grade

Parent Guide for Fifth Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore historical events in the United States that have influenced theatre and compare theatre skills and processes with those in other arts disciplines.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn the roles and tools of front-of-house and business staff.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise characters, and they create original works and technical designs to support their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students explore adapting a variety of genres into theatre, and they learn to correctly cite artists they borrow theatrical ideas from.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use feedback to improve their artistic choices and perform using blocking, characterization, and memorization.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop monologues or small group scenes, demonstrate audience skills, use technical elements to enhance their presentations, and demonstrate staging choices adapted to fit a performance space.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students analyze story structure and cite evidence to interpret an artist’s purpose.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students defend personal preferences and construct respectful critique of peer works.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share examples of theatrical works from the USA they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing their theatrical preferences
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the role of the front-of-house and business staff
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.)
* Encourage your student to create interesting characters, creating technical elements to support their stories at home out of craft supplies or electronics (under the guidance of adult caregivers)
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
