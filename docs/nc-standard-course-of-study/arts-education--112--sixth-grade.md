# Arts Education - Sixth Grade

Parent Guide for Sixth Grade General Music
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for music is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with music builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed music educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore music styles and composers from around the world, including indigenous and other cultural groups, as well as explore how music is connected to other disciplines. Students also explore how people are affected by music.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how music teaches habits and behaviors useful in music careers, and compare how innovations have been used in musical works.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise rhythms and record musical ideas through either music notation or by using a digital audio workstation (DAW).
CR.2 Adapt - Students explore changing musical elements in an existing piece of music and learn legal terminology related to copyright protection.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn how to receive feedback to improve compositions and the music they perform or produce, honing their ability to demonstrate rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, and phrasing.
PR.2 Develop - Students compare how production elements enhance productions and demonstrate appropriate performer and audience etiquette.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students identify the use of rhythms, pitches, forms, dynamics, textures, tempos, articulations, phrasing, and timbres of voices and instruments in a variety of traditional, contemporary, and world music. They also examine how music can be interpreted in multiple ways.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students describe what they like about particular pieces of music and use given criteria to objectively evaluate presentations, compositions, and musical ideas.

How to Support Your Music Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your music student:
* Ask students to share the songs they are learning at school
* Listen together to music from different cultures, time periods, or genres, and talk about how it makes you feel
* Talk about favorites—ask what they like about a song, and share music that’s meaningful to you
* Visit local music festivals, showcases, concerts, recitals, ballets, operas, musicals, etc.
* Encourage students to compose and produce music at home
* Ask students to share their improvisations and compositions with you
