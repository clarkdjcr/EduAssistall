# Healthful Living - Elementary School

North Carolina Standard Course of Study
Physical Education K-12

MS = Motor Skills, MC = Movement Concepts, HF = Health-Related Fitness, PR = Personal/Social Responsibility

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Physical Education.
Kindergarten
Motor Skills (MS)
Essential Standard:
K.MS.1 Demonstrate motor skills and movement patterns needed to perform a variety of physical activities.
Clarifying Objective:
PE.K.MS.1.1 Demonstrate recognizable forms of the locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide).
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Perform recognizable forms of locomotor skills that include critical elements of the mature form.
Walk
Run
Jump
Hop
Skip
Gallop
Leap
Slide
Assessment Example:
Observational checklist including critical elements of mature form for each locomotor skill (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide).

Clarifying Objective:
PE.K.MS.1.2 Demonstrate recognizable forms of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Perform recognizable forms of manipulative skills that include critical elements of the mature form.
          Throwing
          Catching
          Dribbling
          Passing
          Volleying
          Striking
          Kicking
Assessment Example:
Student conducts a thumbs up/down self-assessment of a manipulative skill performance.  (e.g., student performs a toss, bounce, catch with a large ball)

Clarifying Objective:
PE.K.MS.1.3 Demonstrate transitions between locomotor skills with recognizable forms.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Perform transitions between locomotor skills.
Examples:
Walk to Run
Walk to Gallop
Hop to Jump
Assessment Example:
Observational checklist of the ability to perform transitions.

Clarifying Objective:
PE.K.MS.1.4 Demonstrate rhythmic locomotor and non-locomotor movements to a beat.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Perform locomotor skills in time to a drum beat or music.
Perform non-locomotor skills (clap, hand tap, foot tap) to a drum beat or music.
Examples:
Marching independently to a drum beat or music.
Hand clap to the beat
Assessment Example:
Observational checklist of the ability to move feet and or body to the beat of music.

Verb Defined:
To show clearly

Successfully perform a student-created series of locomotor and/or non-locomotor movements.
