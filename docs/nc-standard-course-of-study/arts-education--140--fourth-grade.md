# Arts Education - Fourth Grade

Parent Guide for Fourth Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore theatre in North Carolina and identify how problem-solving strategies learned in theatre can be applied to other subjects.
CN.2 Explore - Students learn the roles and both traditional and innovative tools of theatre technicians.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise stories, create monologues, and explore lighting and sound options to support their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students adapt literature into theatre, and they learn how to responsibly access theatre online.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use feedback to improve their use of technical elements and explore how vocal and physical choices distinguish different characters.
PR.2 Develop - Students collaborate to develop small group scenes, model appropriate audience behavior in various performance spaces, use technical elements in their presentations, and adapt staging choices that are affected by a performance space.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students compare story elements and how themes are communicated across multiple theatrical works.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students respond to differing opinions with respect and critique peer works with nonverbal cues and theatrical vocabulary.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the North Carolina theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing their theatrical preferences
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the technical elements: lighting, costuming, special effects, etc.
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.)
* Encourage your student to write stories and monologues, creating technical elements to support their stories at home out of craft supplies or electronics under the guidance of adult caregivers.
* Discuss how your family legally and ethically accesses theatre and film through TV, streaming services, movie theatres, purchases, etc.
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
