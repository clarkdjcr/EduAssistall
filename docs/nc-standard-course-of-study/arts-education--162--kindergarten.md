# Arts Education - Kindergarten

Parent Guide for Kindergarten Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students learn how art changes over time and that it is part of everyday life—at home, at school, and in their community
CN.2 Explore - Students learn that everyone is an artist in their own way and that art can be created not only with traditional materials like crayons and paint, but also with technology and digital tools
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students learn to explore and play with different materials to create their own original art without copying or tracing
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to share materials and ideas with others and examine the differences between original and copied visual arts ideas.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students learn to use positive feedback from instructors to improve personal art and to describe how personal art is made
PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to use given criteria to make selections of art and about the various locations where works of art can be viewed.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn about the media used to make specific artworks and how symbols and themes are used to communicate meaning in art.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine personal preferences in art and learn how to identify positive feedback in self-evaluation of their work

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:

* Play “I Spy” identifying art in everyday life, such as in books, clothing, murals, or TV shows.
* Explore both crayons/paints and simple digital tools like drawing apps.
* Provide a variety of materials (paper, boxes, fabrics, paints) for play and experimentation.
* Make art together, sharing supplies and combining ideas.
* Discuss the differences between making original art and copying someone else’s ideas.
* Display their artwork at home to show that it is valued.
* Ask your child to tell you how they made their art to help them reflect on their process.
* Visit libraries, museums, or community spaces to see art on display.
* Look at art together and have them tell you about the shapes, colors, objects, and symbols..
* Talk about feelings or stories the artwork might tell.
* Look at different works of art and have your child tell you something they like.
* Practice having your child share positive feelings about their artwork.
