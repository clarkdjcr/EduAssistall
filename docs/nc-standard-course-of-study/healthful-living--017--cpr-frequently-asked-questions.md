# Healthful Living - CPR Frequently Asked Questions

CPR Graduation Requirement FAQ
1. Are students required to receive CPR certification cards?
No.
2. Are teachers required to be certified as CPR instructors?
No.
3. Are teachers required to have current CPR certification cards?
No.
4. What is the first cohort of students that will be required to meet this requirement?
The students graduating in 2015 will be the first group that must fulfill this requirement to graduate.
5. Can CPR instruction be delivered online followed by the skills test being delivered
face to face?
Online CPR instruction should only be used as a last resort makeup option for students who have not fulfilled the requirement through traditional classroom instruction.
6. If a student comes from another school system or state and they have a letter saying
they passed the CPR test would that suffice?
Yes.
7. Can the guidance which was given in regard to students completing the CPR
requirement through the successful completion of their eighth grade Healthful Living course, in which CPR was regularly taught, be applied to courses taken in higher grade levels as well?
Yes.
8. Where can I find the CPR test which students must pass for this requirement?
The test which students must successfully complete is a skills test in which they demonstrate proper chest compressions at a rate of 100 per minute and at a depth of 2 inches per compression. This test is represented as part III c of the CPR Checklist. No written test is required.
9. What can we do to solve the issue of lacking CPR training equipment?
Contact your local resources such as emergency services, hospitals, community colleges, universities, health departments, and/or other health-related agencies for assistance in meeting this legislative requirement
