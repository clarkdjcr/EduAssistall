# Arts Education - Third Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Third Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

3.CN.1.1 Compare purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies.
Glossary
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Compare - To locate and/or list similarities and differences.14
* Period - Of, relating to, or representing a particular historical period.14
* Society - An enduring and cooperating social group whose members have developed organized patterns of relationships through interaction with one another; a community, nation, or broad grouping of people having common traditions, institutions, and collective activities and interests.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States can reflect American visual arts and are now comparing the purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies. In the next level, students will make connections of visual art to North Carolina by describing how the visual arts have influenced, and been influenced by the customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups across the state.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How visual arts can serve a purpose to individuals and society
* Cultures and time periods for the study of visual arts
* How visual arts is or has been used in different cultures, time periods and societies
* How to recognize similarities and differences in the purposes of art in different time periods, cultures, and societies
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Identifying similarities and differences in the purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies.
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to compare purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, AND societies. Depending on the learning activity, the comparison of purposes of art in all three; cultures, times, period, and societies can be assessed simultaneously or broken apart to be assessed in multiple ways over the course of study
* This objective can be paired with CN.2.2 in which students compare how art has changed and remained the same with advances in technology, innovations, and digital media and with PR.2.2 in which students describe how art exhibitions add value to communities
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give a demonstration of how art is used in multiple cultures, time periods, and societies
* Provide examples of artworks from a variety of cultures, time periods, and societies for student observation
* Assist students in making comparisons in the purposes of art from different time periods, societies, and cultures
* Facilitate discussion as students compare and contrast the purpose of specific cultural artwork
* Design learning activities for students to compare similarities and differences in the purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to compare the purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Take a virtual tour of an art museum
* Use history books or other resources to observe purposes of art in a variety of cultures and time periods
* Art across cultures collage

Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

3.CN.2.1 Categorize a variety of artists and art careers.
Glossary
* Artist - A person who creates art (such as painting, sculpture, music, or writing) using conscious skill and creative imagination; a person skilled in any of the arts.14
* Career -  Profession for which one trains and which is undertaken as a permanent calling.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students listed visual arts careers that exist in the local community and are now able to categorize visual artists and professions based on similarities. In the next level, students describe visual arts careers in North Carolina.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that the roles, skills, and behaviors of a visual artist are necessary for success in both artistic and other professions.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to organize characteristics of visual artists and careers into categories
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Organizing visual artists and careers into groups with similar features
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Make sure students understand art careers extend beyond the roles of fine artists
* It is possible to have a career in a field outside of visual arts and work part-time in visual arts as well
* This objective can be paired with other CN objectives as students learn about artists and careers in connection with the purposes of art (CN.1.1), how it exists in daily life (CN.1.2) and how it has evolved (CN.2.2). It can also be paired with CR.2.1 as students could first categorize artists and then select an artist or style as inspiration for their personal work
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective. Students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to categorize a variety of artists AND art careers. Artists and careers can be categorized simultaneously in one learning assessment or assessed separately in different assignments over the course of study.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Activate students' prior knowledge of visual art careers in the local community
* Describe careers explaining types of tasks and responsibilities involved
* Introduce students to an array of visual artists of different styles and cultures
* Model categorizing artists or arts careers based on similar attributes
* Guide student learning of how to make categorical associations between different artists and art careers
* Provide opportunities for students to categorize a variety of artists and art careers
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to categorize a variety of artists and art careers
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Present images depicting various visual art careers, such as graphic designer, illustrator, fine artist, animator, photographer, art director, interior designer, museum curator, or art teacher for students to categorize
* Present artists from different time periods, cultures, or who use different media and have students categorize the artists based on similarities
* Provide possible categorical headings and have students find examples for each appropriate category

Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights

In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

3.CR.1.1 Use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork.
Glossary
* Develop - To (cause something to) grow or change into a more advanced, larger, or stronger form; to invent something or bring something into existence.4
* Generate - to create by means of a defined process : produce.14
* Ideas - An entity (such as a thought, concept, sensation, or image) actually or potentially present to consciousness.14
* Personal - Unique experiences and relationships that surround a single person and are influenced by personal life, family, habits, interest, and/or preferences.17
* Use - To put into action or service.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students experimented with creative solutions to challenges to create art and now use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork. In the next level, students will develop imaginative and expressive ideas using imagery from North Carolina as source material to create art.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of visual artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to use imaginative and expressive processes in the creation of art
* How to use media, materials, and tools safely and appropriately
* The creative design process - generate ideas, plan solutions, develop art
* The elements of Art
* The principles of design
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Putting imaginative and expressive processes into practice in the generation of ideas, planning of solutions, and development of personal artwork
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* Utilizing imaginative and expressive processes also include technical processes. Students may require assistance with safely and responsibly creating with specific art materials.
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
* Note the conjunctions “and” used twice in this objective - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to use imaginative AND expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, AND develop personal artwork. Imaginative and expressive processes could be used simultaneously in the generation of ideas, planned solutions, and development of personal work or could be assessed independently in multiple art-making lessons. The creative design process should include the student generating ideas, planning solutions, and developing personal work.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Demonstrate how to use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork
* Guide students in ideation and creation processes
* Provide clear expectations in the appropriate and safe use of tools and materials
* Provide learning activities in which students can use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Use creative brainstorming techniques to assist with imaginary ideation
* Have students create original art in which they must use imaginary and expressive processes throughout the design cycle
* Matisse Collage, Picasso portraits, still life drawings or paintings, watercolor resist projects, origami, 3D found object sculptures, Marc Chagall dreamscapes

3.PR.1.1 Improve composition and art skills based on constructive feedback from instructors, peers, and self.
Glossary
* Composition - The organization of the elements of art and principles of design in creating a work of art.2
* Feedback - A reaction or response to a particular design problem or design solution.29
* Improve - to enhance in value or quality : make better.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students incorporated constructive feedback from teachers, peers, and self to improve personal artwork and now improve composition and art skills based on constructive feedback from instructors, peers, and self. In the next level, students will apply changes to personal art based on self-evaluation.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that visual artists improve by incorporating feedback into their artistic practice.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How to glean feedback for improvement on composition and art skills from self-evaluation
* How to apply feedback from self and others to refine compositions and art skills
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Enhancing composition and art skills by applying feedback from instructors, peers, and self
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* This objective is about APPLYING feedback and should be paired with the RE.2.2 objective throughline about GIVING feedback.
* This objective can be paired with CR & RE objectives as students create original artwork, present work created to peers or teachers, learn how to self-evaluate their work and give and receive constructive feedback, and then make revision of the composition and art skills used in the work
* Students may be able to identify areas for improvement in self-evaluation but might not always know how to make improvements
* The previous level was constructive criticism more holistically about a work but now takes a deeper examination of the student’s composition and art skills

* Provide an opportunity for students to show how constructive feedback from self-evaluation,  teachers and peers was used to improve their composition and art skills

* Take photos of before and after improvement student work
