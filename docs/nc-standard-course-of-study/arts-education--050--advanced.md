# Arts Education - Advanced

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Theatre Arts Standards Unpacking Advanced Proficiency Level
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Students at the high school level will have the option of studying an individual arts discipline as an area of interest, or specializing or completing a concentration in studies to prepare them for further education and/or a career in the arts. The Standards communicate what students should know and be able to do as a result of instruction at each proficiency level: beginning, intermediate, accomplished, and advanced (9-12). Advanced High School Theatre Arts standards are designed for those students who have achieved accomplished level high school standards for Theatre Arts education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed music educator. Credit earned from middle and high school courses meeting these minimums satisfies the Arts Education Graduation requirement and applies toward the Arts Education Diploma Endorsement. Courses that do not meet these minimums are not designed to build proficiency and are often used to spark interest in more intensive proficiency-based study later in a student’s education.
The North Carolina Theatre Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 theatre programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

AD.CN.1.1 Analyze the impact of theatre throughout the world from the 1900s to the present on trends, styles, and artists, and their future implications.
Glossary
* Analyze - Identifying and examining separate parts as they function independently and together in creative works and study of [the] arts.22
* Artists - A person who creates art (such as painting, sculpture, music, or writing) using conscious skill and creative imagination; a person skilled in any of the arts.14
* Styles - Specific movement characteristics, qualities, or principles that give it distinctive identity; style may also refer to the unique artistic choices of a particular [artist].17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students analyzed the development of theatre from the 1800s through the 1900s, identifying connections to history, society, and culture. Now they are analyzing the impact of theatre throughout the world from the 1900s to the present on trends, styles, and artists, and their future implications.
Enduring Understanding
A theatre artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of stories, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How theatre developed from 1900s-present
* Examples of styles that developed theatre from 1900s-present
* Examples of artists that developed theatre from 1900s-present
* Examples of trends that developed theatre from 1900s-present
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Drawing conclusions based on the identification and examination of the development of theatre between 1800-1900s
* Forecasting based on projections of current trends, styles, and artists
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
* Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As society has developed theatre has developed including reacting to and influencing changing dynamics of societies and cultures throughout history.
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts.
* Since this objective has the language “trends, styles, and artists” all three must be analyzed in relation to both the past and for future implications.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare examples of global theatrical movements, key artists, and defining trends from the 20th century to today, including postmodernism, devised theatre, and digital performance.
* Demonstrate an analysis of a contemporary work (e.g., immersive theatre, Lin-Manuel Miranda’s Hamilton, or global devised ensembles) to trace its stylistic lineage and cultural influence.
* Guide student inquiry projects that connect past and current theatrical innovations to cultural, technological, and artistic shifts across the globe.
* Provide tools like comparison matrices, futurecasting prompts, and graphic organizers to support analysis of historical influence and prediction of future directions.
* Monitor students’ ability to articulate clear connections between global theatre developments and emerging artistic trends, using accurate disciplinary language through formative and summative assessment, offering feedback as needed.

AD.CR.1.3 Implement original technical designs reflecting various time periods or cultures incorporating available facilities, technology, and tools.
Glossary
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Implement - To put a plan or system into operation.4
* Original - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14
* Technical (theatre) - The elements of spectacle such as sets, sound, costume, lights, music, props, and makeup used to create a unified and meaningful design for a theatrical production.17
* Technology - The mechanical aspects and contexts of arts production, including hardware, software, networks, code, etc.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students implemented original technical designs reflecting specific situations, styles, or genres incorporating available facilities, technology, and tools. Now they are implementing original technical designs reflecting various time periods or cultures incorporating available facilities, technology, and tools.
Enduring Understanding
Unique ideas are actualized by theatre designers and craftspersons intentionally to fulfill dramatic purposes.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Considerations for creating original technical designs
* How to read and carry out technical designs
* Characteristics of various time periods
* Characteristics of various cultures
* Considerations for using facilities, technology, and tools to enhance works
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Creating original technical designs for implementation that reflect various time periods or cultures while incorporating available facilities, technology and tools
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Technical design is a creative process grounded in intentional choices that support, enhance, and maintain a theatrical production. These choices must present time periods and cultural elements historically, respectfully, artistically, and with sensitivity in keeping with the dynamics of associated productions in including style and genre
* While design is imaginative, it must also be practical, safe, and executable. Effective design requires balancing artistic intent with the realities of space, budget, materials, and safety procedures, ensuring that concepts can be implemented successfully and responsibly
* Technical theatre artists must be creative, innovative, and skillful at implementing designs and applications using resources available to them while meeting overall production goals.
* While many theatre technicians design and implement simultaneously in real-world settings, the CR.1.3 objectives are specifically focused on the act of creating original technical designs. Instruction should center on generating design concepts—costumes, props, lighting, sound, or sets—that reflect student ideas, creative choices, and artistic intent
* Though implementation is often part of the design process, the performance strand (PR.2.3) is where students should focus on bringing designs to life—adapting, executing, and problem-solving within real-world constraints. These two throughlines are meant to work in tandem, but CR.1.3 is about the creation of technical ideas, while PR.2.3 is about the realization of those ideas onstage
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare research resources and design planning materials that support students in exploring time periods or cultural contexts relevant to their production. Begin by auditing the available facilities, tools, and technology, noting how these will impact historically or culturally informed design work
* Determine which students will work on which technical elements, and assign or let students choose specific time periods or cultural lenses to guide their design
* Demonstrate how to research and compile lists of design considerations unique to a specific time period or culture. Show how cultural and historical accuracy must be adapted to work within the boundaries of the available tools and space
* Guide students in creating original technical designs that reflect a specific time period or culture, incorporating their research and adjusting their plans to fit the actual performance context and available resources
* Provide research scaffolds and planning prompts to help students ensure cultural/historical authenticity, and encourage reflection on how their design choices balance historical intent with the realities of contemporary production constraints
* Monitor students’ ability to design for the implementation of technical elements through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
* Technical categories of theatre: costumes, make up, props, scenery, lighting, sound, media, etc.

Glossary
      * Adapt - To change, or to change something, to suit different conditions or uses.⁴

Performance spaces influence how theatre artists adapt their work.

Skills

Key Insights
      * Theatrical works are presented through the words and actions of performers in relation to an audience in a designated performance space.  Performance space and configuration will impact how words and actions are presented on stage. Directors and actors must consider how a performance space impacts staging and adapt choices to achieve a successful production
      * Since this objective has the language “staging and direction” students must adapt both

AD.RE.1.1 Analyze aesthetics in various settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences in written and presented theatrical works.
Glossary
      * Aesthetics - Artistic choices which give meaning and clarity to a production. 29
      * Analyze - Identifying and examining separate parts as they function independently and together in creative works and study of the arts. 22
      * Audience - A reading, viewing, or listening public. 14
      * Works - Something produced by the exercise of creative talent or expenditure of creative effort: artistic production.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students analyzed the impact of historical and cultural context on written and presented theatrical works. Now they are analyzing aesthetics in various settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences in written and presented theatrical works.
Enduring Understanding
The techniques and elements in a theatre work can be objectively identified and analyzed.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
      * Aesthetics in theatre refers to the visual, auditory, and stylistic qualities that shape the artistic experience (e.g., design elements, directorial vision, tone, symbolism, mood).
      * Aesthetic choices are influenced by setting (venue, environment), production situation (budget, resources, format), time period (historical style or sensibility), and audience (age, culture, expectations)
      * Aesthetic choices change based on time period, location, or target audience
      * Aesthetic decisions can support or challenge the meaning and emotional impact of a theatrical work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
      * Identifying and examining how aesthetic choices impact settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences
      * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
      * The term “settings” in this objective refers to physical space—such as venue or performance area—rather than narrative setting. This marks a shift from its earlier use to mean the story’s time and place, and may require clarification to avoid confusion
      * Theatrical works, both written and performed, are products of the time, place, and societies in which they are created. Various settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences, including their personal and shared experiences, impact how and why theatrical works are developed, including aesthetic choices
      * Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts
      * Since this objective has the language “written and presented” both types must be considered
      * Since this objective has the language “settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences” any types may be considered
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
      * Prepare scenarios of various settings, production situations, time periods, or audiences that require students to analyze aesthetic choices and consider how context shapes those decisions
      * Demonstrate how to identify the impacts of specific aesthetic choices on a particular setting, time period, or audience and model how different choices would result in different effects or meanings
      * Guide exploration of a real production’s aesthetic choices, identifying how they were influenced or limited by the setting, production situation, time period, or audience, citing evidence to support conclusions
      * Provide examples of written and presented theatrical works for students to analyze aesthetics and require them to support their inferences about why choices were made using evidence from the work
      * Maintain established rules and procedures for appropriate, supportive, respectful, and reflective exploration of theatre development as it relates to society and history
      * Monitor individual students' ability to analyze aesthetics in various settings, production situations, time period, or audiences in written and presented theatrical works through formative and summative assessment

      * Pop culture and trends
