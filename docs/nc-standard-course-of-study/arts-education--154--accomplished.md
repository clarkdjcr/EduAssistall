# Arts Education - Accomplished

Parent Guide for Accomplished Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the art form of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed theatre arts educator.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 Music and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students see how theatre connects to history, society, and culture from the 1800s to the 1900s and teaches skills, characteristics, and behaviors useful in all careers.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore influential professional theatre artists and the contributions they made to the field. They also learn how technical elements enhance theatrical productions.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students create both improvised and scripted extended scenes and design original technical elements based on specific parameters.
CR.2 Adapt - Students arrange primary materials into theatre works and also explore how parody, adaptation, and artificial intelligence relate to copyright protection.

Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students integrate feedback to improve and show how characters progress through performances.
PR.2 Develop - Students develop scenes for specific audiences and purposes, design audience engagement procedures, design technical elements to support specific audiences, spaces, and budgets, and adapt blocking for a variety of venues.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students explore how historical and cultural context impacts theatre, and how an audience’s experiences impact how those works are interpreted.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how current theatrical trends impact personal preferences before creating objective critique and constructive criticism of performances.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods (especially those 1800-1999 AD), or genres
* Talk about the impact of history, culture, and professional theatre trends on the work
* Encourage your student to write stories, designs, and create props, costumes, or sets at home and share their ideas with you
* Watch parodies, discussing the original source and how it has been changed
* Attend school or community performances when possible
* Celebrate their creativity, problem-solving, and efforts, even for small achievements
