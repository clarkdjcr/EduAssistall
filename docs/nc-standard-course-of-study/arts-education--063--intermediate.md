# Arts Education - Intermediate

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Intermediate
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a students’ choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator. Credit earned from middle and high school courses meeting these minimums satisfies the Arts Education Graduation requirement and applies toward the Arts Education Diploma Endorsement. Courses that do not meet these minimums are not designed to build proficiency and are often used to spark interest in more intensive proficiency-based study later in a student’s education.
To account for variations in skill progression, as well as to embed multiple entry points across the K-12 spectrum, the objectives in the high school NCSCOS for Visual Arts are organized by proficiency level, rather than grade level. Minimum proficiency outcomes are provided at each level to show what a student should know and be able to do by the end of a course or program when they create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts. Intermediate High School Visual Arts standards are designed for those students who have had a complete K-8 progression in Visual Arts education or who have achieved beginning level high school standards and objectives.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

I.CN.1.1 Explain the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts.
Glossary
* Cultural - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Society - An enduring and cooperating social group whose members have developed organized patterns of relationships through interaction with one another; a community, nation, or broad grouping of people having common traditions, institutions, and collective activities and interests.14
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because students may be entering this level from either 8th grade where they explained why and how the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina or beginning where they described how the emergence of visual arts as an art form has influenced society. At the current level, students explain the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts. In the next level, students describe how society, culture, and politics are reflected in visual arts.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How art is reflective of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives
* How local and global historical perspectives in a given time, place, or period has impacted the work of individual visual artists or groups
* How to draw personal connections, meaning, and interpretations from the research of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives in writing, discussion, or in art forms
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the reasoning behind the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to explain the impact of historical, societal, AND cultural perspectives on the visual arts. Depending on the learning activity, all three: historical, societal, and cultural perspectives can be assessed in simultaneously or this objective can be broken apart and covered in multiple assessments over the course of study
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Give examples of how historical, societal, and cultural perspectives impact visual art
* Guide student investigation in the ways historical, societal, and cultural perspectives have influenced visual art
* Lead classroom discussions on the impact historical, societal, and cultural perspectives have had in visual arts
* Provide learning activities in which students can explain the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain the impact of historical, societal, and cultural perspectives on the visual arts
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Create a visual arts time capsule and have students include explanations of the impact history, society, or cultural perspectives.
* Assign students to research various historical periods, movements, or cultures and curate artwork that reflects the impact relevant perspectives had on the works
* Gallery walk and debate of various works that exemplify the impact of historical, cultural, and societal perspectives on the artistic creations

At the current level, students now describe how various creative design processes are used in college and careers. In the next level, students will describe applications of visual arts skills in a variety of workplace, college, and career pursuits.

* The stages of the creative design process
* How the creative design process is used in to meet various college and career demands
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of the use of the creative design process to find solutions in college and career tasks
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* The creative design process is needed in various collegiate and career situations including in fields outside of visual arts
* Use the definition to assist students in identifying the skills and processes used in the creative design process
* This objective teaches students of the value and importance of the transferable skills they learn in the arts
* College and careers do not need to be specific to visual arts
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective. Students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how various creative design processes are used in college AND careers. Depending on the learning assessment how the creative design processes are used in both, college and career can be assessed simultaneously or each one independently in multiple assignments over the course of study
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Guide student discussion on creative design processes used in art
* Have students reflect on when they have used the creative design process in art and other contexts
* Brainstorm with students on possible college and career experiences in which the application of various creative design processes would be beneficial for success
* Provide learning activities in which students can describe how various creative design processes are used in college and careers
* Create formative and summative assessment on the student’s ability to describe how various creative design processes are used in college and careers
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Bring alumni together for an in person or virtual presentation on how they are using design processes in collegiate and career opportunities
* Invite parents or community guests to come speak to the class about their careers and how they use creative design processes
* Have students interview an individual and create a presentation that describes how the creative design processes were useful in that individual’s collegiate and career opportunities

   * Innovative - Imagining and conceiving something new and unexpected, including fresh ideas and ways of looking at things and new approaches to old problems as well as formulating new problems.17
   * Original Art - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14

Knowledge

   * How to generate innovative solutions to artistic problems
   * How to create art based on personal experience
   * The meaning of applied design

   * The creative design process
   * The elements of art
   * The principles of design
   * How to use tools and materials safely & responsibly

   * Creating or producing novel solutions to generate original art based on personal expression and applied design
Evidence of Mastery

Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.

The assessment could show how a student used innovative solutions in the creation of an original work that exemplifies both personal expression and applied design or the use of innovative solutions could be assessed separately in the creation of work based on personal expression and in the creation of work based on applied design in separate learning activities

   * Guide student ideation and creation of art
   * Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to generate innovative solutions to create original art based on personal expression and applied design

I.CR.2.2 Evaluate the ethics of making, producing, and distributing both original and derivative art.
Glossary
   * Derivative - Having parts that originate from another source.14
   * Ethical - Moral guidelines and philosophical principles for determining appropriate behavior.17
   * Evaluate - A judgment about the worth or quality of something. In education, data from tests, tasks, or performances are used to make judgments about the success of the student or program.28
   * Original - Not secondary, derivative, or imitative; being the first instance or source from which a copy, reproduction, or translation is or can be made.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because students may be entering this level from either 8th grade where they explained how to share works of art legally and ethically in both physical and digital spaces or beginning where they compared artistic adaptation, appropriation, fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons in the visual arts. At the current level, students evaluate the ethics of making, producing, and distributing both original and derivative art.
