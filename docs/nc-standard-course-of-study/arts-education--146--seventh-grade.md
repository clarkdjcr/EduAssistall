# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

Parent Guide for Seventh Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore world theatre after 1400 and explain how theatre skills and processes are related to concepts and ideas from other subjects.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how theatre teaches skills and behaviors useful in all post-secondary pursuits, and analyze the tools used in creative, technical, and business careers in theatre.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise characters and situations to develop works, create works with specific dramatic structures, and explore building props and set pieces for their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt - Students adapt stories in a variety of genres and styles while they explore both legal and ethical issues around creating work inspired by other artists.
________________
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use objective feedback to improve their scripted and devised works and perform characters and stories using body, voice, observation, and concentration.
PR.2 Develop - Students explore dramatic styles, use technical elements to support the style of their presentations, and explore how the expectations of both performers and live audiences change in various performance venues.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students compare dramatic elements and the creative choices made across multiple theatrical works.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how personal preferences are affected by social and cultural background and engage in feedback cycles during collaborative work.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the post - 1400 AD theatre they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing their theatrical preferences and the creative choices made by the artists
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres
* Talk about the role of the creative, technical, and business personnel
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.) and various performance types (i.e., interactive shows, musicals, plays, etc.)
* Encourage your student to create interesting characters, creating costumes and masks to support their stories at home out of craft supplies.
* Discuss how the skills they practice in theatre class can be leveraged in all post-secondary pursuits
* Point out when actors, writers, artists, and designers have borrowed from another artist, discussing how they had to ask permission
* Attend school or community performances when possible
