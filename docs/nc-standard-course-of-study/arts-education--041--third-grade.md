# Arts Education - Third Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Theatre Arts Standards Unpacking Third Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 theatre standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed theatre educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Theatre Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 theatre programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

3.CN.1.1 Describe theatre in the local community or region.
Glossary
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Community - A unified body of individuals: such as a group of people with a common characteristic or interest living together within a larger society; a body of persons of common and especially professional interests scattered through a larger society.14
* Region - Relating to or coming from a particular part of a country, such as a regional accent/dialect/newspaper.4
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how American theatre reflects heritage, customs, and traditions of people in the United States, including various indigenous and cultural groups and now they are describing theatre in the local community or region. In the next level they will describe diverse North Carolina theatrical groups by geographic region.
Enduring Understanding
A theatre artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of stories, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Who is in the local community or region
* A variety of local theatrical styles
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of local theatre
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
* Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Local theatre contributes to the cultural life of a community by offering meaningful artistic experiences that broaden students’ understanding of the world around them.
* Community theatre provides space for a wide range of cultural expressions, allowing students to see and hear stories from different perspectives and backgrounds.
* Students may encounter unique or underrepresented stories in local theatre that they might not see in mainstream media or their everyday lives.
* Engaging with theatre in their own community helps students develop a deeper appreciation for performance arts and their role in society.
* Ensuring that students are using disciplinary language is important for building proficiency in the theatre arts.
* This objective calls for “community or region” so either can be covered.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Prepare examples of local or regional theatre and identify key vocabulary students will need to understand.
* Demonstrate how to describe local theatre opportunities, highlighting its cultural and community relevance.
* Guide a discussion that helps students connect theatre to their own community experiences and observations.
* Provide resources, sentence starters, and structured tools to support students in describing theatre clearly and thoughtfully. Inviting a local theatre group, community theatre, or professional to come visit would enrich the experience.
* Monitor student work and discussions to ensure they include specific, accurate details about local theatre through formative and summative assessment, offering feedback as needed.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
* Community Theatre
* Children’s Theatre
* Regional Outdoor Drama
* Historical Events (local, regional)
* Cultural Celebrations
* Educational theatre groups

3.CR.1.1 Improvise a variety of resolutions to given problems using dramatic play.
Glossary
   * Dramatic Play - Spontaneous dramatic enactment often done by children pretending or imitating while playing.21
   * Improvise - To make, invent, or arrange offhand; to make or fabricate out of what is conveniently on hand.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students improvised a variety of characters in given scenarios using dramatic play. Now they are improvising a variety of resolutions to given problems using dramatic play. In the next level, they will improvise comprehensive stories with support or coaching.
Enduring Understanding
Exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of theatre artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * Instructions for dramatic play
   * Components of resolutions
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Spontaneously inventing resolutions to dramatic problems
Evidence of Mastery
   * Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Dramatic play encourages the use of expressive language and introduces new vocabulary, which can help develop vital communication skills.
   * Dramatic play can build empathy and a better understanding of different perspectives.
   * Dramatic play enhances creativity and imagination as they create stories and narratives.
   * Dramatic play provides opportunities to explore scenarios and find solutions to a variety of real-world problems.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare new or familiar scenarios that require problem-solving.
   * Demonstrate how to think on your feet by modeling a variety of character-driven responses to a given dramatic problem
   * Guide students in exploring how a range of choices can lead to a variety of outcomes, or next-steps in an improvised activity
   * Provide tools such as problem cards, story maps, and reflection questions to guide reflection on improvisation and evaluating resolutions
   * Monitor each students’ ability to spontaneously create and act out resolutions within a scenario through formative and summative assessment, collecting individual evidence of mastery through observation and documentation.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Improvise solutions to different situations (Ex. The dog ate your homework - What will you tell the teacher? and Two students want to play on the same swing --- How will you decide who gets to swing?)
   * Use silly, serious, or absurd solutions to solve problems

3.CR.1.2 Create a cohesive scene using elements of playwriting with support.
Glossary
   * Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
   * Elements of Playwriting - Characters, setting, plot, conflict, and the resolution. Dramatic structure.29
   * Support - Assistance provided temporarily to enable a student to perform a task that would be difficult to perform unaided, best implemented in a manner that helps develop that student’s capacity to eventually perform the task independently.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students created an original scene using dialogue with support. Now they are creating a cohesive scene using elements of playwriting with support. In the next level, they will create a monologue using elements of playwriting with support.
Enduring Understanding
Unique ideas are recorded by theatre artists and playwrights through both written and visual documentation.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * The elements of playwriting (Plot, theme, setting, characters, dialogue, style, shape, length)
   * The components of cohesive scenes
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Putting the elements of playwriting into practice when creating a new scene
Evidence of Mastery
   * Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Creating cohesive scenes will build clear purposes, conflicts, and character development.
   * Appropriately applying elements of playwriting will create scenes that move the plot forward, establish relationships, and reveal characters.
   * Effectively using principles and techniques of playwriting will create well-structured, engaging, and believable scenes.
   * Playwriting draws heavily on language arts fundamentals.
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare examples of cohesive scenes which highlight playwriting elements such as character, setting, plot, conflict, and resolution.
   * Activate prior knowledge with students about what makes a cohesive scene
   * Demonstrate how to use playwriting elements in the creation of a short scene
   * Guide students to brainstorm and outline a cohesive scene using playwriting elements in a cohesive way
   * Provide graphic organizers, sentence frames, and checklists to help students include all required playwriting elements in their scene development.
   * Monitor each student’s ability to put the elements of playwriting into practice when conceiving a new scene through formative and summative assessment, collecting individual evidence of mastery through observation and documentation.
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Story Cards
   * Groups work on creating cohesive text
   * Charts, graphic organizers to create ideas for scenes

3.CR.1.3 Create simple set design, costume design, or prop design to support the story of a play.
Glossary
   * Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
   * Costumes - Clothing worn by actors to portray specific characters.29
   * Props - Anything that the actor uses on stage that is not scenery (i.e., a coffee cup or a cell phone).29
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students created simple costumes, props, or masks to enhance a dramatic presentation. Now they are creating simple set design, costume design, or prop design to support the story of a play. In the next level, they will create simple lighting or sound options to support theatrical work.
Enduring Understanding
Unique ideas are actualized by theatre designers and craftspersons intentionally to fulfill dramatic purposes.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * Components of simple costumes, props, and set design
   * Design considerations for supporting the play story
   * The basics of creating the physical environment of the play because scenic design helps establish the production's setting, time period, and atmosphere
   * Costumes convey character, era, and social status is important for students because it enhances their ability to analyze and interpret narratives, understand historical context, and develop critical thinking skills
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   *  Creating new sets, costumes, or props
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Costumes, props, and masks help students connect with the characters they are portraying. By creating their own costumes, props, or masks, students learn how these items reflect personality, role, social status, and story and enhance the dramatic presentation of each
   * This objective will be most meaningful when supporting improvisations (CR.1.1), original creation (CR.1.2), or as part of presenting works (PR.1.2). This objective has students creating the technical elements used in PR.2.3
   * Since this objective has the language “set design, costume design, or prop design” any may be created to master this objective
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare materials for set, costume, or prop design. While students are not required to physically create the items they design, doing so can support the implementation of PR.2.3. Having materials available can also spark design creativity (e.g., fabric, cardboard, scarves, paint, markers, yarn). Share pictures or videos of simple costumes, props, or masks used in children's theater or well-known stories and discuss how these items help tell the story
   * Determine whether your students will all design costumes, props, or masks, or if students will be assigned to differing tasks
   * Demonstrate how to consider personality, role, social status, and story when designing a simple costume, prop, or mask, emphasizing freedom of creativity and imagination. Explain how design choices support the narrative and evoke emotion
   * Guide students in designing their own costumes, props, or masks, considering key aspects of the play that inform design choices such as setting, period, mood, themes, characters, and plot
   * Provide character cards or descriptions to help students connect with the characters they are portraying (e.g., personality, role, social status, and story)
   * Monitor individual students’ ability to design costumes, props, or masks through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:
   * Analyze script for design elements as indicated by character dialogue, setting, or stage directions
   * Adapt designs to different periods and locations
   * Apply principles to real-world objects
   * Analyze props and design
   * Sets, Costumes, Masks, and/or props for CR.1.1, CR.1.2, or CR.2.1
   * Sets, Costumes, Masks, and/or props for PR.1.2, PR.2.1, or PR.2.3

3.CR.2.1 Derive dialogue from familiar stories distinguishing unique characters.
Glossary
   * Characters - A person portrayed in a drama, novel, or other artistic piece.
   * Dialogue - A conversation between two or more characters.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level, students adapted a familiar story for dramatic presentation with support. Now they are deriving dialogue from familiar stories distinguishing unique characters. In the next level, they will adapt various pieces of literature into theatrical works.
Enduring Understanding
Theatre artists balance inspiration and originality with ethics by creatively transforming or adapting other artistic ideas into new works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * Procedures for deriving dialogue from stories
   * How dialogue distinguishes unique characters
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Taking dialogue from familiar stories for character building
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Creating dialogue distinguishes unique characters by revealing their personality, background, and relationships through what they say, how they say it, and how they interact with others
   * Using dialogue from familiar stories to create characters can be an effective way to bring characters to life by revealing their personalities, motivations, and backstories
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare familiar stories with strong, distinct characters and clear plot points. Use puppets, illustrations, or read-alouds to help students internalize character traits, voices, and motivations
   * Demonstrate how to transform narrative text into character dialogue by speaking in-role, showing how each character might express themselves differently based on personality or intent
   * Guide students in devising their own dialogue through improvisation and collaborative scene-building, encouraging them to make bold choices that reflect each character’s unique voice
   * Provide character prompts, emotion cards, or simple sentence starters to support students in sustaining in-character responses and developing original dialogue during performance
   * Monitor individual students’ ability to derive dialogue from familiar stories distinguishing unique characters through formative and summative assessment

3.CR.2.2 Identify theatre artworks that can be used by everyone fairly.
Glossary
   * Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
   * Fairness - Complying with appropriate, ethical, and equitable rules and guidelines.17
   * Use - To put into action or service.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students explained how to share theatre artworks fairly and now identify theatre artworks that can be used by everyone fairly. In the next level, students will identify how to responsibly use resources for online access of theatre artworks.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
   * That materials are freely available through public domain and Creative Commons
   * That principles of fair use can be used for copyrighted material
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
   * Recognizing, listing, or naming theatre artworks available through public domain, open source, or fair use
   * Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
   * Students should be able to answer the question, “What art is available for me to use?” to show mastery of this objective
   * Students have access to art that is in the public domain, licensed under Creative Commons, or used in ways that meet fair use guidelines
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
   * Prepare a examples of works that can be used by everyone fairly, along with a student friendly definition of public domain, Creative Commons, and fair use
   * Demonstrate determining which theatre can be used under fair use principles, public domain, or Creative Commons
   * Guide students in finding examples of theatre that can be used under fair use principles, public domain, or Creative Commons
   * Provide checklists and criteria for determining if something can be used “fairly”
   * Monitor individual students’ ability to explain how to share theatre fairly while correctly using theatrical vocabulary through formative and summative assessment
Possible Learning Activities
May include, but are not limited to:

      * Guide students in practicing respectful feedback using modeled sentence starters
      * Provide feedback practice structures such as “Two Stars and a Wish” or peer conferences
      * Monitor individual students' ability to demonstrate respect when providing feedback to peers through formative and summative assessment

      * Reflection and praise
