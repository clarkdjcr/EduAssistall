# Healthful Living - Parent Guides

Physical Education Parent Guide

Purpose:
This document is intended to support parents/guardians with understanding the major concepts in the NC Standard Course of Study (SCOS) for Physical Education and provide possible ways to partner with their student’s teacher, school, or district at home.

What is the NC Standard Course of Study?
North Carolina's Standard Course of Study (SCOS) defines content standards for each grade level and/or high school course to provide a consistent set of learning standards for every public school in North Carolina. The Physical Education Standard Course of Study (PESCOS) defines what students know and should be able to do in Physical Education by the end of a specified year or course. The PESCOS provides expectations with the end goal of preparing students to become healthy, safe, supported, engaged, and prepared for future success.

Who determines how the standards are taught?
Local public school units (PSU) possess the authority to select or create the curriculum (materials and resources) necessary for assisting students in achieving the expectations set forth by the PESCOS. The Physical Education standards and objectives are not designed to constitute the curriculum, nor do they represent the entirety of a curriculum adopted by a PSU or school. Instead, they have been formulated to serve as a framework that will direct each PSU in the curriculum development or adoption process for K-9 Physical Education. All curricula must be aligned with the North Carolina Standard Course of Study.

Healthful Living is distinctive in that it encompasses two sets of standards and curricula while being delivered within a single class. Although State Board Policy SHLT-000 Healthy Active Children Policy offers guidelines for a balanced distribution of instruction between Health and Physical Education, local PSUs have the discretion to determine the time and methods of instruction for each grade level.

Physical Education should be provided by a certified Physical Education Instructor.

Physical Education Strands & Standards
Physical Education maintains consistent strands and standards across all grade levels.
Motor Skills (MS)
Demonstrate motor skills and movement patterns needed to perform a variety of physical activities.
* Addresses demonstration of locomotor skills, manipulative skills and the transitions between them as well as rhythmic movements.
Movement Concepts (MC)
Demonstrate understanding of concepts that apply to the learning and performance of movement.
* Addresses demonstration of understanding concepts like space, pathways, speed, levels, and force, and utilizing feedback to improve motor performance.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
Demonstrate the knowledge and skills needed to achieve a health-enhancing level of physical activity and fitness.
* Addresses demonstration of knowledge and understanding of the five health-related fitness components and their associated exercises, recognizing opportunities and benefits of physical activity, and active participation in class.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
Demonstrate responsible personal and social behavior that respects self and others.
* Addresses demonstrating cooperation, following directions, accepting responsibility, identifying enjoyable activities, and practicing safe behaviors.

Physical Education Objectives

Kindergarten
Upon completing Kindergarten, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate recognizable forms of the locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide).
* Demonstrate recognizable forms of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
* Demonstrate transitions between locomotor skills with recognizable forms..
* Demonstrate rhythmic locomotor and non-locomotor movements to a beat.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Demonstrate understanding of the movement concepts of space, pathways, and speed through movement.
* Identify one or more of the critical elements for fundamental manipulative skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide).
* Use teacher feedback to improve basic motor performance.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify one or more of the five health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, or body composition) and their associated exercises.
* Identify opportunities for increased physical activity.
* Actively participate in physical education class.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate cooperation by following directions in group settings.
* Identify physical activities that are personally enjoyable.
* Demonstrate safe practices when engaging in physical activities with minimal teacher reminders.
First Grade
Upon completing First Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate emerging forms of all eight basic locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) in different pathways, levels, or directions.
* Demonstrate emerging forms of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
* Demonstrate transitions between sequential locomotor skills.
* Demonstrate a combination of rhythmic locomotor and non-locomotor movements to a beat.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Demonstrate understanding of the movement concepts of space, pathways, and speed
* through movement.
* Identify two or more of the critical elements for fundamental manipulative skills (throwing,
* catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
* Use teacher and/or peer feedback to improve basic motor performance.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify two or more of the five health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, or body composition) and their associated exercises.
* Identify benefits of being physically active.
* Actively engage in physical education class.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate cooperation by following directions and accepting responsibility in group settings.
* Identify positive feelings that result from participation in physical activity.
* Demonstrate safe practices when engaging in physical activities with minimal teacher reminders.
Second Grade
Upon completing Second Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate mature form of six or more locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) using different pathways, levels, or directions.
* Demonstrate emerging forms of a variety of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
* Demonstrate combinations of locomotor skills with emerging form.
* Perform a teacher and/or student designed rhythmic activity with correct response to simple rhythms.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Demonstrate understanding of the movement concepts of space, pathways, levels, speed, and force through combined movement skills.
* Identify three or more of the critical elements for fundamental manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking).
* Explain the value of feedback in improving motor performance.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify three or more of the five health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition) and their associated exercises.
* Identify ways to be physically active outside of physical education class.
* Actively engage in physical education class in response to instruction and practice.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate ability to work independently and cooperatively with others in a variety of class environments.
* Demonstrate understanding of physical activities that provide opportunities for self-expression.
* Demonstrate the ability to work independently and safely in Physical Education.
Third Grade
Upon completing Third Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate combinations of locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) with maturing form and smooth transitions.
* Demonstrate maturing form of a variety of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) using different pathways, levels, or directions.
* Demonstrate combinations of locomotor and manipulative skills with emerging form.
* Demonstrate rhythmic sequences with smooth transitions.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Demonstrate combinations of the movement concepts of direction, levels, force, and time with skills as directed by the teacher.
* Demonstrate understanding of the critical elements of manipulative skills through successful performance.
* Use evaluative tools to demonstrate understanding of successful skill performance.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify four or more of the five health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition) and their associated exercises.
* Demonstrate understanding of physical activity as a way to become healthier.
* Develop personal goals to improve one or more of the health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition).
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate personal and social responsibility in a variety of class environments.
* Demonstrate understanding of the positive social interactions that come when engaged with others in physical activity.
* Demonstrate independent and safe practices when engaging in various physical activities.
Fourth Grade
Upon completing Fourth Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate mature form of locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) required in a variety of physical activity settings.
* Demonstrate mature form of a variety of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) in varied physical activity settings.
* Demonstrate combinations of locomotor and manipulative skills in a variety of physical activity settings.
* Demonstrate combinations of movement patterns and/or dance steps in an original sequence.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Demonstrate understanding of manipulative skill combinations (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) in small-sided practice tasks.
* Demonstrate understanding of simple offensive and defensive strategies and tactics.
* Use evaluative tools to improve skill performance of self and others.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify each of the five health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition) and their associated exercises.
* Identify personal opportunities for physical activity outside of physical education class.
* Develop personal goals to improve two or more of the health-related fitness components (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition).
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate personal and social responsibility in independent group situations.
* Demonstrate understanding of how positive social interactions help achieve common goals when engaged with others in a variety of physical activities.
* Demonstrate independent and safe practices when working with peers and equipment in various physical activities.
Fifth Grade
Upon completing Fifth Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate mature form in locomotor skills (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) required in a variety of small-sided practice tasks.
* Demonstrate mature form of a variety of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) in small-sided practice tasks.
* Demonstrate combinations of locomotor (walk, run, jump, hop, skip, gallop, leap, slide) and manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) in small-sided practice tasks.
* Demonstrate combinations of rhythmic movement patterns and/or dance steps with smooth transitions.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Apply combinations of movement concepts with skills to improve performance (direction, levels, force, and time)
* Explain basic offensive and defensive strategies used in small-sided practice tasks.
* Demonstrate understanding of manipulative skills (throwing, catching, dribbling, passing, volleying, striking, and kicking) through successful evaluation of performance.

Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Identify differences between health-related (cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility and body composition) and skill-related fitness (power, speed, agility, coordination, balance and reaction time) components.
* Identify differences in aerobic and anaerobic activities.
* Develop personal goals to improve three or more of the health-related fitness components.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate engagement in physical activity with responsible interpersonal behavior.
* Demonstrate understanding of the social benefits gained from participating in physical activities to achieve common goals.
* Demonstrate age-appropriate safety principles independently when engaging in various physical activities.
Sixth - Eighth Grade Span
By the end of Eighth Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Demonstrate competency in specialized skills and combination skills for sports and games.
* Apply game strategies and tactics during game play.
* Demonstrate competency in specialized skills and/or combination skills for individual performance activities and/or outdoor pursuits.
* Perform various movement combinations that demonstrate an understanding of rhythm and coordination to the beat of music.
* Demonstrate correct technique for a variety of cardiorespiratory endurance, resistance training, and flexibility exercises.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Apply concepts and principles related to movement and performance in static or dynamic settings.
* Understand and apply game strategies and tactics at appropriate times and in appropriate ways.
* Demonstrate understanding of the proper form and the mechanics of various skills or sequences of movement to improve performance.

Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Describe the FITT principle and training principles (frequency, intensity, time, type, overload, progression, specificity), and how they affect physical fitness for each of the five health- related fitness components.
* Summarize the potential short- and long-term physical, social, and emotional benefits of physical activity.
* Evaluate progress towards achieving physical activity and fitness guidelines.
* Participate in a variety of moderate to vigorous activities with the goal of accumulating at least 60 minutes of physical activity per day, either during or outside of school, at least 5 days per week.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Demonstrate self-management, responsible decision making, and problem-solving skills in a physical activity setting.
* Demonstrate skills to work cooperatively within a group to establish and achieve group goals in physical activity settings.
* Apply relationship skills and strategies that promote team/group dynamics and inclusion.
* Implement the skills needed to avoid and resolve conflicts independently.
Ninth Grade
Upon completing Ninth Grade, your student will possess the skills to:
Motor Skills (MS)
* Integrate specialized skills and combination skills appropriately during live game play.
* Implement appropriate strategies and tactics based on varying game conditions.
* Demonstrate basic and advanced skills to participate successfully in lifetime individual activities.
* Design and perform various movement combinations that demonstrate an understanding of rhythm and coordination to the beat of music.
* Apply correct technique for a variety of cardiorespiratory endurance, resistance training, and flexibility exercises that can lead into lifetime activity.
Movement Concepts (MC)
* Integrate concepts and principles related to movement and performance in static or dynamic settings.
* Adapt strategies and tactics based on game conditions and situations.
* Construct a plan to improve overall performance based on mechanics of movement principles.
Health Related Fitness (HF)
* Apply FITT (frequency, intensity, time, type, overload, progression, specificity) and training principles to improve a self-selected component of health-related fitness.
* Analyze factors that affect personal health and physical activity behaviors to determine appropriate goals and strategies to improve health-related outcomes.
* Design and implement a personal physical activity program, based on current physical activity and fitness status, that applies SMART (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant and time-bound) goal setting, FITT (frequency, intensity, time, type, overload, progression, specificity), and training principles.
* Participate in targeted activities based on interests, accessibility, and personal goals to improve health-related fitness.
Personal/Social Responsibility (PR)
* Implement leadership skills to promote responsibility in self and others.
* Develop and execute group norms to establish and achieve group goals in competitive as well as cooperative physical activity settings.
* Apply relationship skills and strategies that promote team/group dynamics and inclusion.
* Model leadership skills, effective communication, problem solving, and conflict resolution to promote positive team/group dynamics.

How to Help Your Child at Home
As a parent, your involvement is crucial in enhancing your child's understanding of Physical   Education. Here are some concrete ways to actively contribute to your students learning at home. Incorporating these activities into your family routine creates a supportive environment that complements and enriches your child's physical education.

      * Recognize and reinforce the importance of Physical Education as a means to a safe, healthy and productive life.
* Encourage your student to participate in Physical Education
* Two-Way Communication:
Establish a pattern of effective communication between yourself and your student.
      * This is an important step in a child's development, building solid parent-child relationships, and fostering a sense of security and well-being. Children learn to express their needs and feelings while developing social skills. Parents who communicate well with their children are more likely to understand their child's perspective and provide appropriate support.
Establish regular communication with your students’ teacher.
      * Stay abreast of curriculum topics that are being and will be covered. Ask the teacher for clarification when needed on concepts and seek guidance on how to best support your child at home.
* Share any concerns with the Physical Education program.
      * Support the Physical Education teacher by acknowledging their expertise in the subject.
* Reinforce Physical Education skills and concepts at home.
* Encourage physical activity for the entire family.
* Practice learned skills.
* Collaborate on goals.
* Assist students in conceiving and achieving reasonable goals within Physical Education.
