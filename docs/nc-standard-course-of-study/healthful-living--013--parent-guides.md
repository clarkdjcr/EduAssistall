# Healthful Living - Parent Guides

Health Education Parent Guide

Purpose:
This document is intended to support parents/guardians with understanding the major concepts in the NC Standard Course of Study (SCOS) for Health Education and provide possible ways to partner with their student’s teacher, school, or district at home.

What is the NC Standard Course of Study?
North Carolina's Standard Course of Study (SCOS) defines content standards for each grade level and/or high school course to provide a consistent set of learning standards for every public school in North Carolina. The Health Education Standard Course of Study (HESCOS) defines what students know and should be able to do in Health Education by the end of a specified year or course. The HESCOS provides expectations with the end goal of preparing students to become healthy, safe, supported, engaged, and prepared for future success.

Who determines how the standards are taught?
Local public school units (PSU) possess the authority to select or create the curriculum (materials and resources) necessary for assisting students in achieving the expectations set forth by the HESCOS. The Health Education standards and objectives are not designed to constitute the curriculum, nor do they represent the entirety of a curriculum adopted by a PSU or school. Instead, they have been formulated to serve as a framework that will direct each PSU in the curriculum development or adoption process for K-9 Health Education. All curricula must be aligned with the North Carolina Standard Course of Study.

Healthful Living is distinctive in that it encompasses two sets of standards and curricula while being delivered within a single class. Although State Board Policy SHLT-000 Healthy Active Children Policy offers guidelines for a balanced distribution of instruction between Health and Physical Education, local PSUs have the discretion to determine the time and methods of instruction for each grade level.

By State Board policy, at the elementary level, the classroom teacher is expected to teach Health Education. Middle and High School instruction is provided by a certified Healthful Living Instructor.
Legislation and State Board Policy Concerning Health Education

GS 115C-81.25
Health Education
Comprehensive health education shall be developed and taught to students from kindergarten through ninth grade.

GS 115C-81.30
Reproductive Health and Safety Education provided by local school administrative units.
Local school administrative units have specific requirements they must include in their reproductive health and safety instruction. These requirements can be found within G.S. 115C-81.30. The 2024 Health Education standards and objectives were created to work hand-in-hand with G.S. 115C-81.30 and have been written in such a way that local school administrative units can seamlessly meet their statutory obligations.

Each local school administrative unit shall provide a reproductive health and safety
education program commencing in the seventh grade.

Health Education Strands

Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)
The area of health that encompasses both psychological and social well-being, which influences how a person thinks, acts and feels.

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)
The area of health that address efforts to develop and maintain overall health and the the information, services and products that may be used to do so.

Interpersonal and Communications and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)
The area of health that involves the exchange of information, ideas and feelings.

Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)
The area of health that deals with the intake of food, the impact it has on the body, and the benefits of physical activity to develop and maintain overall health.

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and other Drugs (ANCOD)
The area of health that delineates the effects of alcohol, cannabis, or other drugs on the health and well-being of the body.

Health Education Standards
Kindergarten
Upon completing Kindergarten, your student will possess the skills to:
Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)
* Remember the association of healthy expression of emotions, mental health, and healthy behavior.
* Identify that effort and practice lead to improved skills.
Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)
* Demonstrate age-appropriate personal behaviors that promote health and prevent disease.
* Understand necessary steps to prevent and respond to unintentional injury.
Interpersonal and Communications and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)
* Recognize healthy and effective interpersonal communication and relationships.
Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)
* Identify sources of food and water and their importance to the body.
* State the importance of physical activity for the body.
Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and other Drugs (ANCOD)
* Understand household safety procedures for products and medicines with adult supervision.

How to Help Your Child at Home
As a parent/guardian, your involvement is crucial in enhancing your child's understanding of Health Education. Here are some concrete ways to actively contribute to your students’ learning at home. Incorporating these activities into your family routine creates a supportive environment that complements and enriches your child's health education.
* Create strong two-way communication.
Establish a pattern of effective communication between yourself and your student.
      * This is an important step in a child's development, building solid parent/guardian-child relationships, and fostering a sense of security and well-being. Children learn to express their needs and feelings while developing social skills. Parents and guardians who communicate well with their children are more likely to understand their child's perspective and provide appropriate support.
Establish regular communication with your students’ teacher.
      * Stay abreast of curriculum topics that are being and will be covered. Ask the teacher for clarification when needed on health concepts and seek guidance on how to best support your child at home.
* Recognize and reinforce the importance of Health Education as a means to a safe, healthy, and productive life.
* Support the Health Education teacher by acknowledging their expertise in Health Education.
* Discuss class topics and reinforce concepts by making them a focus of daily life.
Examples :
* Assist students in making good decisions.
* Reinforce hand washing techniques before meals.
* Create healthy habits at home.
Model healthy behaviors like exercise and reducing screen time while assisting your student in their endeavors in all areas of health.
* Collaborate on goals.
Assist students in conceiving and achieving reasonable goals within Health Education.
