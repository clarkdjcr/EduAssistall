# Arts Education - Accomplished

Parent Guide for Accomplished Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
Levels Beginning through Advanced courses for high school credit are intended to develop proficiency in the artform of a student’s choice and to support a well-rounded education. These courses meet for as long as other high school courses meet and are also taught by a licensed visual arts educator.

Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how art reflects ideas from society, culture, and politics, and examine how visual arts skills can be used to strengthen work in other academic subjects or career fields.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how visual arts skills are used in a variety of post-secondary and career pursuits. They also examine how digital media and technology have influenced how art is created, edited, and presented.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students investigate a variety of techniques, ideas, materials, and meanings when making art and create art with an identifiable personal style.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to be respectful when using images from another culture or artist, and also explore how parody, adaptation, and artificial intelligence relate to copyright protection.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students explain the choices they made in making changes to personal artwork based on feedback and create a statement that describes the process they used to create art.
PR.2 Develop - Students explain how a presentation format they select represents their unique style or vision, as well as examine how interpretations of an exhibition or collection can differ based on the social, cultural, or political community in which it is displayed.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn how movements and themes in art differ and how to interpret intent or purpose of a work based on the cultural or historical context of the artist or style.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how society and culture influence personal preferences in art and use criteria to analyze and evaluate several works of personal art that share a common theme.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Talk with your child about murals, political posters, or social media graphics.
* Point out how art skills and concepts can support work in other academic areas or careers.
* Explore digital tools together, like editing apps or online design platforms, and compare how digital and traditional methods create different results.
* Encourage your child to create multiple works of art in the development of a personal style.
* Discuss the difference between being inspired by an artist and copying their work.
* Encourage your child to share their artwork with the family and explain the design process.
* Visit a gallery or exhibition and discuss how that collection might be interpreted differently in various social, cultural, or political communities.
* Expose your child to different works of art and have them sort them by style, media, themes, or other similarities.
* Look at art together and discuss how artistic choices were made to convey meaning.
* Discuss aesthetic responses to art and how society and culture may have influenced preferences.
