# Healthful Living - 3rd Grade

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)

Essential Standard:
3.MEH.1 Explore characteristics of healthy expression of emotions, mental wellness, and personal awareness that contribute to wellbeing.
Clarifying Objective:
3.MEH.1.1 Identify the body's physical response and appropriate ways to express needs, wants, and feelings.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
The body has physical responses associated with needs, wants and feelings.
Physical responses to emotions:
* Nervous
   * racing heart
* Sadness
   * tears
   * sluggish feeling
* Fear
   * tightness in chest
   * rapid breathing
* Happiness
   * feel full of energy
Physical response to need:
* Food
   * stomach growl
* Water
   * feeling thirsty
Physical response to want:
* Increased heart rate
* Pupils dilate
* Sweating
* Increased focus
These physical responses may affect how needs, wants and feelings are expressed in both positive and negative ways. Appropriate ways of expression include:
1. Using I messages
2. Messages should be clear and direct
3. Active listening should be employed
4. Express the need as a request not a demand

Clarifying Objective:
3.MEH.1.2 Elaborate on the influence of peers, social media, technology, and the family on feelings, emotions, and behaviors.
Verb Defined:
To expand something in detail : to work out in detail
Student will know and be able to:
Children are increasingly influenced by peers, family dynamics, and the growing role of technology and social media in their lives. These influences can shape their emotions, feelings, and behaviors both positively and negatively. Recognizing these influences helps develop self-awareness, critical thinking, and the ability to make choices that support emotional well-being.
* Peers can affect their feelings and behaviors:
   * feeling happy when included by friends
   * feeling sad when left out
* Family members influence through:
   * support
   * encouragement
   * shared experiences.
* Technology and social media may influence based on the content shared:
   * positive images or messages may cause feelings of  happiness, excitement, or connectedness
   * negative images or messages may cause frustration, sadness, or pressure

Clarifying Objective:
3.MEH.1.3 Explore strategies that can be applied to promote a growth mindset and resiliency.
Verb Defined:
To investigate, study, or analyze : look into
Student will know and be able to:
Challenges are opportunities to grow. Overcoming obstacles and embracing mistakes are a part of learning. A growth mindset is a belief that improvement can occur with practice and effort. Resiliency is the ability to keep trying, even when things are hard, and to bounce back from mistakes or challenges.
Strategies to promote growth mindset and resiliency:
* positive self-talk
* breaking problems into steps
* asking for help
* reflecting on progress

Clarifying Objective:
3.MEH.1.4 Summarize how to access resources for assistance with feelings, various challenges, and mental wellness.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Teaching students to identify and access resources—such as trusted adults, friends, and tools—empowers them to take proactive steps to manage their emotions and address difficulties. By summarizing where and how to seek support, students build the foundation for self-advocacy and emotional well-being.
* There are resources (people, places, and tools) that can help them when they have strong feelings, face challenges, or need support.
* Trusted adults—like teachers, family members, school counselors, or coaches—are there to help.
* Friends can also support them by listening and offering encouragement.
* Mental wellness tools, like journaling, taking deep breaths, or using calming spaces, can help them feel better.
* The student will summarize how to access resources in different situations (e.g., asking for help at school, talking to a family member at home, or using calming strategies when upset).

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)

Essential Standard:
3.PCH.1 Apply personal behaviors and practices that promote health and prevent disease.
Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.1.1 Recognize dental hygiene practices prevent plaque, gum disease and cavities.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge formally : to acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Proper dental hygiene practices are important in preventing the buildup of plaque, gum disease and prevention of cavities.

Plaque
* Sticky film of bacteria found on teeth
* Regular brushing with a fluoride toothpaste helps remove plaque before it turns into tartar

Gum disease
* an infection in the tissue that supports the teeth
* if plaque is not removed gum disease my result
* brushing along the gumline and flossing prevents the build of plaque

Cavities
* holes in the teeth caused by bacteria that produce acid that dissolves enamel
* the bacteria in plaque contains acid, which erodes the teeth
* brushing twice a day, flossing daily, and the use of mouthwash helps eliminate the bacteria

Benefits of dental hygiene:
* healthy teeth and gums
* prevent cavities
* fresh breath

Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.1.2 Implement proper flossing to prevent tooth decay and gum disease.
Verb Defined:
To carry out: accomplish
Student will know and be able to:
Flossing teeth is an important part of dental hygiene, it helps remove food particles and plaque between teeth and along the gum line where a toothbrush can't reach. It assists with the removal of bad breath-causing bacteria rather than brushing alone. Removal of plaque from below the gumline, which can erode tooth enamel and develop into tartar.

1. Break off about 18 to 24 inches of dental floss. To hold the floss correctly, wind most of the floss around both of the middle fingers. Leave only about 1 to 2 inches of floss.

2. Next, hold the floss taut with the thumbs and index fingers.
3. Place the dental floss in between two teeth. Gently glide the floss up and down, rubbing it against both sides of each tooth. Don’t glide the floss into the gums. This can scratch or bruise them.
4. As the floss reaches the gums, curve the floss at the base of the tooth to form a C shape. This allows the floss to enter the space between the gums and tooth.
5. Repeat the steps, moving from tooth to tooth. With each tooth, use a new, clean section of floss.
Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.1.3 Explain the importance of personal hygiene in supporting one’s health.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Personal hygiene effectively prevents the spread of infectious diseases, protects health by reducing the risk of illness, boosts self-esteem by maintaining a clean appearance, and positively impacts social interactions by showing respect to others through cleanliness.

Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.1.4 Describe how sleep and regular physical activity benefit your health.
Verb Defined:
To represent or give an account of in words
Student will know and be able to:
Sleep and regular physical activity improve energy, mood, focus, and long-term health. By making these connections, a person is more likely to adopt healthy behaviors that support their growth and prevent disease.

* Sleep:
   * Third graders need about 9 to 12 hours of sleep every night to feel rested and help their bodies and brains grow.
* Physical Activity:
   * Children should get at least 60 minutes (1 hour) of physical activity every day. This can include running, playing sports, dancing, biking, swimming, or playing games outside.
* Sleep and physical activity help with:
   * Having more energy.
   * Feeling happier and less stressed.
   * Focusing better and doing well in school.
   * Staying healthy and growing stronger.

Essential Standard:
3.PCH.2 Explain necessary steps to prevent and respond to unintentional injury.
Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.2.1 Demonstrate methods for prevention of common unintentional injuries.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly : to prove or make clear by reasoning or evidence
Student will know and be able to:
When children know ways to prevent common unintentional injuries, they can be empowered to make safe choices that protect themselves and others. Learning prevention strategies and practicing safety habits can build confidence in handling potentially harmful situations.

* Unintentional injuries can happen during everyday activities like biking, skateboarding, playing, or cooking.
* Common injuries include:
   * cuts
   * burns
   * bruises
   * head injuries
* Methods for preventing injuries:
   * Wearing protective gear appropriate for activities (helmets, pads, etc.)
   * Watching for hazards
   * Following safety rules
   * Being aware of potential dangers
   * Looking both ways before crossing the street
   * Keeping sharp objects out of reach when not in use.

Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.2.2 Summarize methods that increase and reduce injuries in and around water.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Student will know and be able to:
Water environments like pools, lakes, rivers, and bathtubs can present risks when safety rules are not followed. Slipping, running, unsafe play, and swimming without supervision can increase the risk of unintended injuries.
Skills to prevent unintentional injuries:
* Following safety rules
   * no running
   * no diving
   * no rough play in the pool
   * never swim alone
* Learning to swim
* Have adult supervision
* Wearing appropriate safety equipment
   * life preservers when boating or kayaking
   * gear (for example, life vests when kayaking) can help reduce the risk of an injury and keep everyone safe.
Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.2.3 Identify ways to prevent injuries from firearms.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Firearm safety is critical. If a firearm is encountered:
* All firearms are dangerous.
* If a firearm is discovered
   * Stop what they are doing
   * Don’t Touch the firearm
   * Tell an adult right away about the firearm
* Lock up firearms in cabinets, drawers or gun safes
* Never leave guns unattended

Clarifying Objective:
3.PCH.2.4 Implement a plan to escape fire at home while avoiding smoke inhalation.
Verb Defined:
To carry out: accomplish
Student will know and be able to:
Fire safety is an essential life skill. Knowing how to implement a fire escape plan while avoiding smoke inhalation can help a person stay calm, act quickly, and make safe decisions to protect themselves and their families.
* Fire produces smoke, which is harmful to breathe.
   * Stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation.
* A fire escape plan should be created that includes:
   * A map of the home showing all rooms
   * Two exits for every room (windows and doors)
   * Primary escape routes
   * A family meeting spot outside of the home
* The plan should be practiced.

Interpersonal Communication and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)

Essential Standard:
3.ICHR.1 Explain healthy and effective interpersonal communications and relationships to enhance wellbeing.
Clarifying Objective:
3.ICHR.1.1
Identify behaviors that promote healthy relationships with families and peers.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
A healthy relationship is one where partners respect each other, communicate openly, and trust each other. A healthy relationship within a family and with peers can be developed and promoted using the following behaviors:
* Pay attention when others are speaking, avoid interrupting.
* Positive body language, such as eye contact and open posture.
* Treat others as you would like to be treated
* Appreciate similarities and differences
* Respect personal space
* Try to understand the feelings of others
* Say thank you
* Compliment the other person
* Spend time together in shared interests
* When conflict arises, it should be handled correctly

Clarifying Objective:
3.ICHR.1.2 Demonstrate effective communication skills and personal boundaries to enhance respect and well-being for others and self.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Effective communication skills should focus on respectful expression. Key skills:
* Active Listening:
   * Listen to others
   * Show that you are paying attention
   * Don’t interrupt
* Speak Clearly:
   * Loud enough to be heard but not too loud
* Take turns
   * Let each person speak
* Ask Questions:
   * Show interest
   * Clarify what they don’t understand.
* Use "I" Statements:
   * This helps them express their feelings in a positive way without blaming others.
* Non-Verbal Communication:
   * Body language, facial expressions, and gestures send messages as well
* Showing Empathy:
   * Recognize how others are feeling
   * Respond with kindness
Personal boundaries are the limits and rules a person sets to feel safe and comfortable. They can be physical (like space) or emotional (like feelings). Everyone has different boundaries.
* Personal space:
   * Everyone has their own space or bubble
* Respectful Play:
   * If someone says "stop" or "no," listen immediately
* Feelings Matter:
   * Everyone has emotional boundaries.
   * Respect the feelings of others and give them space if they need it, just like personal space
* Kind Words:
   * Be careful with words.

Clarifying Objective:
3.ICHR.1.3 Recognize the causes of conflict and apply appropriate strategies for resolution.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge formally : to acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Conflict is defined as a serious disagreement or argument. Conflicts usually arise from a difference in opinion, competition for resources, misunderstandings or personal space.

Causes of Conflict:

* Different Opinions:
   * People want different things
   * People have different ideas about what they want to do
* Competition for Resources:
   * If one person gets more turns than another
   * If there are not enough playground balls during recess for everyone to have one
* Personal Space:
   * “invading” the personal physical space (bubble) or emotional space of others

Resolution Strategies:
* Use Kind Words:
   * Use “I” messages.
* Listen to Each Other
* Compromise:
   * Find a solution where both sides give a little to create a solution
* Apologize and Forgive
* Ask for Help:
   * Ask an adult or teacher for assistance if the conflict cannot be resolved.

Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)

Essential Standard:
3.NPA.1 Recall why the body needs a variety of foods.
Clarifying Objective:
3.NPA.1.1 Recall the food groups and what foods are in each food group, according to the USDA.
Verb Defined:
To call back : to bring back to mind
Student will know and be able to:
The USDA categorizes foods in 5 food groups, fruits, vegetables, grains, protein foods and dairy. Each food group contains foods with similar nutritional value.

The fruit group includes: apples, bananas, kiwi, pineapple, raisins. oranges, and many others.
The vegetable group includes: broccoli, carrots, green peas, bell peppers, cabbage, and many others.
The grain group includes: rice, oatmeal, crackers, bread, tortillas, quinoa, corn, farro, and many others.
Examples of foods in the protein group are: chicken, beef, pork, fish, shrimp, eggs, nuts/nut butters, beans, and lentils.
Lastly, the dairy group includes: milk, cheese, yogurt and ice cream.
Clarifying Objective:
3.NPA.1.2 Identify food group components of a variety of example meals.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Regardless of cultural background, meals typically include foods from several food groups. Some meals have food components served separately to create a meal, while other meals include food groups that are served combined in different ways. By eating meals and snacks with foods from different food groups, our bodies get the different nutrients they need.

The components of a meal will be separated into the USDA 5 food groups, fruits, vegetables, grains, protein foods and dairy.

Clarifying Objective:
3.NPA.1.3 Plan activities for fitness and recreation during out of school hours.
Verb Defined:
To arrange the parts of : design
Student will know and be able to:
It's important to engage in activities that provide benefits to the mind and body.  Fitness, or physical activity, has many benefits at every age and includes any voluntary movement that uses energy.  Recreation is an activity done for enjoyment.  An activity may be both a physical activity and recreation. Fitness and recreation have physical and mental/emotional benefits. Activities outside of school hours may include:
* playing on playground or playground equipment
* participating in recreational leagues
* nature trail walks
* bicycling or skating
* dancing
* playing sports with friends, not in a league or on an organized team

Consideration for creating a plan:
* think through the limitations of their schedule
* access to facilities and equipment
* activities that are enjoyed
* possible benefits

Clarifying Objective:
3.NPA.2.2 List the body's signals when you are hungry and thirsty.
Verb Defined:
Catalog : checklist : enumerate
Students will be able to:
Food and water are very important to the body. Bodies have built-in systems to tell us when we are hungry and thirsty.  These signals can be louder or quieter for different people.

When someone is hungry, they may:
* think about food and eating more
* feel like their stomach is empty
* have increased salivation when thinking about food
* have growling/rumbling stomach
* have low energy or feel tired
* have trouble concentrating
* feel irritable or angry

When someone is thirsty, they may:
* have dry mouth
* think about water or drinking
* have a headache
* have the urge to drink
* have an itchy throat

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and Other Drugs (ANCOD)

Essential Standard:
3.ANCOD.1 Apply household safety procedures for products and medicines with adult supervision.
Clarifying Objective:
3.ANCOD.1.1 Summarize how medications can help with common health problems.
Verb Defined:
To tell in or reduce to a summary : to make a summary
Students will know and be able to:
Medications, both over the counter (OTC) and prescription, can assist in alleviating many health problems. Medication or anything that looks like medication should be taken without the permission and guidance of an adult

* Pain relief
   * Over the counter (OTC) pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can reduce inflammation and alleviate pain from conditions like headaches, muscle aches, or minor injuries.

* Infections
   * Antibiotics are used to treat bacterial infections
   * Antiviral medications help relieve symptoms of the flu.

* Chronic Conditions:
   * Insulin for the treatment of diabetes
   * Hypertensives for high blood pressure
.
* Allergies:
   * Antihistamines and decongestants to assist with sneezing, congestion, and itching

* Respiratory Conditions
   * Inhalers to help manage asthma

Clarifying Objective:
3.ANCOD.1.2 Explain the consequences of disregarding medical recommendations for prescription and nonprescription medications.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Medications are designed for specific purposes, and when they are used correctly or AS DIRECTED, then they are most effective.
When taking any medication, it is important to read the label and take AS DIRECTED, if medication is misused, there can be serious side effects.

* Over the Counter (OTC):
   * illness may be prolonged
   * condition may become worse
   * memory loss
   * kidney failure
   * heart problems.

* Prescription:
   * mood and behavior changes
   * trouble thinking clearly
   * breathing problems
   * increased side effect
   * possibility of dependence

Essential Standard:
3.ANCOD.2 Apply strategies involving risk reduction behaviors to protect self and others from the negative effects of alcohol, nicotine, and other drugs.
Clarifying Objective:
3.ANCOD.2.1 Recognize refusal skills when confronted or pressured to use alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, or other drugs.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge formally : to acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Refusal skills are effective strategies to use when a person wants to say no when they are pressured to use alcohol, nicotine, cannabis or other drugs:

Strategies

* Direct Refusal:
   * Calm and assertive, No thanks!
* Give a Reason:
   * Explain why you are refusing

* Suggest an Alternative:
   * Suggest engaging in another activity

* Blame Someone Else:
   * Use an excuse that involves someone else
   * “My mom will ground me for a month if I do this!”

* Walk Away:
   * Leave the situation entirely if safe and possible

* Use “I” messages
   * Make the refusal about yourself not others

* Repeat if Necessary:
   * Repeat any of the refusals above again if need be

Clarifying Objective:
3.ANCOD.2.2 Identify refusal skills when personal safety is at risk.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
As spoken about in the previous objective, refusal skills are also effective strategies to use when a person’s personal safety is at risk. All of the above strategies are effective with the addition of:

* Stay Calm
   * Remain composed
   * Avoid getting angry or defensive
   * Prevent escalating the situation
* Seek Support
* Try to get help from a parent, guardian, trusted adult
* Try to get assistance from an authority figure, SRO, teacher, or principal.
* Example: "I’m going to call my friend to pick me up."
