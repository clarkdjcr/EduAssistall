# Arts Education - Fourth Grade

Parent Guide for Fourth Grade Visual Arts
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for visual arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with visual arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed visual arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore how customs, traditions, and history of indigenous and other cultural groups have influenced and been influenced by visual arts. They also learn to use concepts and skills from everyday life and other content areas in visual arts.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore visual arts careers in NC and examine the impact emerging technologies and contemporary media have had on the field of visual arts.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students use imagery from North Carolina as source material in developing imaginary and expressive ideas in personal art. They also create original abstract art that expresses ideas.
CR.2 Adapt - Students combine ideas from multiple sources to create artwork and learn how to responsibly access visual arts online.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Present - Students learn to use feedback from self-evaluation to make changes to personal art and to use the vocabulary of art when creating an artist statement about their work.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to select artwork based on common ideas, themes, or media. They also learn about art museums, galleries, and other venues in North Carolina.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students learn to group North Carolina artists by the style, type of art, or artistic movement they represent and how to interpret meaning in a work of art.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students make comparisons between how they respond to a work of art throughout the creative process and use specific criteria to provide feedback in self-evaluation of their work.

How to Support Your Visual Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your visual arts student:
* Discuss cultural art traditions and historical events, and explore their influence on art you see in North Carolina.
* Point out how concepts from other subjects can be used when making and sharing art.
* Explore multiple visual arts careers in North Carolina.
* Encourage your child to draw inspiration from North Carolina landscapes, landmarks, or cultural events.
* Provide materials for experimenting with abstract art or combining multiple ideas.
* Ask your child to explain the story, theme, or choices in their artwork.
* Display their work at home or create a mini “gallery” for family and friends.
* Visit local museums or galleries together.
* Look at art together and discuss interpretations of the work.
* Have your child explain how they feel about a work at different times throughout the creative process.
* Talk about different styles or types of art in North Carolina and compare artists’ approaches.
* Encourage your child to reflect on their own work and give thoughtful feedback.
