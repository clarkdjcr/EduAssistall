# Arts Education - Third Grade

Parent Guide for Third Grade Dance

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for dance is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with dance builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K-5 standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed dance educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Dr. Laura Stauderman, K-12 Dance and Visual Arts Consultant
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore the various dance forms found in their local community or region and examine the cross-curricular relationships between dance elements and the elements used in other arts disciplines. Students also learn of strategies to address common mental challenges that are common to dancers.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore dance careers and organizations in the local community or region and examine how new ideas and technologies used to create and present dance have developed over time.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students explore spontaneous movement invention using the dance elements and how to combine short dance sequences in the creation of longer dance phrases. Students also learn how to represent spatial directions or pathways of movement in dance by drawing pictures, maps, or using symbols.
CR.2 Adapt - Students learn how to adapt dance sequences by changing the directions or pathways of movements, and learn which dance works can be fairly used by everyone.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students learn to use formal and informal feedback to improve their choreography and dance skills and to demonstrate coordination of multiple body parts in the basic dance technique of a particular style. Students also strengthen performing with focus when dancing for and with others.
PR.2 Develop - Students learn how to use instructional support to identify criteria for the selection of a theme, topic, or music for a small group performance and examine safe and respectful behaviors when performing and observing performances in various cultures and settings. Students use a production element, such as stage lighting or costumes, to enhance a dance performance.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students describe movement sequences that demonstrate how multiple elements of dance (body, space, time, effort quality) are used in dance and also provide reasoning for possible meanings in a variety of dance movements or works.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students examine personal preferences when viewing dances from a variety of styles, techniques, and cultures, and provide constructive criticism in self and peer-evaluation of work.

How to Support Your Dance Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your dance student:
* Clear a little space so your child can move freely and safely.
* Take your child to a local festival, performance, or cultural event and talk about the styles of dance you see.
* Encourage your child to relax before performing by doing deep breathing or short stretches together.
* Talk about dance teachers, choreographers, or dance companies in your community.
* Ask your child to make up a move using one element (body, space, time, or effort)
* Try a dance move forward, backward, in circles, or diagonals, then have your child draw a picture or map of the directions or pathways that were used.
* Talk about why copying a move is okay in learning, but choreographers should always be credited for original ideas.
* Model giving praise and constructive feedback
* Expose them to dance in different performance spaces and discuss behavioral expectations
* Watch dance together and have your child give specific examples when describing what the dancers are doing and how the meaning of the dance was communicated.
* Discuss preferences for watching different styles of dance.
