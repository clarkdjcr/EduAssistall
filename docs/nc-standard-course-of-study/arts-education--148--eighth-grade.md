# Arts Education - Eighth Grade

Parent Guide for Eighth Grade Theatre Arts

The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for theatre arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The ability to create, present, respond to, and make connections with theatre arts builds competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The grade 6-8 standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed theatre arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
For additional questions, please contact Brandon Roeder, K-12 theatre arts and Theatre Arts Consultant.
Connect
Explore and relate artistic ideas and works to past, present, and future societies and cultures.
CN.1 Relate - Students explore influential North Carolinian theatre as well as theatre skills and processes that can be used to solve problems.
CN.2 Explore - Students explore how theatre teaches skills, characteristics, and behaviors useful in all careers, and explore how theatre tools are expanding careers beyond the theatrical world.
Create
Create and adapt new artistic ideas and work individually or collaboratively.
CR.1 Originate - Students improvise conflict/resolution scenarios, perform research to build authentic characters and situations, and create lighting and sound designs for their dramatic presentations.
CR.2 Adapt -  Students adapt historical or current events into theatre, and explain how to legally and ethically share theatre both physically and digitally.
Present
Present, perform, produce, and develop artistic ideas and works.
PR.1 Perform - Students use objective feedback and criteria to improve their presentations and use blocking given by directors and playwrights.
PR.2 Develop - Students explore literary styles in theatre, use technical elements in their presentations, and model appropriate behaviors of both performers and live audiences.
Respond
Analyze and evaluate how the arts communicate.
RE.1 Analyze - Students analyze theatre within its historical and cultural contexts, exploring how artistic choices are affected by culture and environment.
RE.2 Evaluate - Students explore how personal preferences are affected by culture and environment and evaluate performances.

How to Support Your Theatre Arts Student at Home
The following strategies can help support your theatre arts student:
* Ask your student to share the North Carolinian theatre and actors they are learning about at school
* Attend local theatre performances, showcases, or festivals together, discussing their historical and cultural context
* Watch theatre, musicals, films, or performances from different cultures, time periods, or genres, and talk about the playwright’s culture and environment
* Discuss what appropriate behavior looks like at various locations (i.e., movie theatre, proscenium theatre, school stage, outdoor amphitheater, etc.) and various performance types (i.e., interactive shows, musicals, plays, etc.)
* Encourage your student to create interesting characters, creating lighting and sound designs to support their stories at home out of craft supplies and flashlights.
* Discuss how the skills they practice in theatre class can be leveraged in all careers
* Discuss how pirating theatre and film hurts every artist involved in the creation of the stolen work
* Attend school or community performances when possible
