# Arts Education - Second Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Second Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The K - 5 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for elementary students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 50 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

2.CN.1.1 Describe how American visual arts are reflected in the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States.
Glossary
* Culture(s) - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
* Custom - A usage or practice common to many or to a particular place or class or habitual with an individual; the whole body of usages, practices, or conventions that regulate social life.14
* Describe - To give a written or spoken report of how something is done or of what someone or something is like.4
* Diverse - Including many different types of people or things.4
* Heritage - Something transmitted by or acquired from a predecessor; Tradition.14
* Traditions - Pattern of practices and beliefs within a societal group.17
* Visual Arts - As defined by the National Art Education Association, include the traditional fine arts, such as, drawing , painting, printmaking, photography, and sculpture; media arts, such as, animation, video and emerging digital technologies; design, such as, communication, product, and interactive; environmental, such as, architecture, landscape architecture, interior and urban planning; conceptual, performance, participatory, street, and folk arts and works of art in clay, glass, metal, wood, fiber, paper and other materials. (Revised March 2017).17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students explained how visual arts are used in cultures around the world. At the current level, students describe how the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States can reflect American visual arts. In the next level, students compare purposes of art in different cultures, time periods, and societies.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* What is meant by American Visual Arts
* What is meant by heritage, customs, and traditions
* Examples of the heritage, customs, and traditions of diverse cultures that exist in America
* How the visual arts are seen in the diverse heritage, customs, and traditions of cultures in the United States
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Summarizing the characteristics of how visual arts are reflected in the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States.
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to describe how American visual arts are reflected in the diverse heritage, customs AND traditions of various cultures in the United States. Depending on the learning activity, how American visual arts is found in all three; heritage, customs, and traditions of multiple cultures in the United States can be assessed simultaneously or broken apart to be assessed through multiple projects over the course of study but ultimately students must master the entirety of the objective
* This objective is not asking students to describe art that reflects customs, heritage, and traditions but rather about how art is reflected IN the heritage, customs, and traditions of various cultures in the United States
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide examples of how American visual arts is seen in the diverse customs, traditions, and heritages of various cultures in the United States
* Guide student exploration in discovering how American visual arts exists in cultural heritage, customs, and traditions of diverse populations in the United States
* Design learning activities for students to describe how American visual arts are reflected in the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States.
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to describe how American visual arts are reflected in the diverse heritage, customs and traditions of various cultures in the United States
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Jazz and music inspired art
* Cultural symbols and emblems
* NC Folk art
* NC pottery

2.CR.1.1 Experiment with creative solutions to challenges to create art.
Glossary
* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Experiment (with) - try out a new procedure, idea, or activity.14
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students experimented with materials to make unique combinations of ideas, themes, or events and now are experimenting with creative solutions to challenges to create art. In the next level, students will use imaginative and expressive processes to generate ideas, plan solutions, and develop personal artwork.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of visual artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* That experimentation can lead to innovative solutions to creative challenges
* How to use media, materials, and tools in a novel way safely and appropriately
* How to think creatively to arrive at possible solutions for creative challenges
* The creative design process
* The elements of Art
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Trying out new ideas, methods, media, tools, applications and techniques, to find creative solutions for creative challenges when making art
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* Artistic challenges may be unique to each student’s personal work
* The concept behind this objective was that students will make mistakes but experimentation can find creative ways to overcome them when making art. Allow students to work through frustrations as part of art experimentation. Students don’t need to start over or throw away their work
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Provide clear expectations in the appropriate and safe use of tools and materials
* Demonstrate how to use experimentation to creatively solve artistic challenges when making art
* Guide students in the design cycle of creating personal art
* Design learning activities in which students can experiment with creative solutions to challenges to create art
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to experiment with creative solutions to challenges to create art
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Challenges in art making may come naturally throughout the creative process but the teacher can also pose a challenge to students that will require them to think creatively and experiment with solutions. Ideas could include not having a specific color of paint so students have to mix colors or not having other materials and students must improvise on what they can use to create art
* Clay exploration
* Paintings with various techniques and tools
* Drawing shapes to look 3-D
* Collage with various materials

This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students differentiated between sharing ideas and copying and now adapt materials, style, or ideas from artists or artworks. In the next level, students will create personal art in the style of another artist or artwork.
Enduring Understanding

Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The materials, style, or ideas of other artists or artworks
* How to alter or modify materials, styles, or ideas used by others
* The creative design process
* How to use tools and materials safely and appropriately
* The elements of Art
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Modifying the materials, style, or ideas from other artists or artworks in the creation of personal art
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Note the conjunction “or” used twice in this objective - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to adapt materials, style, OR ideas from artists OR artworks. Depending on the learning activity, the teacher can decide if the student is to adapt the materials, style, or ideas and if the original source is artists or artworks. Autonomy can also be given to the student to decide how they want to create art to demonstrate mastery of this objective
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.2 in which students explain how to share visual artworks fairly as well as with CN objectives as students learn about art in personal, societal, cultural, historical, and daily life contexts and other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Expose students to artists and works that exemplify a variety of media, styles, and ideas
* Model how to adapt, but not copy the media, styles, and ideas of other artists and works.
* Facilitate a discussion about how to make adaptations of other work
* Provide opportunities for students to create personal art by adapting the materials, style, or ideas from other artists or artworks
* Guide student ideation and creation
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to adapt materials, style, or ideas from artists or artworks
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Looking at the style of famous artists (such as James Van Der Zee or Kandinsky) and making personal work that captures the essence of their style
* Make personal artwork inspired by other artistic works by changing the materials (use colored pasta and other materials to make 3D images of original 2D works)
* Have students bring in artistic examples of ideas and create personal work based on the ideas (i.e. seascapes, beach art, or cityscapes)
* Matisse style shape collage
* Kandinsky circles with new materials/shapes/style
* Color wheel with unique materials

2.CR.2.2 Explain how to share visual artworks fairly.
Glossary
* Explain - To make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it.4
* Fairness - Complying with appropriate, ethical, and equitable rules and guidelines.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students explained how and when to give credit to others when creating derivative work and now explain how to share visual artworks fairly. In the next level, students will identify visual artworks that can be used by everyone fairly.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* How visual artwork is shared
* What “fairly” looks like in practice (getting permission or limiting use)
* How to credit artists for their work
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Describing the process for sharing visual artworks fairly
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “How do I ethically share someone else’s work?” to show mastery of this objective.
* Students should be able to explain that artists choose how and when they want their works to be shared. Just as students want others to respect when and how others share their artwork, they need to respect when and how other artists want their work to be shared
* An age-appropriate explanation of fair-use could include respecting ownership, giving credit, using publicly available resources, and making decisions about sharing their own original work
* Sharing artwork fairly means giving credit to the person who made it and not taking, exhibiting, posting online, showing, or copying it without asking
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students adapt materials, style, or ideas from artists or artworks. Using works under fair use conditions the student can then explain how principles of fair use support making adaptations of preexisting works
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined
Teacher Actions
* Model and narrate these positive and ethical behaviors during instruction
* Prepare student-friendly definitions for understanding of “share” and “fairly” keeping in mind ethical and legal issues
* Provide examples of art that can be used fairly
* Guide classroom discussion about how to share visual artworks fairly
* Support student learning in how to explain how art can shared fairly
* Provide learning activities in which students can explain how to share visual artworks fairly
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to explain how to share visual artworks fairly
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Students can be paired to explain to their partners how to share art fairly
* Students can be assisted in creating an audio/visual recording in which they explain how to share art fairly
* Students can write a short paragraph in which they explain how to share art fairly
