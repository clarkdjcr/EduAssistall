# Healthful Living - Kindergarten

North Carolina Unpacking Document for Health Education

Health Education K-12

The Unpacking Documents for North Carolina K-12 Health Education were created in collaboration with teachers, NCDPI leadership, and members of the NCDPI Healthy Schools Team. These documents are intended to supplement the standard course of study and provide a comprehensive understanding for the teaching of the standards and objectives. The explanations and examples in this document are intended to be helpful in the planning of local curriculum and classroom instruction.

This document will provide:
Strand: defined area of instruction
Essential Standard: the State Board of Education approved standard(s) for a strand
Clarifying Objective: the State Board of Education approved objectives for teaching and learning
Verb: definition of the verb used to assess student understanding
Students will know and be able to: information that the students should know and a description of how the student should be able to demonstrate mastery of the objective

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Content examples for instruction that help to build student knowledge and understanding of the objective
Sample activities to gauge learning that may be used to determine whether students are meeting the learning objective
Recommendations, with the understanding that PSUs retain local control to determine curriculum

The example topics provided with each objective are:
Not meant to be an exhaustive list
Not meant to be content that must be taught all at once
Not a checklist for basic recall or memorization
Not a checklist for assessment for each objective

Note: These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a local public-school unit (PSU) or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-9 Health Education.

Kindergarten

Mental and Emotional Health (MEH)

Essential Standard:
K.MEH.1 Remember the association of healthy expression of emotions, mental health, and healthy behavior.
Clarifying Objective:
K.MEH.1.1 Identify a variety of feelings that people experience and ways of expressing them.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Everyone has thoughts and feelings. Some of the different ways they can show how they feel are using facial expressions, moving   the body, and using words. Feelings can be categorized as comfortable or uncomfortable.

Happiness
* smile or laugh

Sad
* a “down” facial expression
* crying

Anger
* clenched fist
* snapping short responses in conversation
* angry facial expression

Fear
* wide eyes
* avoiding eye contact
* seeking comfort

Clarifying Objective:
K.MEH.1.2 Demonstrate personal responsibilities for actions and possessions.
Verb Defined:
To show clearly
Student will know and be able to:
Rules in both the home and in the classroom are designed to be helpful and keep them safe. Personal actions can have both positive and negative consequences.
* Ability to follow classroom rules
* Being kind to others
* Using good manners
* Taking turns
* Sharing, being a good listener, and taking care of classroom supplies.

Personal responsibility:
* Self-control
   * impulses
   * emotions
   * movement in the classroom
* Track, maintain, and care for possessions, both personal and school-owned

Essential Standard:
K.MEH.2 Identify that effort and practice lead to improved skills.
Clarifying Objective:
K.MEH.2.1 Remember that mistakes are important for learning.
Verb Defined:
To keep in mind for attention or consideration
Student will know and be able to:
Mistakes are normal and an integral part of the learning process. Correcting mistakes promotes the use of alternatives and helps movement towards the acquisition of new skills. Normalizing mistakes allows a person to attempt new things without self-recrimination.
Clarifying Objective:
K.MEH.2.2 Remember that practice is needed to improve performance.
Verb Defined:
To keep in mind for attention or consideration
Student will know and be able to:
The correct, consistent, and repeated performance of an action is necessary for skill acquisition as well as increased levels of performance. Practice enables confidence, expertise, and discipline in the performance of skills.

Personal and Consumer Health (PCH)

Essential Standard:
K.PCH.1 Demonstrate age-appropriate personal behaviors that promote health and prevent disease.

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.1.1 Identify that healthy behaviors impact personal health.

Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown

Student will know and be able to:
Healthy behaviors can impact personal health in many positive ways:

* Regular physical activity
* Balanced diet, eating nutritious foods
* Proper amount of rest
* Hydration
* Positive relationships
Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.1.2 Use steps of correct hand washing at appropriate times throughout the day.
Verb Defined:
to put into action or service : utilize
Student will know and be able to:
One of the best ways to prevent diseases is to wash hands after every trip to the bathroom, before eating or touching food, after blowing the nose, coughing or sneezing, after petting animals, and after playing outside.

The following steps are recommended by the CDC:
1. Wet hands with clean, running water (warm or cold) and apply soap.
2. Rub hands together to make lather and scrub them well, including the backs of hands, between fingers, and under nails.
3. Continue rubbing for at least 20 seconds. Hum the “Happy Birthday” song twice.
4. Rinse hands well under running water.
5. Dry hands using a clean towel or air-dry them.

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.1.3
Model proper tooth brushing techniques.
Verb Defined:
An example for imitation or emulation
Student will know and be able to:
The teeth and gums are very important for chewing and nutrition, for speech, and for an attractive smile. To keep teeth healthy, brush with toothpaste and floss every day, eat healthy foods, and visit the dentist. Children should brush their teeth twice a day for 2 minutes and floss once a day.

Proper technique for brushing and flossing recommended by the American Dental Association:
1. Place a soft brush at a 45-degree angle against the gum.
2. Move the brush gently in short strokes.
3. Brush the outside, inside, and flat parts of teeth.
4. Be sure to rinse well.
Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.1.4 Explain rationale for not sharing hygiene products (combs, brushes, toothbrushes).
Verb Defined:
To make known ; to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
For health and hygiene, it is important to care for hair, teeth, and skin. By keeping bodies clean, people are less likely to spread germs. Germs make children and adults sick. Germs are carried by combs, brushes, or toothbrushes. To prevent the spread of germs, these items should not be shared.

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.1.5 Select and use appropriate clothing according to the weather.
Verb Defined:
to choose from a number or group : pick out
Student will know and be able to:
When seasons change, clothing should change to reflect the outdoor surroundings.

* Summer
   * shorts
      * t-shirts
      * loose fitting clothes
      * hats
* Fall
   * light layers
   * long sleeves
   * socks/shoes
* Winter
   * coats
   * hats
   * gloves
   * scarves
   * boots
* Spring
   * light layers
   * long sleeves

Essential Standard:
K.PCH.2 Understand necessary steps to prevent and respond to unintentional injury.
Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.2.1 Recognize the meanings of traffic signs and safe practices for being a pedestrian and bicyclist.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Whenever people walk on the sidewalk or ride their bikes, they must follow rules to stay safe. Knowing and following the rules allows bike riders and walkers to be safe.

* Stop, Look, and Listen!
   * look left, right, and left again before crossing a street.
   * never enter into a street without stopping or enter a street between cars or from behind an obstruction
   * cross at street corners and crosswalks.
* Identify and describe the meanings of traffic signs and signals, such as:
   * stop lights
   * stop sign
   * walk/don’t walk signals
   * yield sign
   * one-way sign
   * do not enter sign
   * crosswalk sign
   * school crossing
   * railroad crossing

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.2.2 Explain the benefits of safety equipment including seat belts, booster seats, helmets, mouth guards, shin guards, and flotation devices.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Safety equipment is designed to ensure protection against unforeseen incidents and injury. Appropriate safety equipment based on age and size should be used.

* Children shorter than 4’9” inches tall should be riding in a car seat or booster seat each time they are in a vehicle to prevent injury in case of an accident.
* Children 4’9” inches and taller should use a seat belt each time they are in a vehicle in case of an accident.
* All children 12 and younger should ride in the back seat because of the risk of airbag injury.
* A bike helmet should fit straight on the head of the rider and fit snugly. The chinstrap should hang just enough to be felt it when yawning.
* Mouth and shin guards should be used to protect the teeth and legs in activities.
* To prevent drowning, flotation devices should be worn when boating and when in water, especially if the person cannot swim.

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.2.3 Illustrate how to get help in an emergency.
Verb Defined:
To make clear by giving or by serving as an example or instance
Student will know and be able to:
Emergencies are situations where someone is hurt or in danger. There is a difference between emergencies. An emergency is a situation that requires immediate attention:
* someone isn’t breathing
* someone with a broken leg
* a car accident

A non-emergency also requires attention, but it is not an immediate need
* someone with a scraped knee
* a cat in a tree

Children and adults should know to dial 9-1-1,
* give the name, address
* what is wrong
* speak clearly and loud enough to be heard
* stay on the line to be certain the operator has all needed information

Clarifying Objective:
K.PCH.2.4 Identify appropriate responses to warning signs, sounds, and labels.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
Warning signs, sounds, and labels are in homes, schools, businesses, community centers, and essentially everywhere people gather. Warning labels help people distinguish between helpful and harmful substances. The best response to harmful substances is not to touch:
* cleaning supplies
* medicines
* paint cleaners

  Unsafe places should be avoided or only visited when with an adult.
* construction sites
* railroad crossing
* crosswalks

   Situations / Alarms
Schools have drills to be prepared for emergencies. Drills should be taken seriously, so knowing what to do in a real emergency is automatic.
* fire drills
* tornado drills
* hurricane drills

Interpersonal Communications and Healthy Relationships (ICHR)

Essential Standard:
K.ICHR.1 Recognize healthy and effective interpersonal communication and relationships.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ICHR.1.1 Explain reasons for sharing.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Sharing is to use or enjoy something with others. When sharing, everyone gets a chance, everyone cooperates better and has more fun. It is directly linked to fairness and honesty. Within a day, there are many opportunities to share:

* Classroom
   * crayons
   * paints
   * books
   * taking turns

* Recess
   * balls
   * jump ropes
   * taking turns at the water fountain

Clarifying Objective:
K.ICHR.1.2 Recognize that all people have inherent value and dignity regardless of differences.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Kindergarten is a foundational time to introduce the idea that every person has value and deserves respect, no matter how they look, what they believe, or where they come from. Each human being has worth and value. Each human is unique. This concept helps build empathy, respect, and an inclusive mindset at a young age. People are naturally curious about differences and benefit from learning that these differences make the world more interesting and special.

* Every person is important and special in their own way.
* People may look different, speak different languages, or do things differently, but everyone deserves kindness and respect.
* It's okay to ask questions about differences in a kind and respectful way.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ICHR.1.3 Give examples of protective behaviors to use when approached by someone who you don't know or someone who makes you feel unsafe.
Verb Defined:
To put into the possession of another for his or her use
Student will know and be able to:
A stranger is a person who is unknown or with whom one is not acquainted. It may be difficult to fully understand, as adults communicate with people they don’t know at times.

General safety rules:
* a family may need a secret password or code.
   * If someone says, “Your Mom told me to pick you up,” the person would have to know the secret password before he or she would be believed.
* stay with a group or friend
* understand where it is okay and not okay to go
* know how to get help in an emergency
* do not communicate with a stranger
* do not take anything from a stranger
* do not go anywhere with a stranger
* if approached by a stranger, Say “NO,”
   * get away and tell someone.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ICHR.1.4 Recognize bullying, teasing, and aggressive behaviors and how to respond.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Bullying behavior is treating someone in a cruel, insulting, threatening, or aggressive fashion. It may include causing someone to do something by means of force or coercion. If the behavior is repeated, done on purpose and done to try to control the other person, it is bullying.

Bullying may appear as any of the following:
* physical violence
* looks
* gestures
* actions
* threats
* name calling
* remarks
* rumors
* social exclusion
* harassment.

Possible responses to bullying
* report the behavior to a trusted adult
* use the buddy system
* avoid the bully
* walk away
* use problem-solving skills to resolve the conflict

Nutrition and Physical Activity (NPA)

Essential Standard:
K.NPA.1 Identify sources of food and water and their importance to the body.
Clarifying Objective:
K.NPA.1.1 Recognize that food gives us energy.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Similar to gas making a car go, food provides the body with energy to move, grow, breathe, digest food, heal, think, and do everything bodies do. It’s important to give the body fuel throughout the day so that it has the energy to do the many things that   bodies do, including reading, playing on the playground, and walking to class. When eating meals and snacks, the body is provided fuel.

Clarifying Objective:
K.NPA.1.2 Identify that fruits and vegetables come from plants.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
  Student will know and be able to:
Many are removed from the sources of food. Fruits and vegetables come from plants that grow. Sources of different fruits and vegetables:

* Trees
   * apples
   * oranges

* Plants
   * strawberry
   * tomato
   * broccoli
   * cabbage
      * Underground plant
         * carrot
         * potatoes websites, and videos about farming and gardening.

Essential Standard:
K.NPA.2 State the importance of physical activity for the body.
Clarifying Objective:
K.NPA.2.1 List ways to be physically active.
Verb Defined:
To express the particulars of especially in words : report
Student will know and be able to:
Physical activity has health benefits at every age. During childhood, regular physical activity helps build strong bones and muscles and helps make the heart and lungs work efficiently. Physical activity also benefits sleep, self-esteem, mood, and stress levels. Children may feel better and have a sense of accomplishment when they participate in physical activity. Physical activity (especially play) provides opportunities for children to make friends and practice teamwork, following rules, and good sportsmanship. Physical activity is any voluntary movement that uses energy. This includes:
* walking to school
* running
* playing
* participating in a sport
* jumping rope
* riding a bike.

Alcohol, Nicotine, Cannabis, and other Drugs (ANCOD)

Essential Standard:
K.ANCOD.1 Understand household safety procedures for products and medicines with adult supervision.
Clarifying Objective:
K.ANCOD.1.1 Explain what is likely to happen if harmful household products are ingested or inhaled.
Verb Defined:
To make known : to make plain or understandable
Student will know and be able to:
Inhaling chemicals found in household products can cause:
* nausea
* headaches
* nosebleeds
* dizziness
* memory loss
* shortness of breath
* asthma.

Touching chemicals can cause:
* itching
* irritation
* burning of the skin.

If chemicals get into the eyes:
* burning/eye irritation
* inflammation
* glaucoma
* temporary or permanent loss of vision
* blindness

Children who swallow any amount of household products may experience symptoms ranging from;
* mild headache
* nausea
* diarrhea
* vomiting
* nervous, gastrointestinal, respiratory, and circulatory system damage

Additional reactions may include:
* difficulty breathing
* throat pain
* burns to the lips and mouth

Permanent physical and tissue damage, coma, and death can occur, depending on type and amount of chemical ingested.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ANCOD.1.2 Recognize that some medications look like candy.
Verb Defined:
To acknowledge or take notice of in some definite way
Student will know and be able to:
Medicines can be difficult to distinguish from candy because medicines come in many colors and shapes. Medicine can be commonly found in all homes and can even be found in places children view as safe, such as Grandma’s purse. Children should never drink an unknown liquid from a cup, bottle, or jar. Many liquid cleaners are colorful and appear very similar to Kool-aid. If a child doesn’t know what something is, he should never put it in his mouth and should always ask a grownup first.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ANCOD.1.3 Identify a parent, guardian, or trusted adult and professionals who can be trusted to provide safety information about household products and medicines.
Verb Defined:
Establish or indicate who or what something is that is unfamiliar or unknown
Student will know and be able to:
The best person in the home to give and provide safety information is a parent, guardian, or other trusted adult. At school, children should seek out a teacher, school nurse, or school counselor for this information. If medicines or household products are found an adult should be told so the container can be placed in a medicine cabinet or other childproof areas young children cannot reach.

Clarifying Objective:
K.ANCOD.1.4 Use appropriate strategies to access help when needed in emergencies involving household products and medicines.
Verb Defined:
To put into action or service : utilize
Student will know and be able to:
Accidents involving household products and medicines can be serious. An adult should be located immediately to call the Poison Control Center at 1-800-222-1222 for instructions on how best to handle the emergency. If this type of emergency occurs at school, a teacher, faculty, or staff member can provide necessary assistance. 9-1-1 may also be called. It is important to give accurate information to the dispatcher, including:
* address or location
* brief description of the emergency or what happened (if known)
* number of people involved.
