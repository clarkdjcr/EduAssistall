# Arts Education - Seventh Grade

North Carolina Standard Course of Study 2024 Visual Arts Standards Unpacking Seventh Grade
The 2024 North Carolina Standard Course of Study for Visual Arts is intended to provide a Comprehensive Arts Education while developing students as artists. The abilities to create, present, respond to, and make connections with Visual Arts build competence by expanding foundational knowledge and skills within each grade band and proficiency level. The standards are organized within the four artistic practices of Connect, Create, Present, and Respond, which are supported by the National Arts Education Standards created by the National Coalition for Arts Standards.
The 6-8 Visual Arts standards and objectives are written for middle school students who are taught by a licensed Visual Arts educator for at least 90 minutes per week.
The North Carolina Visual Arts Standards maintain the respect for local control of each Public School Unit (PSU). These standards and objectives are not intended to be the curriculum, nor do they indicate the whole of a curriculum which will be written by a PSU or school. The standards for this course have been developed to serve as the framework which will guide each PSU in the development of the curriculum for K-12 Visual Arts programs, and the proficiency level objectives show the minimum student outcomes for each skill.

7.CN.1.1 Analyze how individuals and groups of artists have influenced various cultures.
Glossary
* Analyze - Identifying and examining separate parts as they function independently and together in creative works and study of [the] arts.22
* Artist - A person who creates art (such as painting, sculpture, music, or writing) using conscious skill and creative imagination; a person skilled in any of the arts.14
* Culture - Values and beliefs of a particular group of people, from a specific place or time, expressed through characteristics such as tradition, social structure, religion, art, and food.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students analyzed the effect of the geographic location and physical environment on the development of visual arts. At the current level, students analyze how individuals and groups of artists have influenced various cultures and more closely explain how and why the visual arts influence ideas, beliefs, and experiences in North Carolina in the next level.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that a visual artist’s perception of events, cultures, and society shape the creation of techniques, styles, and genres; equally worthy of study, expression, and celebration.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* About customs, traditions, and artistic expressions of various cultures
* How art can shape cultural traditions, practices, and expression
* Visual Artists (individuals and groups) who have influenced various cultures
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Identifying and examining how the influence of individual and groups of visual artists impacted various cultures
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* The influence of visual artists (individuals and groups) is dynamic and interconnected
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students need to demonstrate mastery of their ability to analyze how individuals AND groups of artists have influenced various cultures. Depending on the learning assessment, students may analyze individuals and groups and various cultures simultaneously or this objective may be broken apart and assessed over multiple learning activities. Ultimately the student must master the entirety of the objective over the course of study
* This objective can be paired with CR.1.1 as students create personal artwork influenced by discoveries in this objective. This objective can also be paired with RE objectives
In the Classroom - Suggestions Only, this content is locally determined

* Provide artwork from a variety of times, places, and cultures

* Assist students in drawing connections between artistic influences from different times, places, and cultures
* Provide opportunity for students to analyze how arts in various cultures have been influenced by individual artists or artistic groups
* Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to analyze how individuals and groups of artists have influenced various cultures
Possible Learning Activities may include, but are not limited to:
* Use sample videos to show students Visual Artists (individuals and groups) who have influenced various cultures
* Use art images and resources from local, national and international museums
* Use interactive websites to show the impact of Visual Artists (individuals and groups)

7.CR.1.1 Use methods and approaches in media, techniques, and processes in both traditional and non-traditional ways.
Glossary
* Creative Design Process - The logical progression in the making of [art]; choose topic, research the topic, identify important aspects of the topic, devise problems to be solved, ask questions, solve problems and produce material, design artwork, self-evaluate, revise, get and use feedback from performance (concept, investigation, exploration, selection, development, refinement, exhibition).12
* Media - Mode(s) of artistic expression or communication; material or other resources used for creating art.17

* Use - To put into action or service.14

In the next level, students will experiment with techniques and processes to plan and create art.

The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that exploration, experimentation, and improvisation deepens the ability of visual artists to develop their creative aesthetic.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* The traditional methods and approaches in media, techniques, and processes
* How to experiment in discovering unconventional methods, approaches in media, techniques, and processes
* How to use safe practices in the use and experimentation of media, techniques, and processes
* The creative design process
* The elements of Art
* The principles of design
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Putting into practice the use of media, techniques, and processes in the creation of personal art through traditional and non-traditional methods and approaches.
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* As the emphasis of this throughline is on experimentation and investigation, mastery is demonstrated through the creative process itself - including ideation, risk-taking, and exploration. As such, demonstration of mastery may not always be a finished work of art
* Encourage experimentation, blend old and new techniques. Encourage innovation and foster personal expression. Non-traditional approaches may appeal to students who are drawn to contemporary, experimental or interactive works. Promote technical versatility.
* This objective can be paired with other CR, PR, and RE objectives for creation, presentation, analysis, and evaluation
* Note the conjunction “and” used three times in this objective - students should be able to show mastery of their ability to use methods AND approaches in media, techniques, AND processes in both traditional AND non-traditional ways. Traditional and non-traditional methods and approaches used in media, techniques, processes can be assessed simultaneously in the creation of one work, or assessed separately through different artmaking lessons. * Create formative and summative assessments on the student’s ability to use methods and approaches in media, techniques, and processes in both traditional and non-traditional ways

7.CR.2.2 Identify the legal and ethical issues for creating derivative art.
Glossary
* Create - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.17
* Derivative - Having parts that originate from another source.14
* Ethical - Moral guidelines and philosophical principles for determining appropriate behavior.17
* Identify - To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.27
* Legal - The legislated parameters and protocols of media arts systems, including user agreements, publicity releases, copyright, etc.17
Vertical Alignment
This objective develops the EQ-throughline because in the previous level students described how fair use, copyright, public domain, open source, and creative commons affect the ability of artists to create original or derivative art. At the current level, students identify the legal and ethical issues for creating derivative art. In the next level, students will explain how to share works of art legally and ethically in both physical and digital spaces.
Enduring Understanding
The Enduring Understanding from this thread of objectives is to have students know that respect for originality and ownership of intellectual property is shown through the ethical use of artistic works.
Knowledge
To master this objective, students need to KNOW:
* Legal issues visual artists face when creating works inspired by other artists
* Ethical issues visual artists face when creating works inspired by other artists
Skills
To master this objective, students will need to be SKILLED at:
* Recognizing, listing, or naming the legal and ethical issues to be considered when using another artist's ideas in the creation of new work
* Using disciplinary and other academic vocabulary in verbal or written form
Evidence of Mastery
Given that the Arts Standards are mastery-based, teachers must collect evidence on every student, not just the class as a whole. Mastery is demonstrated when observable and/or documented student work shows each individual has met the expectations of this objective. Use the Knowledge and Skills listed above as the specific criteria for what each student must demonstrate—regardless of how well or artistically it is done.
Key Insights
* Students should be able to answer the question, “Why does it matter to follow both legal and ethical guidelines when creating derivative art?” providing examples of concrete issues to show mastery of this objective
* There are parameters to using intellectual property correctly, and by handling them appropriately, users avoid legal or ethical penalties
* This objective can be paired with CR.2.1 in which students adapt imagery of another artist's work by changing the tools or media. Students would first need to understand the legal and ethical issues of using the imagery in works by another artist
* This objective builds on 6th grade because it introduces the idea of ethics. Note that legality and ethicality are independent concepts and not synonymous. It’s important to address both elements in the objective. For instance, a student may legally create derivative art by obtaining permission from the original artist, but still act unethically by presenting the work as entirely their own. This would be considered plagiarism, which undermines artistic integrity and disrespects the creative contributions of others. Understanding this distinction helps students recognize that being legal is not always the same as being ethical
* Note the conjunction “and” used in this objective - students should be able to demonstrate mastery of their ability to identify both the legal AND ethical issues when creating derivative art. The legality and ethicality can be addressed simultaneously in one learning activity or assessed separately in multiple activities if appropriate
